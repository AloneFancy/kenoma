Part One
Vision, Pillars & Source Integration
願景 · 設計支柱 · 來源整合
1.1
The Six Unbreakable Design Pillars
Every mechanic, scene, asset and UI element must conform to all six. No exceptions.
All Departments
I
No Phase 2. No Second Chance.

The Vanguard's final form at the Child Phase is absolute and apparent from her first second in the arena. No transformation sequence. No health-bar refill. No true form. She walks in, straightens her coat, and begins. The power was always there. The Scholar simply finished building it.

II
Light Before Gunpowder. Intellect Before Either.

The Scholar fights — but the jade lamp is their primary weapon, the spring-pistol is secondary, and intellectual combat (creature naming, environmental kills) is the highest tier. Every major encounter's killing blow is an idea, not a shot. This is the Aline Cedrac model, corrected and applied fully.

III
The Game Watches Without Speaking.

No UI element, dialogue line, or environmental cue acknowledges the behavioral tracking system. No karma meter. No relationship gauge. The 10-scenario ending system is completely invisible until the ending makes the player's behavior visible to them retroactively.

IV
Every Resource Is a Compound Decision.

Lamp oil and spring pellets are simultaneously combat tools and Camel Phase trade goods. Every shot fired is capital not donated. Every donation reduces Lion Phase capacity. Inspired by Metro's military-bullet-as-currency system: no resource decision is ever purely about the immediate moment.

V
The Vanguard Is Always Human.

She never transforms. No wings, no second anatomy, no glowing form. Her evolution at the Child Phase is entirely psychological — the expression of a superior mind that has consumed the Archive's full logic. She puts her coat on, straightens her blade, and looks at the Scholar. That is enough.

VI
Total Diegetic Information.

No HUD elements during gameplay. Health lives in the Scholar's animation state. Threat proximity lives in compass static timbre. All status information lives in physical objects the Scholar carries and opens. Metro's total physicalization doctrine applied to Kenoma's Eastern Zhou toolkit.

飛鳥盡，良弓藏；狡兔死，走狗烹 "When the birds are gone, the bow is put away. When the hare is dead, the hunting dog is cooked." Fan Li · Eastern Zhou Chronicles · The game's thesis. The Scholar is the bow. The Scholar is the hunting dog.

2.1
The Scholar — 士 (Player Character)
Aline Cedrac model: puzzle-primary, combat-secondary, intellect as ultimate weapon
Narrative Art Code

Identity & Psychology

A wandering Warring States intellectual mercenary — a 士. Constitutionally driven to serve the system they inhabit, constitutionally incapable of stopping when the system turns on them. Physically slight, academically extraordinary. They read combat situations the same way they read archive texts: looking for the structural weakness that cannot be argued with. Their frailty is not a weakness — it is the design constraint that makes their intelligence meaningful.

Their core tragedy is that they are genuinely excellent at their work. They translate correctly. They solve every puzzle. They make the Vanguard formidable, then more formidable, then unstoppable. The Scholar's professional excellence IS the mechanism of their death. This is Fan Li's aphorism made playable.

Combat Architecture (Corrected from v3.0)

The Scholar fights. The combat system follows the Aline Cedrac model precisely. Tier hierarchy:

The Dark Voice — Mid-Camel Phase (PoP Two Thrones)

After the Scholar's third major puzzle sequence, a second internal voice emerges — their own strategic mind, finally overriding their academic servility. Speaks exclusively in Eastern Zhou aphorisms (Sun Tzu, Fan Li, Wu Qi). Three player states: Suppress (ignore it — extend Camel Phase cooperation, intensify Lion Phase), Acknowledge (listen but don't act — modifies behavioral tracking), Follow (act on its suggestions — unlocks early sabotage options, affects ending spectrum). No state is labeled. First playthrough: feels like an intrusive thought. Second playthrough: feels like the most important mechanic in the game.

2.2
The Vanguard A & B — 將 · Shadow and Flame (AI Antagonist)
Farah + Kaileena + Eastern Zhou female commander. Never a monster. Never wrong.
Code (AI) Art Narrative

Core Identity

The Vanguard is designed at the intersection of: Farah (SoT — cold independent reasoning, not malice), Kaileena (WW — antagonist using protagonist as instrument, sympathetic), and the female military commanders of the Eastern Zhou period — analytical, systemic, operating under a rational agenda the Scholar's emotional investment prevents them from reading clearly. She protects the Scholar in the Camel Phase not out of loyalty but because they are the most efficient available key to the Archive's locked systems. When they cease to be the most efficient option, she acts.

Critical Design Rule The Vanguard does not betray the Scholar. She optimizes around a changed constraint. In her own frame, she has never been inconsistent. Players who fully understand her will find the Lion Phase more devastating than players who frame her as a villain.

2.3
The Archive Guardian — Three Encounters (Hybrid Elder + Dahaka)
Cannot be killed conventionally. Frequency = function of Traversal debt. Final defeat: environmental intellect.
Design Code Art

The Archive Guardian synthesizes AitD:TNN's Hybrid Elder (recurring pursuer, three encounters, final kill requires environmental puzzle) and PoP WW's Dahaka (appears because of temporal law violation; frequency increases with offending action; cannot be fought). It appears because the Scholar is traversing dimensions illegally — accumulating dimensional debt. It is not hostile toward the Vanguard. This deliberate asymmetry is visible to the player and significant.

EncounterPhaseCorrect ResponseCombat RoleDesign Note
Encounter 1 Mid-Camel Complete nearby puzzle fast enough to trigger mechanism that collapses Guardian's entry aperture. Resonance Grenade can stun 8s as puzzle-buy. Combat is support — the puzzle is the fight Teaches: Guardian has a solution. Solutions are environmental. Combat tools buy time only.
Encounter 2 Late Camel Run. No mechanism solution nearby. Evasion only. Zero combat value Fort Exterior equivalent. Teaches: not every appearance has a solution. Running IS the correct answer here.
Encounter 3 Lion Phase (final) Activate Archive's primary armillary sphere to collapse ALL traversal apertures simultaneously. Seals Guardian's access route permanently. Guardian dissolves. No weapon fired in killing blow The Scholar uses the astronomical instruments they've been studying all game. Intellect as the killing blow. The Hybrid Elder's lightning rod solution made structural.
TraversalDebtManager must be built before content production begins. Guardian frequency = direct function of debt counter. Every Traversal crossing increments debt. Frequency thresholds (low/medium/high/permanent) must be tuned in the prototype phase. If this system is retrofitted into an existing codebase, the tuning data won't be available early enough to inform level design.

3.2
The Observatory — Scholar's Base Camp (SH4: The Room)
Starts safe. Progressively haunts. Cleansing costs saves.
Design Code

The Observatory is the Scholar's base camp, discovered and established in Act I's first hour. It functions exactly as SH4's Room 302: a safe, warm space that provides save access, item storage, and the resonance instrument for Vanguard communication. It is designed to be loved by the player before it begins to fail them.

PhaseObservatory StateMechanic
Early Camel Safe, clean, warm. Two save altars inside. Resonance instrument on central table. Items organize normally. Standard safe room. Player builds attachment.
Late Camel Items given to Vanguard and returned begin accumulating a corruption state (visible discoloration). Save altars still functional. Corruption value per item accumulates over time. Items above threshold: carried = health drain. Player must decide: carry corrupted item or leave it.
Lion Phase Multiple corrupted items. One save altar has been located by Vanguard (from residue tracking). Observatory aggregate corruption may trigger spawning. Cleansing ritual (costs 1 incense stick) removes corruption from all items. If aggregate corruption exceeds threshold, a creature spawns inside the Observatory. The safe room is actively dangerous.
Child Phase Observatory is the final arena. Its state — clean, corrupted, partially haunted — determines the ending's starting conditions. Observatory state is the physical manifestation of the Scholar's behavioral score. The ending begins in a room that reflects every decision the player made.
Design Intent The Observatory's corruption is never attributed to a cause in the game's text. There is no scene where the Vanguard "curses" it. The corruption is environmental causality — items that have been handled by a being of the Vanguard's magnitude retain a charge that is incompatible with the Scholar's biology. The player understands this by inference. The game does not explain it.

M-01
Jade Lamp Combat & Oil Economy
AitD:TNN Metro
CodeDesignAudio

The Scholar's primary offensive tool. An oil-burning jade lamp carried at all times. Damages shadow-class creatures (World B origin) through sustained directional exposure. Also the Scholar's save ignition tool (lights incense/candles) and the only light source in World B Otherworld state. Oil management is the Aline Cedrac battery problem applied to a resource with three simultaneous demands. A Scholar who spends oil freely on combat may arrive at a critical World B navigation segment with an empty reservoir, making progress impossible without backtracking to find oil.

M-04
Intellectual Combat — Creature Naming
Eastern Zhou AitD:TNN Language
DesignNarrativeCode

Certain World B creatures are anchored to their origin text — a Chu ceremonial designation that defines their nature and existence. If the Scholar has completed a full-depth read (M-08) of the relevant scroll or oracle bone, they unlock a "Naming" option when facing a nameable creature. Scholar speaks the designation aloud. M-05
Diegetic 15-Slot Inventory (Satchel System)
AitD 1 Grid TS1 3D Manipulation
CodeDesignArt

Scholar physically opens satchel (diegetic animation, 1.2 seconds) to access a 15-slot grid. Items exist as 3D physical objects that can be removed from slots and rotated freely in 3D space before use or combination (TS1). Giving an item to the Vanguard requires physically carrying it to her in the game world — not via menu. She takes it without looking at the Scholar. She is already examining it.

Combination requires physical spatial compatibility in 3D (TS1+2 rules). Volatile items cannot be adjacent to ignition-class items. Weight of carried items affects Scholar movement speed. Heavy loadout = slower evasion. Resource management is also body management.

Part Six

Two worlds × five levels = ten distinct execution scenarios. The Vanguard never transforms. Every ending is a human being who has become fully what she always was — the quality of that becoming is determined by the Scholar's behavioral score accumulated over the run. The Child Phase arena is the Observatory — its corruption state provides the starting conditions.

Unbreakable rule: No ending involves the Scholar surviving. The spectrum is not about survival chances — it is about the quality of the Vanguard's ascension and the dignity of the Scholar's end.

Part Eight
Technical Architecture & Production Plan
技術架構 · 製作計畫 · 風險登記冊
8.1
Critical Systems — Build Order & Specifications
Build in this order. Systems marked Priority 1 must exist before any content production begins.
WorldSyncManager
Observer / EventBus · Priority 1 · BUILD FIRST

Manages all state synchronization between World A and World B. All state changes in either world broadcast events that the other world subscribes to. Never direct cross-world calls — only events. A broken mechanism in World A must broadcast an event that World B's mirrored space receives within one frame.

Critical rule: No game-state code writes directly to the other world. All modifications go through WorldSyncManager. This is the architectural load-bearer of the entire game. Retrofitting this system into an existing codebase would be project-threatening.

Dependencies: None. All other systems depend on this.
EndingSpectrumCalculator
Weighted Score Accumulator · Priority 1 · BUILD BEFORE CONTENT

Maintains two hidden 0–100 scores (one per world). All score modifications arrive via EventBus events — never direct calls. Scores are never written to UI or logs accessible to the player. At Child Phase trigger, scores are evaluated against band thresholds to select ending level.

Requirement: All content must be designed with full knowledge of its score contribution. If content is built before this system exists, it will be impossible to balance the ending spectrum during QA.

Dependencies: WorldSyncManager (EventBus)
TraversalDebtManager
Counter + Event Dispatcher · Priority 1

Increments a hidden debt counter on every Traversal crossing. Dispatches threshold-crossing events to GuardianSpawnSystem when debt crosses 3 / 8 / 14 thresholds. Scholar never sees this counter — only feels its effect through Guardian frequency. Vanguard post-Traversal-Skill-absorption: zero debt contribution, immune to Guardian spawns.

Dependencies: WorldSyncManager (EventBus), GuardianSpawnSystem
TrapRegistryManager
Dictionary + Observer · Priority 1

Logs every trap armed by the Scholar: type, position, arming timestamp, Vanguard-observed boolean (was Vanguard within 8m during arming animation?). VanguardBehaviorModel subscribes to TrapRegistry events to update its own knowledge state. Observed traps flagged for circumvention logic. Unobserved traps available for one-catch events.

Dependencies: VanguardBehaviorModel
VanguardBehaviorModel
Behavior Tree + Runtime Scholar Profile · Priority 2

Continuously ingests Scholar behavioral data during Camel Phase: preferred routes (path frequency per corridor), save altar positions (timestamp + position), weapon usage (type + range + frequency), puzzle solve speeds (time-to-completion per category), trap observations (via TrapRegistry). Lion Phase: all collected data feeds pathing decisions, ambush positioning, altar detection range, and save-residue triangulation.

This is not machine-learning — it is a behavior tree with runtime-populated parameters. All data structures must be serializable for save-state persistence across sessions.

Dependencies: TrapRegistryManager, WorldSyncManager, IncenseSaveSystem
ObservatoryCorruptionSystem
Per-Item State Machine · Priority 2

Tracks corruption value per Observatory item (0–100). Passive accumulation rate: +5/in-game-hour for all Vanguard-contacted items. Items above 50: carried = Scholar health drain (−1/min). Observatory aggregate corruption (sum of all item values): above 40 aggregate = creature spawn inside Observatory. Cleanse action (1 incense stick): resets all items to 0.

Dependencies: IncenseSaveSystem (cleanse cost), ScholarHealthSystem
FalseVictorySequencer
Timeline Controller · Priority 2

Triggers the False Victory UI suite at precisely calibrated intervals after the boss-kill event. Achievement notification (T+2.5s), Scholar breathing normalization (T+4s), save prompt (T+6s), dimensional tear (T+9s). All timings are tunable parameters — this entire sequence must be playtested extensively to find the exact window that maximizes relaxation before the tear. The sequence cannot be skipped or interrupted by any player action.

Dependencies: EndingSpectrumCalculator (selects ending level on sequence complete)
DarkVoiceController
State Machine + Audio Sequencer · Priority 3

Three states: Suppress / Acknowledge / Follow. State transitions triggered by player response inputs. State affects: available Scholar actions (Follow: early sabotage unlocks), EndingSpectrumCalculator contributions (Follow/Acknowledge: negative), and Camel Phase cooperation arc length. No UI state indicator. Audio implementation: same voice actor, +8dB reverb, −300Hz low-cut.

Dependencies: EndingSpectrumCalculator, SabotageActionsSystem (Follow state only)
8.2
Pre-Rendered Camera Architecture
AitD 1 doctrine applied. Every angle is a composed painting.

Backgrounds are pre-rendered at high visual quality, composited with real-time character models in Unity URP. Camera transitions are zone-triggered, not player-controlled. Every camera angle is authored as a deliberate composition — never procedural, never automatic.

Vanguard placement rule: During the Camel Phase, every camera angle in a puzzle chamber must have a valid background position for the Vanguard to occupy without breaking composition. Art and level design must coordinate on this. The Vanguard's background presence is not a cinematic event — it is an ambient design truth present in every room.

Technical benefit: Pre-rendered backgrounds also allow the VanguardBehaviorModel to pre-calculate optimal ambush positions relative to the Scholar's anticipated arrival direction at each camera zone transition. The camera system's predictability makes her appear prescient without requiring omniscient pathfinding.
