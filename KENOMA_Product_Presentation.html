01 · The Opportunity
Survival Horror Has an Unresolved Contradiction.
Every great game in this genre made a trade-off. TS1 chose narrative coherence and lost scope. TS2 chose scope and lost coherence. Silent Hill 2 built the deepest behavioral spine in the genre and made it a black box players could never interact with. No one has held all three at once. Until now.
Market Position Analysis
Narrative-Puzzle Coherence vs. World Architecture Depth
Derived from Tormented Souls I & II comparative analysis

What TS1 Proved and Couldn't Scale

Tormented Souls 1 achieved something rare: the act of solving a puzzle IS the act of discovering the character who left it. The TV dial that reconstructs an abusive relationship biography. The pocket watch stopped at the twins' birthday. The Judge's Podium that requires understanding a hearing-impaired family to solve. 75–80% of TS1's puzzles have this quality. This is not accidental. It is the result of a small, cohesive team designing from a complete story bible outward — placing every puzzle by asking: "Given that this room was used by these specific people, what did they leave behind?"

The limitation: one building, one story. It exhausted its architectural scope before exhausting its design quality.

TS1 Core Doctrine — Adopted Wholesale The story produces the puzzle. Not: "this room needs a puzzle." A puzzle exists because a real person made a real decision in this real space and left traces of it behind. The Scholar is here to read those traces. Reading is solving. Solving is the story.

What TS2 Proved and Where It Broke

Tormented Souls 2's seven zones generated genuine variety — acoustic bells, chess cryptex, time-travel gardening, mannequin-to-character crossing. Its best puzzles match TS1's quality. But seven zones required parallel development teams, and without a single designer owning the crossing mechanic, each zone invented its own rule. Six crossings, six different logics. Players cannot build intuition. Every mirror becomes a surprise rather than a mastered system.

TS2's Cemetery and Bunker zones are the clearest evidence: puzzles designed by zone type ("this is a cemetery, therefore grave symbols") rather than character history. Functional. Atmospheric. Not discoveries.

The Key Insight — What No Game Has Solved TS2 generated variety through seven geographic zones. That approach dilutes coherence as the team grows. KENOMA generates variety through two philosophical worlds in one building. Sacred Fire classification logic vs. Hollow inversion logic. Two puzzle vocabularies, one architect. Zero zone-brief design.
02 · The Thesis — Five Sentences That Define This Game
KENOMA is a tragedy about professional excellence — a scholar who has read every warning about their own nature, enters the Archive anyway, and succeeds at everything. That success is the mechanism of their elimination.
The Archive contains two civilizations, built on opposite philosophical halves of the same truth — Sacred Fire and the Hollow — separated for 300 years inside a single building. One Scholar is the only person alive who can cross between them. The Vanguard from each world has been waiting, specifically, for this person.
Every puzzle is a scar left by a specific historical decision. The Scholar's expertise — translation — is the mechanical key to everything. Reading deeply opens better solutions. Reading shallowly produces survival under pressure. Every deep-read decision compounds forward. There is no HUD. No hint system. Only comprehension.
The invisible behavioral scoring system watches every Scholar decision across the full run without ever announcing itself. The Vanguard's final state is not a transformation. She straightens her tunic. She turns. And the player understands, in her face, the exact history of what they chose.
The game ends ten endings across two surviving worlds. In the highest-scored ending, the Vanguard says: "You were extraordinary. Past tense is not a judgment. It is a precision." Players who reach this ending feel it as a gift, not a punishment. The Scholar built something that outlasted them. That is the tragedy and the triumph simultaneously.
"When the birds are gone, the good bow is put away. When the hare is dead, the hunting dog is cooked." — Fan Li, Advisor to the King of Yue · Eastern Zhou Chronicles · The Scholar's thesis, taught as a warning for others · Arrived at as their own epitaph

04 · The Design Doctrine
Five Principles. Derived from Research. Not Invented.
We analyzed Tormented Souls 1 & 2 at the puzzle level — every mechanism, every crossing, every inventory decision. The gap between what TS1 achieved and what TS2 couldn't maintain at scale is the exact blueprint for how KENOMA is built.

The Five Unbreakable Principles

I
Every puzzle is a scar left by a specific person's specific decision.
Before any puzzle is placed, answer: who built this mechanism, why, and what were they protecting? The Grand Armillary's activation sequence exists because the Archive's founders encoded an emergency dissolution protocol in cosmological notation — because they feared what they had created. The Shadow Well exists because annual Armillary recalibration requires solar alignment. Character necessity, always. Not zone necessity, never.
What every other game does instead
This room is a cemetery, therefore death-symbol puzzles. This zone is a school, therefore academic puzzles. The puzzle is appropriate. It is not necessary. It makes the player solve, not discover.
II
One crossing rule, applied everywhere, never renegotiated.
Players learn the crossing rule in PZ-002 (first required crossing) and apply it correctly in every subsequent puzzle without reconsidering whether this crossing might have different rules. Knowledge from the Hollow enables action in Sacred Fire. Material from Sacred Fire produces physical change in the Hollow. This rule is structural — it is the game's entire cross-world logic in one sentence.
What TS2 did instead
Six different crossing logics across seven zones. Each zone designer independently solved how crossing would work in their area. The Classroom 202 "opposites" rule was genuinely clever and should have been the game's unifying principle. It appears once and is never generalized.
III
Reading IS solving. The translation is the mechanic and the story simultaneously.
When the Scholar translates the Grand Armillary manual deeply, they have the activation sequence memorized. When they translate it shallowly, they face the puzzle under pressure with no prior knowledge. The act of reading is not separate from the act of solving. The TV dial biography in TS1 is the model: understanding the character's life IS the combination. KENOMA's entire translation system enforces this at every level.
The design failure to avoid
Documents explain the story. Puzzles deliver the item. The reading and the solving are sequential but separate. Players finish the document, then solve the puzzle. Two separate actions that could have been one discovery.
IV
Difficulty comes from understanding history. Not from decoding abstractions.
The Judge's Podium in TS1 is hard because understanding that the entire family is hearing-impaired requires genuine empathy with character biography. It is not hard because the symbol encoding is obscure. KENOMA's puzzles are always hard in the first way. The Armillary sequence is hard because the Scholar didn't read the manual — not because the sequence was encrypted in a cipher.
What produces the wrong kind of difficulty
Clock times → button grid positions. Image pairs → switch configurations. The encoding rule is internally consistent but arbitrary — no narrative reason connects the clue form to the solution form. Players use guides. The solution, once known, makes no emotional sense.
V
Two philosophical puzzle vocabularies replace seven geographic zones.
TS2 generated variety through seven zones with different visual identities and creature types. This required a larger team and broke the narrative-owner model. KENOMA generates variety through philosophical divergence within one building: Sacred Fire classification (dangerous = restricted, valuable = accessible) vs. Hollow inversion (dangerous = accessible, valuable = restricted). A Scholar who mastered World A classification logic fails World B on first attempt. Same space, same Scholar, two completely different knowledge systems.
What expansion usually costs
Seven zones require seven zone designers. Each designer owns their zone's puzzle logic and often reinvents the crossing mechanic. The story-bible author cannot be in seven rooms simultaneously. The first zone (Convent) feels authored. The last zone (Bunker) feels filled. KENOMA has one Archive. The authorial ownership never dilutes.
05 · Proof of Concept
The Interference Puzzle System
Four puzzles where both Vanguards attempt the same mechanism simultaneously — and cannot solve it because of the other's invisible presence from the other world. These are not design documents. They are demonstrations that every principle above has been implemented, not just stated.
What makes these four puzzles the proof of concept for everything KENOMA claims to be:
They exist because characters exist, not because rooms exist. Each mechanism was built for a real administrative purpose by real people with specific intentions. Each fails because of a physical or logical property of the mechanism itself — not scripting. Each solution reveals something about the Archive's dual nature before the Scholar has found a crossing point. And every Scholar choice accumulates in both Vanguards' invisible behavioral models, building the Lion Phase one decision at a time. Five principles, four puzzles, all demonstrated simultaneously.
01 Cannot be solved with both present
Not because of scripting. Because of a physical or logical property of the mechanism itself. Two light sources split a shadow. Two vibration patterns cancel each other. Two footstep sources vibrate water that needs perfect stillness. Two translation conventions produce an administratively invalid hybrid.
02 Scholar invents a plausible reason to move one away
Without ever revealing the other world exists. The reason must be technically plausible within what each Vanguard currently knows. The Scholar's professional expertise — acoustic resonance knowledge, classification systems, administrative law — is the tool. The expertise that makes the Scholar valuable is the same tool used to manage the situation that exists because they are too valuable.
03 Solved puzzle leaves a second-world trace
The Calibration Marker has two concentric calendar rings. The Keeper Designation names two Keepers appointed simultaneously. The still water maps 8 constellations when the sky has 7. The Foundation Obelisk's final clause is written in both notations simultaneously. The Scholar accumulates evidence of the second world before they can physically reach it. The Vanguards do not register the significance of what they see.
04 Every choice carries behavioral weight
Both Vanguards are watching. Both are filing. The Scholar sends Vanguard A on a plausible errand — she checks on her return and finds the errand's premise was false. One hypothesis flagged. The Scholar defers and reads the well alone at night — both Vanguards independently note the Scholar operated off-schedule without informing either. Both update their behavioral models: Scholar operates independently when the environment becomes complex. Filed for the Lion Phase.

What every other game would have done
Two people need different items. The player fetches one, then the other. Or chooses between them. The choice is framed explicitly: "Who do you help first?" The consequence is declared immediately after the choice.

The puzzle is an allocation problem. The human element is mechanical, not social. There is no behavioral accumulation. The moment resolves completely and moves on.
What KENOMA does instead
The Foundation Obelisk's clauses require precise, complete translation in correct administrative Elamite. Both Vanguards issue simultaneous requirements in different notation conventions. Eleven minutes of cross-correction produces a hybrid that is legally invalid in both worlds.
The Fan Li Moment
The Scholar is a resource being allocated by two commanders with equal claim on it. This is the 士 psychology made mechanical: the Scholar cannot stop serving, and the act of serving both perfectly is impossible, and the attempt reveals exactly who the Scholar is.

06 · The Tragedy Engine
The Scholar and the Vanguard — Why This Dynamic Is the Breakout
Survival horror antagonists are usually monsters, or mysteries, or the protagonist's past. The Vanguard is none of these. She is a person who has been waiting for this specific person for 300 years, has planned everything correctly, cares about the Scholar genuinely, and will eliminate them when the Archive is complete — because that is what the plan always required.
The Scholar The 士 Who Read Fan Li and Stayed Anyway

Not naive. They have read every relevant warning. They know Fan Li's aphorism — they have taught it. They know the 士 archetype from the inside: the wandering intellectual whose constitution requires finding the problem that matches their exact skills and applying those skills without reservation until the problem is resolved.

CoreThey know what they are. They enter the Archive anyway. This is not ignorance. This is character. The 士 cannot decline the problem that matches them exactly.
CombatThree tiers — Environment first, fire vessel second, Naming last. The most powerful action costs only time and prior reading. No weapon fired, no oil spent. Comprehension as the killing blow.

The Vanguard — Two Versions Three Hundred Years. One Plan. One Person, Two Expressions.

She entered the Archive as a senior commander in the Achaemenid Immortals corps. Her regiment is dead — they died one by one mapping the Archive's dangers across a decade, until she was alone. She has been alone in a sealed building ever since, managing its degradation, building a behavioral model of a Scholar she would not meet for 300 years.

She cares about the Scholar. The three Camel Phase emotional resonance moments are real. Her warmth is real AND calculated. Both are true simultaneously. This is not contradiction — this is Kaileena from Prince of Persia: Warrior Within. She uses the Scholar as a tool and genuinely cares about them and both things are true in the same gesture.

Vanguard AMilitary precision. Sentences end on certainties. Asks one question per subject, never repeats it. 300 years managing the Apadana as a logistics problem.
Vanguard BExpansive, patient, philosophical. Measures time in generations. 300 years in absolute darkness. The Scholar's fire vessel is the first warmth in 300 years.
The DesignShe never transforms. She straightens her tunic. She turns. The quality of her ending is entirely a function of what the Scholar invested in the Archive, in her, in the knowledge they shared or withheld.

The Three Acts — Nietzsche's Three Metamorphoses

🐪 Act I — The Camel Nietzsche · "Thou Shalt"
The spirit that says yes to every burden. The Scholar in Act I — carrying every puzzle, donating every resource, teaching everything they know. This is the Scholar's finest hour and their trap. The Camel finds meaning in service. It cannot imagine not serving.
🦁 Act II — The Lion Nietzsche · "I Will"
The spirit that says no. The Scholar when the astrolabe delivers a position report instead of a greeting. The Lion uses every trap the Camel set, every translation the Camel completed. The Lion's tools are the Camel's gifts turned inside out.
◎ Act III — The Child Nietzsche · "Sacred Yes"
This belongs to the Vanguard, not the Scholar. After the False Victory, what steps through the fold in the air is someone who has become fully, completely, irreversibly what they always were. The Scholar built the Child. That is the 士's tragedy at its largest scale.

The Behavioral Spine — SH2 Extended

Silent Hill 2's invisible tracking, made interactive and legible through the Vanguard
In Silent Hill 2, the game watches James Sunderland's behavior across the entire run without announcing itself. How often he checks the map. How long he looks at Angela's knife. The ending fires based on who he was, not what he chose. Players only understand the tracking when the ending reveals it.

KENOMA extends this: the Scholar's behavioral scores are visible — not to the player, but to the Vanguard. She acts on what she observes. In Act II, she is pre-positioned based on the Scholar's demonstrated crossbow range, measured over weeks. She knows every trap the Scholar set because she watched them arm each one. She knows exactly how much oil is in the Scholar's fire vessel because she has been counting every drop spent since Day One.

The behavioral tracking is not a black box. It is legible — but only after the fact. The Scholar spent the Camel Phase thinking they were teaching the Vanguard. They were being studied. Both things are true.

07 · The Call
We Know Exactly
What We're Building.
The survival horror genre has been waiting for a game that achieves what TS1 proved was possible — at the scope TS2 demonstrated the genre could sustain — with the behavioral depth Silent Hill 2 pioneered as a black box and never let players interact with. KENOMA is that game. Every design decision in this document is derived from studying exactly where each predecessor succeeded and where each fell short. We are not inventing new mechanics. We are solving the unsolved problems with evidence.
What We Never Compromise
Every puzzle is a scar. Story produces puzzle, always. One crossing rule, everywhere. Reading is solving. Two philosophical worlds replace seven geographic zones. The story bible is open during every puzzle placement decision. These are not guidelines. They are the game's structural DNA.
What Makes This Achievable
One Archive building. One Vanguard character (two expressions). One Scholar. No open world, no procedural generation, no branching dialogue trees. The complexity is narrative and behavioral — not spatial. A cohesive small team with the story bible open can produce something TS1 proved possible. We have the proof of concept in four interference puzzles that work.
What Success Looks Like
Players who reach the Level 5 ending feel they have reconstructed, completely, a 300-year tragedy — not just beaten a game. Players who replay find that every decision they made was visible to the Vanguard in ways they could not see the first time. The Scholar who reads deeply has a completely different game than the Scholar who skims. This is the experience TS1 achieved for one playthrough. KENOMA extends it across ten.
