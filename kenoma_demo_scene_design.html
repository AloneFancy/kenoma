The Resonance Obelisk

PZ-CC-DEMO · Camel Phase II · Demo Scene · Calibration Channel Adjacent
A puzzle that has been failing for 300 years because two people, both trying to solve it from opposite sides of reality, keep canceling each other out. The Scholar is the first person who can see both attempts simultaneously — and must choose what to do with that knowledge. There is no correct solution. There are only solutions that pass, and what they cost.
Interference Puzzle Dual Vanguard AI Dual World · One Room BMS-Lite Demo Unity 6 · URP 30–40 Min Demo Slice
Scene Overview
🏛 What This Scene Is And Why It Works As A Demo §00 ▶

The Resonance Obelisk is a single room that exists in both World A (Sacred Fire, above) and World B (The Hollow, below) — the same architectural space rendered in two states simultaneously. In the center of each version of the room stands an Obelisk covered in cuneiform acoustic notation. The Obelisks are paired: what physically happens to one changes the resonance state of the other.

For 300 years, Vanguard A has been attempting to tune the World A Obelisk to a specific harmonic. For 300 years, Vanguard B has been attempting the same on the World B Obelisk. They have always failed — because their tuning attempts produce destructive interference with each other, canceling out in the space between worlds. Neither can hear the other's work. Neither knows why they keep failing.

The Scholar is the first person in three centuries who can cross between worlds and observe both tuning states simultaneously. The puzzle becomes solvable the moment the Scholar understands what is actually happening. But how the Scholar resolves the interference — whether by helping one Vanguard, both, neither, or by exploiting the interference itself — determines what both Vanguards understand about the Scholar's character, and changes how they behave for the rest of the game.

Demo Purpose: This single scene proves all four of KENOMA's core ideas in 15–20 minutes: (1) dual-world traversal as a mechanic with real cost, (2) the Vanguards as intelligences being modeled by the Scholar's behavior, (3) warmth as something the Scholar generates through choices, not something the game delivers on a schedule, (4) no correct solution — only solutions that pass, each with a different BMS consequence.

Puzzle Logic · What The Scholar Discovers
⚖ The Interference · What Is Actually Happening §03 ▶

The Scholar's astrolabe — the resonance instrument — detects interference when held near either Obelisk. The resonance display (physical: the astrolabe's rings vibrate and align into a specific pattern) shows the Scholar that the two Obelisks are producing complementary frequencies that cancel each other in the space between worlds.

The Scholar must cross between worlds at least twice to understand the full picture — once to observe WA, once to observe WB, and optionally a third time to act. Every crossing increments Traversal Debt. The Boundary manifests on the second crossing for 8 seconds (not lethal in the demo — establishing dread). On the third crossing, it manifests for 20 seconds and pursues.

Discovery Sequence — What Reading Reveals
1
Observe WA Obelisk (no crossing required). Astrolabe rings show 3/7 tones aligned. The floor hum is wrong. The Scholar can deep-read the north wall frieze (25 seconds): the tuning sequence is a 7-tone acoustic formula. This deep-read is optional but unlocks a +BMS consequence later. Deep-read complete → Scholar murmurs "these four are the answer to something — but not this room's question."
2
Cross to WB (Crossing 1 — Traversal Debt +1). Boundary appears briefly (3-second flash, retreats). Lantern-Thieves begin circling. Scholar observes WB Obelisk: 4/7 tones aligned — the complementary 4. Astrolabe now shows both sets simultaneously and the interference pattern becomes visible as overlapping ring vibrations. Scholar internal voice (Dark Voice): "The same problem. Different angle. One is the answer to the other."
3
Optional: Read VB's notation tablet (WB alcove). She has been writing about the interference for 300 years without knowing its source. Her notation system is her own invention — elegant, internally consistent, wrong about the cause. Reading it requires proximity to VB (she does not move away). This proximity is itself an observable behavior — the Scholar chose to approach VB's private work. +BMS consequence.
4
The Scholar understands. The puzzle is now solvable by three distinct methods. The Scholar must choose one — or stall until the Lantern-Thieves force a decision. There is no prompt. There is no waypoint. The three solution states become available through accumulated observation.
Three Solution Paths · No Correct Answer
⚔ How To Lure Both Vanguards · Solution Architecture §04 ▶

Each solution requires the Scholar to manipulate one or both Vanguards' position — getting them into a specific location at a specific moment. This is the "lure" mechanic. The Vanguards follow coherent AI behavior; they are not waiting to be moved. The Scholar must understand each Vanguard's routine to lure her effectively.

Lure Mechanics Available in Demo: (1) Direct address — Scholar speaks to Vanguard, she moves toward the Scholar's position. (2) Resource drop — Scholar leaves a specific item (oil, tablet) where VA/VB will pass; they pause to examine it. (3) Translate aloud — Scholar reads a cuneiform inscription aloud; both Vanguards respond to new translation activity. (4) Cross between worlds — movement between worlds causes acoustic disruption detectable by both Vanguards, drawing attention to the crossing point.
🔥 Path A — Serve Vanguard A

Help VA complete the WA Obelisk by sharing the WB tuning data. She finishes what she started 300 years ago. VB's work is cancelled.

Lure: Scholar crosses back to WA (Crossing 2, Debt +2, Boundary manifests 8 sec), approaches VA directly, translates the WB complementary sequence aloud — "The missing four are in the Dark Archive. Tones 4, 5, 6, 7 in inverted sequence." VA is still for three seconds. Then she picks up her tuning tools.
Mechanical result: VA tunes the remaining 4 tones. The floor channels stop humming. The Obelisk reaches full harmonic resonance. A sealed alcove in WA-CC-02 opens — contains oil (resource reward) and a scroll referencing the Grand Armillary sequence (Act II foreshadow).
WB consequence: As WA resolves, the WB Obelisk's 4 tones go cold. VB stops writing. She sits in silence for a moment. "The interference resolved." A pause. "From your side." She closes her tablet. She does not leave. But she is quieter than before.

BMS · VB Warmth −3 · VB Utility assessment: Scholar chose a side
BMS · Event Tag: SOLVED_SIDED_VA
❄ Path B — Serve Vanguard B

Share the WA tuning data with VB so she can complete the WB Obelisk. VA's 300-year effort is made obsolete. VB finishes what she started.

Lure: Scholar stays in WB, approaches VB with the WA frieze translation — the 3-tone sequence from the north wall. VB takes the translation and reads it twice. "This is what I've been missing. The first three." Not a thank you. An observation. She stands and moves to the Obelisk.
Mechanical result: VB tunes the remaining 3 tones. The WB Obelisk reaches full resonance. A sealed void-chamber opens in WB-CC-02 — contains a Lantern-Thief's core (useful for Naming Bell crafting) and VB's 250-year acoustic journal (deep-readable, major VB Warmth event if Scholar reads it fully).
WA consequence: Scholar returns to WA to find VA has noticed the WA Obelisk's 3 remaining glyphs have stopped resonating with WB entirely — the interference is gone, but the WA side is now incomplete. VA examines the Obelisk. She says nothing to the Scholar. She sets her tools down in the same precise order as before. This is how she signals she is done for the day.

⚖ Path C — Resolve Both Simultaneously

The Scholar realizes the full sequence only works if both Obelisks are tuned at the same moment. Requires luring both Vanguards into position and coordinating a simultaneous activation. Most costly. Most consequential.

Lure (hardest): Scholar must get VA to the WA Obelisk and VB to the WB Obelisk simultaneously. Step 1: Scholar tells VA the complete 7-tone sequence is split across both worlds and both halves must activate together. VA: "That requires the Dark Archive." She is quiet for a moment. She has not been near the TFP in 300 years. She walks toward it. Step 2: Cross to WB (Crossing 3 — Boundary pursues for 20 sec, Scholar must manage). Tell VB the same. She is already at her Obelisk. She waits.
Mechanical result: Scholar crosses back to WA (Crossing 4 — Debt critical, Boundary appears immediately). Scholar speaks the activation phrase (translated from the frieze — only available if Scholar did the deep-read in Step 1 of discovery). Both Obelisks respond simultaneously. The floor channels in WA and the void-pits in WB pulse in unison. Both sealed chambers open. Through the TFP glass, for exactly 3 seconds, VA and VB see each other — standing on opposite sides of the same Obelisk, separated by a world. Neither moves.
Both warmth consequences apply. Unique scene: VA says — "She's been doing the same work." Not a question. VB (heard by Scholar in WB): "Three hundred years." Same rhythm, different register. Then both return to their worlds. The puzzle is complete. Nothing more is said about it.

BMS · Both Recognition Stage +1

BMS · Event Tag: SOLVED_UNIFIED — Highest weight. Both Vanguards' models updated: Scholar perceives both worlds as one problem.
Path C Unique Condition: Only available if Scholar completed the WA deep-read (Step 1) AND read VB's notation tablet (Step 3). If either was skipped, the Scholar does not have the activation phrase, and the puzzle cannot be resolved simultaneously. The Scholar who skimmed cannot reach the best outcome — but they never know this is why.

ObeliskController.cs — Core Puzzle State
public class ObeliskController : MonoBehaviour { public ObeliskController pairedObelisk; // WA ↔ WB sync bridge public WorldSide side; // WA or WB // 7 tone slots — true = correctly tuned private bool[] tones = new bool[7]; private int tonesCorrect = 0; private bool isComplete = false; // WA starts with tones 0,1,2 correct (indices) // WB starts with tones 3,4,5,6 correct // Full sequence = all 7 — but interference prevents simultaneous resonance // when both obelisks are in partial state public void ReceiveTonesFromScholar(int[] toneIndices, SolutionPath path) { foreach (var i in toneIndices) tones[i] = true; tonesCorrect = System.Array.FindAll(tones, t => t).Length; if (tonesCorrect == 7) OnComplete(path); else OnPartialTune(); } private void OnComplete(SolutionPath path) { isComplete = true; // Notify paired obelisk — it goes dark (destructive) unless Path C if (path != SolutionPath.Unified) pairedObelisk.OnInterferenceLost(); // Trigger Vanguard ACTIVATING state for the correct world's Vanguard VanguardStateEvent.Dispatch(side, VanguardState.Activating, path); // Open sealed chamber SealedSpaceEvent.Dispatch(side, true); // Record to BMS BMSController.Instance.RecordEvent(new BMSEvent { eventType = BMSEventType.PuzzleSolved, tag = path.ToString(), wa_warmthDelta = path == SolutionPath.VA_Only ? 5 : path == SolutionPath.VB_Only ? -2 : 4, // Unified vb_warmthDelta = path == SolutionPath.VB_Only ? 6 : path == SolutionPath.VA_Only ? -3 : 5 }); } public void OnInterferenceLost() { // Called on the losing side's Obelisk // Glyphs dim. Floor channels/void-pits go silent. // Trigger losing Vanguard's CLOSED/MEASURED state WorldSide losingSide = (side == WorldSide.WA) ? WorldSide.WB : WorldSide.WA; VanguardStateEvent.Dispatch(losingSide, VanguardState.Closed, SolutionPath.None); } }

Critical (from Evaluation research): Dark Voice must be binaural from day one — not a post-production reverb treatment. Every Dark Voice line has a specified spatial origin: behind (suppressed thought surfacing), beside (strategic analysis), ahead (warning about what the Scholar is about to do). End-of-Scene · Demo Consequence Display
◎ Post-Puzzle Consequence Card · BMS Readout (Demo Only) §09 ▶
Demo-only exception: The full game never reveals BMS data. For the demo, after the puzzle resolves and the Scholar reaches the next save point, a consequence card is displayed — a single screen that shows what each Vanguard now understands about the Scholar, in the Vanguard's own voice. This is not a score. It is a behavioral readout in character. It teaches players that their choices were observed and interpreted — without breaking the "never show the BMS" rule, because the card appears outside active gameplay as a demo-context teaching moment.
Consequence Card — Path A (SOLVED_SIDED_VA)
Vanguard A's Assessment

"The Scholar shared information from the Dark Archive without being asked. The assessment: they understand that the two worlds are one problem. They chose to resolve this problem on the side of Sacred Fire. This tells me something about their priorities."

Warmth register: trust extended. Routes adjusted. Resources pre-staged.

Vanguard B's Assessment

"The Scholar chose which world mattered. I have recorded this. My analysis of their intellectual priorities was slightly incorrect. I have updated it. A reasonable choice. I do not require them to be otherwise."
