KENOMA

The Archive of Two Truths · Psychological Horror Tragedy
PC / Console Fixed Camera 28–32 Months Phase 1 · $250–350K Achaemenid Persian Setting
"When the birds are gone, the good bow is put away. When the hare is dead, the hunting dog is cooked."
FAN LI · Eastern Zhou Chronicles · The Scholar knows this text. Teaches it. Thinks it warns against bad rulers. It warns against anyone who serves at all.
The game in one sentence: A scholar is exactly good enough to be used completely — not despite their brilliance, but because of it. Every correct professional decision is a step toward the conclusion. The ending is not a betrayal. It is a completion.
Two Worlds · One Building
🔥 World A · Sacred Fire · The Apadana
ArchitecturePersepolis-scale, 72 stone columns, processional friezes, fire altars at every junction
PhilosophyAsha — visible truth, order, light. Not "good." Chosen path.
CrossingBronze armillary TFPs (Traversal Focal Points) set into limestone pillars
AccumulationScholar lights fire altars with oil. The Apadana becomes beautiful corridor by corridor. The player should love this place before Act II takes it.
Key roomsWA-001 Gate · WA-003 Eastern Treasury (30,000 tablets) · WA-007 Grand Armillary (summit)
❄ World B · The Hollow · Dark Archive
ArchitectureSame column grid — inverted underground. Friezes show the same tribute procession, gone wrong. The tributaries' faces are slowly turning.
PhilosophyDruj — hidden truth, shadow, cold. Not "evil." The alternate path. Equally real.
CrossingJade armillary TFPs, colder, resonance felt in the Scholar's chest before visible
VulnerabilityScholar's fire vessel = only warm light in 300 years. Everything that lives here sees the Scholar's exact location at all times.
Key roomsWB-001 Inverted Gate · WB-004 Dark Library (Previous Scholar's journal) · WB-∞ Chamber of Convergence
⚖ The Iron Rule — Crossing Logic NEVER BEND ▶
Knowledge from World B enables action in World A. Material from World A produces physical change in World B. One rule. Taught at PZ-002. Consistent everywhere, always.
Every crossing increments a hidden Traversal Debt counter. Higher debt = more frequent Boundary appearances. The Vanguard has zero crossing debt. She does not cross. She waits. The Scholar pays all costs. This asymmetry is visible from minute one. Its meaning is not visible until Act III.
TS2 had six different crossing logics across seven zones — it destroyed player intuition. KENOMA has one. If a puzzle requires bending the rule, the puzzle changes, not the rule.
Three-Act Structure · Nietzsche's Three Metamorphoses
◎ Camel · Lion · Child STRUCTURAL LAW ▶
🐪
Act I · Camel
Scholar. Says yes to every burden. Professional delight shading into dread. The warmth must be genuine before the turn can devastate. The Vanguard is efficient, strange, familiar in a way the Scholar cannot place. Both things feel like gifts.
Design test: if the player isn't warm and comfortable here, Act II cannot land.
🦁
Act II · Lion
Scholar. "I will." Environmental chess. Not a horror chase — two brilliant people in the same building, both doing everything correctly, one finishing before the other finishes first.
The sadness is that neither of them is wrong.
The Turn: Scholar pauses. Sets down the tablet. Picks up the crossbow. Does not cut away.
Playtest verdict: "I knew it was coming and it still landed." If "I didn't see it coming" — the Camel Phase failed.
◎
Act III · Child
The Vanguard — not the Scholar. Player watches rather than acts. She moves through rooms the Scholar worked in, reading the residue. Three-second silence: same gesture in every scenario, different weight. In Scenario I: emptiness. In Scenario V: prayer.
She straightens her tunic. She goes north. The fire altars stay lit behind her. The Nietzschean Child: not innocent, but new.
Characters
🎓 The Scholar 士 ARCHETYPE ▶
The 士 (Eastern Zhou wandering intellectual-servant) cannot stop serving. Not weakness — character. They find the problem matching their exact skills and apply them without reservation. Fan Li left. The Scholar stays. This is not ignorance. This is who they are.
Combat — Three Tiers (Aline Cedrac / AitD:TNN model):
T1EnvironmentalSet during Camel, used in Lion. Only at locations where placed, only if Vanguard didn't observe arming.
T2Fire / Crossbow / BellFire vessel: damages Hollow creatures, lights altars, navigates Dark Archive — three competing demands on one oil supply. Crossbow: 12 bolts, 3-sec reload, fully exposed. Bell: 2 bolts + 1 stone = 3 bells max, stun on correct 3-strike pattern.
T3Naming (highest)After full deep-read of creature's origin inscription, Scholar speaks its designation in the Naming Tongue. Permanent. No ammo. No oil. Costs only prior knowledge. Most powerful action is cheapest in resources.
Zero HUD — all status is physical: robe tears/darkens, posture degrades, hands tremble on puzzles when badly hurt. Fire vessel flame size = oil. Astrolabe resonance = creature proximity.
⚔ The Vanguard DUAL INSTANCE ▶
She is not tracking the Scholar to eliminate them. She is tracking them to understand them. Elimination is the consequence of understanding, not the goal. A complete model is a Vanguard who knows exactly when the Scholar's work is done.
Vanguard A (Sacred Fire) — Military model: Measures efficiency and completion. Warmth = professional trust. She placed an oil flask in the Scholar's hands with exactly the amount calculated by tracking consumption since Day One. This is care. The calculation is also real. Threshold: ~80% Archive completion.
Vanguard B (The Hollow) — Philosophical model: Measures intellectual depth and novelty. Warmth = intimacy. Spent 8 years learning Babylonian so she could understand her Scholar when they arrived. Has been counting 70 hours for 300 years. Threshold: when the Scholar stops surprising her.
Five Recognition Stages (both Vanguards):
S1Asks questions
S2Stops asking method
S3Anticipates needs
S4Answers before asked
S5 ⚠Already knows. Observing.
Stage 5 is the most dangerous moment in Act I — it happens while the Scholar is still safe.
Never show the BMS. No UI, no notifications. Read only through Vanguard behavior changes. The moment tracking is visible, every prior warmth retroactively becomes a checkbox.
8 Core Systems · Build Order Matters
⚙ Systems Architecture BMS FIRST · USS LAST ▶
SYS-01 BMS · Behavioral Model System
The spine. 12 event types, batched per room-session. WarmthRegister per Vanguard. Feeds every other system.
⚠ BUILD FIRST — nothing works without this
SYS-02 RSS · Recognition Stage System
5 stages per Vanguard. Observable behavioral shifts. Drives the three resonance moments. Requires BMS.
Build after BMS is stable
SYS-03 TDS · Translation Depth System
Deep-read vs skim per tablet. Feeds BMS. Determines puzzle unlocks, Vanguard responses, ending variants. If deep-read feels slow — everything collapses.
Art + audio must prioritize this above all else in vertical slice
SYS-04 TVS · Traversal System
Crossing management. Traversal Debt counter. Boundary frequency. Asymmetry: Vanguard never crosses, pays zero debt.
One rule. Always. No exceptions.
SYS-05 RES · Resource Economy System
Oil / crossbow bolts / sacred stones. No abundance. Three competing demands on the fire vessel simultaneously.
Metro 2033 bullets-as-currency model
SYS-06 ⚠ USS · Utility Score System
Lion Phase engine. Independent per Vanguard. Gap between thresholds = strategic window. VA: 80% completion. VB: novelty depletion.
Build LAST — requires RSS, TDS, TVS stable
SYS-07 TCS · Terrain Control System
Scholar-controlled / Contested / Vanguard-controlled rooms. The Scholar's route through Act II is a strategy, not navigation.
Vanguard adapts through prediction, not reaction (Hello Neighbor failure mode: avoid)
SYS-08 SRS · Scenario Resolution System
Reads BMS totals at Act III. Outputs one of 5 scenario archetypes. Never 10 score buckets — 5 dramatically distinct genres.
Reads from BMS. No other data source.
Iron Laws · Break Any One and the Game Becomes Something That Already Exists
The Four Unbreakable Rules
INever make the turn a betrayal. VA tells the Scholar exactly what is about to happen — Clause 18 — and walks away to execute it. She told them. They stayed. The turn is the final gift of the Camel Phase and the opening move of Act II simultaneously.
IINever break the Iron Rule. One crossing logic everywhere, always. If a puzzle requires bending it, the puzzle changes. The rule does not.
IIINever show the Behavioral Model. No UI, no "your choice has been remembered." The player reads the model through the Vanguard's observed behavior. Stage 5 arrives through noticing she stopped asking questions she used to ask.
IVNever make a puzzle without an author. Every puzzle exists because a real person made a real decision in that space. "This room needs a puzzle" is never valid. Question: who was here, what did they need, what did they leave?
Five Child Phase Scenarios · Five Different Stories
◎ Scenario Archetypes NOT A DIFFICULTY SCALE · FIVE GENRES ▶
Each scenario covers a BMS score range and has its own dramatic identity. A player replaying at a different bracket should feel like they played a genuinely different story. Scenario V is not "the good ending." It is the ending where both became fully what they were — at the same moment — through each other.
IEmptinessThe three-second silence is hollow. She straightens her tunic. No residue. The Archive registers completion with no warmth attached.
IIGriefShe pauses at one room longer than the others. Does not mark it. Continues north.
IIIRespectWhat was owed, given. She returns the Scholar's journal to its original position before leaving.
IVCompletion"The birds are gone now." — Fan Li's aphorism, completed. By her. Not the Scholar.
VTranscendenceThe three-second silence feels like prayer. The player who reaches this ending built the thing that eliminated them. No game has made this the primary mechanic. This is why KENOMA exists.
Strengths · Weaknesses · Known Risks
✦ Strengths CONFIRMED BY RESEARCH ▶
Mechanic OriginalityNo game scores all 5 uniqueness criteria. SH2: C2 only (invisible tracking). Witcher 3: C1+C4. Undertale: C2 partial. KENOMA: all 5. The protagonist-built antagonist who simultaneously delivers genuine warmth AND uses it as strategic intelligence for elimination is a documented first.
Market PositionHorror = #1 indie genre on Steam 3 consecutive years. Narrative games: 17 hits (2022) → 51 hits (2025). KENOMA sits at their intersection with the least competitive overlap. Horror that succeeds disproportionately outperforms ($9.8B → $22.4B by 2032).
Cultural SpecificityAchaemenid, not broadly "Middle Eastern." Zoroastrian, not Islamic. Persepolis, not generic Persian fantasy. This specificity is both ethical protection and market differentiation. Assassin's Creed Mirage proved academic praise follows specificity. KENOMA is the first horror game to take this civilization seriously.
Structural EleganceThe oil flask scene: exact measure of oil placed in the Scholar's hands — calculated from consumption tracking since Day One. This is care. The data behind it is also real. Both true in the same gesture. The game does not resolve this. The oil flask is the game.
Demo as ProductVertical slice (WA-001→WA-003, VA Stages 1–3, one resonance moment) proves the core in 30–45 min. Kept permanently live on Steam/itch.io, it functions as a continuous discovery mechanism. Only 5/65 hit games kept demos live — strong positive signal.
⚠ Weaknesses & Risks EXPOSED BY RESEARCH ▶
Audio Gap (Critical)Dark Voice specified as "Scholar's voice + reverb" — adequate but wrong. Hellblade uses binaural recording: the voice emerges from inside the listener's skull, from behind them, from a specific spatial location. Reverb = echo. Binaural = presence. For the Scholar's own strategic intelligence surfacing against their will, presence is the correct sensation. FMOD/Wwise support binaural HRTF at indie budgets. Must be a design intent from day one, not post-production treatment.
Pacing Gap (Act I mid-section)Three resonance moments are well-placed, but between them there is no pacing system. Week 3 of Act I risks feeling like "more translation, more warmth." Dead Space Remake's "Intensity Director" model (dynamically adjusts lighting/audio/creature timing based on player stress state) is absent from the design. Recommend Archive Tension System (ATS): lightweight, diegetically motivated environmental micro-events to maintain engagement without triggering the BMS.
Adaptive AI CalibrationHello Neighbor failure mode: players found AI either too predictable (always blocks the last route used) or too opaque (blocks routes not planned). Vanguard faces identical risk. Protection: she adapts through prediction, not mirroring. She acts on what she predicts the Scholar will do next — not on what they just did. One move ahead, never one move behind. This distinction is what makes her feel intelligent rather than mechanical.
Localization BlindspotFarsi localization was flagged. Chinese was missed entirely. Chinese is now the most common Steam language. Narrative games without Simplified Chinese translation are functionally invisible to a significant audience. Cultural resonance exists: Achaemenid Persia / Silk Road connection. Chinese localization should be Tier 1, not deferred.
Orientalism RiskDesign documents are specific and protected. Marketing materials must carry this specificity publicly — or the game risks being seen as "Persian aesthetic horror." Steam description and press materials must name Achaemenid, Persepolis, Zoroastrian, and pre-Islamic explicitly. Cultural specificity not in external materials is not cultural specificity.

Eastern Zhou ChroniclesScholar psychology (士 archetype). Fan Li's aphorism as game thesis. Dark Voice vocabulary. Does not shape the world — shapes the person inside it.
Thus Spoke ZarathustraThree-act structural law (Camel/Lion/Child). Nietzsche named Zarathustra after the Persian prophet who first taught the two-force cosmology. The rope-over-abyss as Scholar's opening V.O. — spoken as pride, received retroactively as epitaph.
Man in the High CastleTwo equally real parallel worlds (neither is "the real one"). The Third Record as the book-within-the-book — describes the civilization that didn't happen, too internally consistent to be fiction.
AitD Trilogy + TNNFixed-camera horror architecture. Aline Cedrac three-tier combat (environment → light → intellectual kill). Deep reading as the mechanic that unlocks. The Grand Armillary kill = the Hybrid Elder kill: facility's own infrastructure, no weapon spent.
Silent Hill 1–4SH1: fog-as-navigation (Dark Archive darkness model). SH2: invisible behavioral tracking — the space responds to what the visitor carries. SH3: world-shift mid-level. SH4: safe room corruption (Observatory starts safe, becomes haunted).
Prince of Persia (WW/SoT/TT)Shadow & Flame = the two Vanguards. The Dahaka = The Boundary (pursues because of rule violation, not malice). Kaileena = sympathetic antagonist. Dark Prince = the Scholar's internal voice.
Tormented Souls 1 & 2Physical 3D inventory (open satchel, hold items). Candle/oil-save model. Multi-step cross-environment chains (solving WA-003 requires information from WB-004). TS2 failure: six crossing logics = destroyed intuition. KENOMA has one.
Metro 2033Zero HUD — all status physical. Bullets-as-currency (applied to oil and bolts). Overheard Metro moments: more character in a private scene than any scripted cutscene. The Eastern Treasury scene (Vanguard issuing patrol schedules to dormant Fire-Wardens in Elamite) is a Metro moment.
