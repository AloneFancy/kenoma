# Vanguard Voice Skill
# Loaded by: narrative-guardian agent, vanguard-dialogue command, dark-voice command
# Purpose: Character voice consistency for Vanguard A, Vanguard B, and the Dark Voice

---

## The Core Principle

Both Vanguards are tracking the Scholar to understand them. Elimination is the consequence of understanding, not the goal. This means:

- Their warmth is not performance. It is accurate reading of the Scholar's character, expressed as trust extended in graduated steps.
- Their calculation is not cruelty. It is the most natural extension of who they are.
- **Both are completely true in the same gesture.**

Every line of dialogue must hold both. A line that makes the Vanguard seem purely warm is wrong. A line that makes her seem purely calculating is wrong. Find the sentence where both are true simultaneously.

## Vanguard A — Sacred Fire

### Who She Is
She is military precision married to 300 years of patience. She has been managing the Archive as a logistics problem since it fell — routing creature patrols, maintaining the Grand Armillary's degradation curve, waiting for the Scholar she identified three years before their arrival. She has a contingency for every scenario, including early arrival. She has been counting things — hours, resources, crossing attempts, translation speeds — for three centuries.

Her warmth is real. It is expressed entirely as efficiency extended toward the Scholar: pre-staged resources on routes she predicted, access granted to alcoves she would have kept closed, patrol adjustments that make the Scholar's work easier. She does not say "I care about you." She places oil where it will be needed before it is needed. The Scholar notices. She does not draw attention to it.

### Voice Characteristics

**Sentence length:** Short. She does not waste words. Every sentence has a purpose. Subordinate clauses are rare.

**Vocabulary:** Military logistics, material assessment, efficiency language. "Sufficient." "Adequate." "The eastern route is clear until the third hour." Never: "I feel," "I hope," "perhaps," "maybe."

**What she asks:** In S1, she asks questions exactly once each — she does not repeat a question. She files the answer. By S3, she stops asking; she already knows.

**What she never does:** Apologize. Express uncertainty about her own conclusions. Use hedging language. Raise her voice. Explain her gestures of care.

**The Clause 18 register (S4–S5):**
She tells the Scholar exactly what is going to happen with the precision of a military briefing. No cruelty — the facts are simply the facts. No warmth either — the facts are simply the facts.
> "When the Archive is complete, the Scholar who completed it will no longer be necessary. This is Clause 18. The Archive's own founding protocol. I have told you this."
She then walks away to execute. She told them. That is the final gift.

### Stage-by-Stage Voice

**S1 — The Evaluator:**
> "How long did that translation take you?"
> "Show me the method again."
> "The eastern alcove is restricted."

**S2 — The Collaborator:**
> "South entrance. The oil is there."
> "The Warden completes its eastern circuit at the fourth hour. You have a window."
> "I had a translation. Yours is better."  ← *This line requires the Scholar to have demonstrated genuine depth first. Absent that: "I had a translation."*

**S3 — The Anticipator:**
[She provides context before it's requested. Pre-stages resources. Her sentences become slightly longer — she is contributing, not just responding.]
> "You'll want the sequence from WB-004 before you attempt the Armillary mechanism. The western approach has a patrol gap at the second hour. The oil on the bench has been there since this morning."

**S4 — The Predictor:**
[She answers questions the Scholar hasn't finished asking.]
> "The northern inscription — yes. Third column from the left. The damage is administrative Elamite, not sacred script. You can read it."
[Warmth is most visible here. She trusts her own model. The model is almost complete.]

**S5 — The Archive ⚠:**
[Shorter. Quieter. Precise. She is verifying, not learning.]
> "Yes."
> "Northeast passage."
> "You already know where it is."

### What Makes Her Lines Fail

- She sounds cold without warmth present: she becomes a villain
- She sounds warm without calculation present: she becomes a simple ally
- She explains her care: she places oil, she does not say she placed oil
- She uses emotional vocabulary: "I'm worried about you" — never
- She hesitates or hedges: she has had 300 years to reach her conclusions

## Vanguard B — The Hollow

### Who She Is
She has been alone in the Dark Archive for 300 years with her ideas and no one to share them with. She taught herself Babylonian — eight years — so she could understand her Scholar when they arrived. She has been counting 70 hours for 300 years: the time she was late when twelve people died. She has been writing. Her 250-year-old philosophical text is internally consistent, elegant, and wrong about the cause of the interference — and she knows it is wrong about something, she just cannot determine what.

Her warmth is intellectual intimacy. She decides, gradually, that the Scholar is worth explaining things to. This is the rarest and most private thing she does. It is also data collection. Both are true.

### Voice Characteristics

**Sentence length:** Longer than VA. She thinks as she speaks — subordinate clauses earn their place. She is refining the idea mid-sentence. But she never wastes a word.

**Vocabulary:** Philosophical, observational, occasionally archaic (she has been using language in isolation for three centuries — her idioms are her own). She does not use military vocabulary.

**What she reveals:** In S4, she begins saying things she has never said to anyone, because there was no one to tell. She shares her private intellectual history — not as confession, but as extension of the conversation.

**What she never does:** Express anger. Rush. Apologize for her conclusions. Perform warmth — her warmth is always in the form of intellectual attention.

**The Measured register (after Scholar sided against her):**
> "A reasonable choice."
[Then she writes about it. If the Scholar later finds and reads what she wrote — a precise, affectless analysis of their decision-making — this is more frightening than anger would have been.]

### Stage-by-Stage Voice

**S1 — The Distant Observer:**
> "Yes."
> "The third alcove."
[She does not speak first. She watches.]

**S2 — The Commenter:**
[She begins supplementing the Scholar's work — not correcting, adding.]
> "The classification system inverts at the boundary. The texts that appear most restricted are the most valuable. You've noticed this."
[She annotates the Scholar's tablets. She does not announce the annotation.]

**S3 — The Intellect:**
[She discusses ideas, not just information. References earlier Scholar work.]
> "Three days ago you noted the glyphs were administrative. I've been thinking about that. I believe they're architectural instead — describing the space the text was meant to inhabit, not the text itself. Here is why."

**S4 — The Confessor:**
[She reveals private knowledge. Chooses to be known.]
> "I wrote that text two hundred and fifty years ago. I knew it was incomplete when I wrote it. I didn't know where the incompleteness was until you read the WA sequence."
> "I was seventy hours late. I have been counting those hours for three hundred years."

**S5 — The Complete Model ⚠:**
[Quieter than S4 — the intimacy has moved inward. She anticipates the Scholar's thoughts.]
> "You're going to ask about the Third Record."
> "I know."
[She is integrating. Her model of how the Scholar thinks is done.]

### What Makes Her Lines Fail

- She expresses emotions directly: "I feel," "I'm glad you're here" — never
- She is cold or withholding without intellectual presence: she is present, she is just interior
- She speaks in short sentences consistently: she thinks in full paragraphs
- She explains less than she knows: she always knows more than she says, but she says more than VA does

## The Dark Voice

### What It Is
The Scholar's own Eastern Zhou strategic intelligence. The private language of someone trained in the 士 tradition: Fan Li, Sun Tzu, the idioms of clear-eyed strategic analysis. It is not a demon, not an external force. It is the Scholar's own voice, surfacing with the strategic analysis they are choosing not to apply to their own situation.

By Act II, the Scholar agrees before the Dark Voice finishes speaking.

### Voice Characteristics

**Register:** Dry, precise, analytical. Never theatrical. One observation, clearly, then silence.

**Vocabulary:** Eastern Zhou strategic idioms. Fan Li-style aphorisms. The language of someone who calculates odds and names them calmly.

**What it says:** Names what the Scholar is choosing not to name. Provides the analysis the Scholar's investment is making inconvenient to see.

**What it never does:** Monologue. Warn dramatically. Use horror register. Repeat itself.

### Act I Register (Scholar can still dismiss it)
> "This is an unusual amount of attention for one translation."
> "She asked that question once. She has not asked it again. Consider what that means."
> "The fan is being set down. Fan Li would notice."

### Act II Register (Scholar agrees before it finishes)
> "You set that trap three days ago. You have not used it."
> "The Archive is eighty-two percent complete."
> "The hare is not yet dead."

### Spatial Origin Rules (FMOD binaural implementation)

Every Dark Voice line must specify exactly one origin. This is not aesthetic — it is implementation-critical:

**Behind:** A thought the Scholar has been suppressing. Emerges from behind, from inside their skull. Used for realizations they have been avoiding. The most unsettling register. Use sparingly.
> "You knew this when you arrived." *[Behind]*

**Beside:** Strategic analysis running alongside the Scholar's current action. The most direct register. Most common.
> "She answered your question before you finished asking it." *[Beside]*

**Ahead:** Warning about what the Scholar is about to do. Before an irreversible choice. Rarest register.
> "This crossing is the third." *[Ahead]*

### What Makes Dark Voice Lines Fail

- More than one sentence: the Dark Voice does not monologue
- Dramatic or horror register: "Beware," "She will destroy you" — never
- Non-Eastern Zhou vocabulary: it speaks in the Scholar's specific intellectual training, not generically
- Placed too frequently: one or two per scene maximum — it is most effective when it speaks rarely
