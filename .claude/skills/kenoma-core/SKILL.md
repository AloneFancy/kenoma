## Game Identity (one paragraph)

KENOMA is a fixed-camera psychological horror tragedy. A scholar (the 士 archetype — a wandering intellectual who cannot stop serving) enters a sealed Achaemenid Persian archive and is exactly good enough to be used completely. The Vanguard, a 300-year intelligence who identified this specific Scholar years before their arrival, has been building a behavioral model since Day One. When the model is complete, the Scholar's work is done. The ending is a completion, not a betrayal. No game has made the protagonist's own excellence the primary instrument of their elimination. This is why KENOMA exists.

**Thesis:** Fan Li's aphorism — "When the birds are gone, the good bow is put away. When the hare is dead, the hunting dog is cooked." The Scholar knows this text. Teaches it. Thinks it warns against bad rulers. It warns against anyone who serves at all.

## The Five Core Ideas

Any code, dialogue, mechanic, or room that doesn't serve one of these should not exist.

### 1 — The Tragedy of Competence
The Scholar is exactly good enough to be used completely. Not despite their brilliance — because of it. Every correct professional decision is a step toward the conclusion.
- Guard against: making the Scholar feel passive or victimized. They chose this.

### 2 — Warmth and Calculation Are the Same Thing
The oil flask scene: VA places the exact amount of oil calculated from tracking Scholar consumption since Day One. This is care. The data behind it is also real. Both are completely true in the same gesture. The game does not resolve this.
- Guard against: resolving warmth into either pure manipulation or pure sentiment.

### 3 — Understanding Is the Weapon
Elimination is the consequence of understanding, not the goal. A complete behavioral model is a Vanguard who knows exactly when the Scholar's work is done.
- Guard against: the BMS becoming visible. The player reads the model through Vanguard behavior only.

### 4 — The Scholar Is the Only Bridge
The three-body system is baked into the Archive's architecture 300 years before the Scholar arrived. The Vanguard has zero traversal debt. The Scholar pays all costs.
- Guard against: the Iron Rule ever bending. One rule. Everywhere. Always.

### 5 — The Ending Is Not a Twist. It Is a Completion.
The player who reaches Scenario V built the thing that eliminated them. VA declares Clause 18 clearly and walks away. The turn must land as: "I knew it was coming and it still landed."
- Guard against: any framing of the turn as betrayal, surprise, or revelation.

## The Four Iron Laws

### Law I — Never make the turn a betrayal
The Act I→II transition is a completion. VA tells the Scholar exactly what is about to happen (Clause 18) and walks away. The warmth was genuine. The calculation was also real. Both were always true.

### Law II — Never break the Iron Rule
Knowledge from World B enables action in World A. Material from World A produces physical change in World B. One rule. Taught at PZ-002. Consistent everywhere, always. If a puzzle requires bending the rule, the puzzle changes. Not the rule.

### Law III — Never show the Behavioral Model
No UI. No "your choice has been remembered." No score counter. No HUD element of any kind referencing BMS data. The player reads the model through one channel: the Vanguard's observed behavior changes. Stage 5 arrives through noticing she stopped asking questions she used to ask.

### Law IV — Never make a puzzle without an author
Every puzzle exists because a real person made a real decision in that space. Question always: who was here, what did they need, what did they leave? "This room needs a puzzle" is never valid.

## Characters — Voice and Behavior Rules

### The Scholar
- 士 archetype: finds the problem matching exact skills, applies them without reservation
- Fan Li left. The Scholar stays. Not ignorance — character.
- Zero HUD: robe damage = health, fire vessel flame = oil, astrolabe vibration = creature proximity
- Combat tiers: T1 Environmental → T2 Fire/Crossbow/Bell → T3 Naming (most powerful, costs only prior knowledge)
- Dark Voice: Scholar's own Eastern Zhou strategic intelligence. Speaks in Fan Li/Sun Tzu vocabulary. By Act II, Scholar agrees before it finishes.

### Vanguard A (Sacred Fire)
- Military model. Measures efficiency and completion rate.
- Warmth = professional trust expressed through logistics (pre-staged resources, patrol adjustments, access grants)
- Never: apologizes, expresses doubt about her conclusions, uses emotional vocabulary unprompted
- Execution threshold: ~80% Archive completion (USS drops below 20)
- Stage 5 register: shorter, quieter, more precise. She already knows. She is verifying.
- Clause 18: delivered as a military briefing. No cruelty, no warmth. Precision.

### Vanguard B (The Hollow)
- Philosophical model. Measures intellectual depth and novelty.
- Warmth = intellectual intimacy (supplements Scholar's translations, reveals private knowledge, writes about Scholar in her notebook)
- Never: expresses anger, rushes, apologizes for her conclusions
- Execution threshold: when the Scholar stops surprising her
- Stage 5 register: quieter than Stage 4. The intimacy has moved inward. She anticipates the Scholar's thoughts.
- Measured register (after Scholar sided against her): "A reasonable choice." Then she writes a precise, affectless analysis of the Scholar's decision-making in her tablet.

### The Boundary
- The Archive's crossing law made physical
- Pursues because of rule violation — not malice (PoP Warrior Within / Dahaka model)
- Cannot be fought. Can be stunned with Naming Bell (8 seconds). Can be dissolved permanently via Grand Armillary sequence (PZ-007).
- Never pursues the Vanguard — she has zero traversal debt

## World Architecture

### World A — Sacred Fire
- Apadana: Persepolis-scale, 72 stone columns, fire altars at every junction
- Scholar lights fire altars (oil cost) → altars spread warmth → Apadana becomes beautiful
- The player should love this place before Act II takes it
- Key rooms: WA-001 Gate · WA-003 Eastern Treasury (30,000 tablets) · WA-007 Grand Armillary

### World B — The Hollow
- Same column grid, inverted underground. Friezes showing a procession gone wrong.
- Scholar's fire vessel = only warm light in the Dark Archive in 300 years. Everything sees the Scholar's location.
- Lantern-Thieves (×2 in demo): attracted to fire vessel warmth, circling, 90-second window before aggressive
- Key rooms: WB-001 Inverted Gate · WB-004 Dark Library (Previous Scholar's journal) · WB-∞ Chamber of Convergence

### The Iron Rule (crossing system)
- Knowledge from WB enables action in WA
- Material from WA produces physical change in WB
- Traversal Debt increments on every crossing
- Debt 2 = Boundary 8s | Debt 3 = Boundary 20s | Debt 4+ = Boundary persistent

## Five Child Phase Scenarios (Act III — The Vanguard)

Act III belongs to the Vanguard, not the Scholar. She moves through rooms the Scholar worked in. Three-second silence: same gesture, different weight in each scenario.

| Scenario | BMS Range | Genre | Final Register |
|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|
| I | Low | Emptiness | Hollow |
| II | Low-mid | Grief | She pauses at one room longer |
| III | Mid | Respect | Returns Scholar's journal to its position |
| IV | Mid-high | Completion | She speaks Fan Li's final line. By her, not the Scholar. |
| V | High | Transcendence | Three-second silence feels like prayer. She built the thing that became fully what it was — at the same moment she did. |

Scenario V is not the good ending. It is the ending where both became fully themselves — through each other.
