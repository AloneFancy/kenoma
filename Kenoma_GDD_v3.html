Chapter 01
Design Thesis & Five Unbreakable Rules
設計理念 · 五條不可違反的法則

Logline

A fixed-camera psychological horror tragedy where you play a wandering scholar-strategist who guides a brilliant tactician through the ruins of a dead civilization across two synchronized dimensions — unaware you are constructing the architecture of your own elimination. Every puzzle solved feeds her mind. Every weapon repaired arms her hand. Every forbidden text you translate gives her the words to rewrite the world without you.

知人者智，自知者明。勝人者有力，自勝者強。 "To know others is wisdom. To know oneself is enlightenment. To conquer others is strength. To conquer oneself is power." — Laozi, Tao Te Ching · The Scholar cannot conquer themselves. They cannot stop building. This is why they die.

The Five Unbreakable Design Rules

These rules govern every design decision at every level. No mechanic, level element, UI prompt, or narrative beat may contradict them.

I
No Phase 2. No Second Chance.

The Vanguard's final state is absolute and apparent from the first second of the Child Phase. There is no mid-fight transformation, no health bar refill, no "true form." True power does not require a second act to convince the observer. She was always this. Ref: Established in conversation, reinforced by the design.

II
The Scholar Cannot Fight.

Combat is not available to the Scholar at any point in the game. Every hostile encounter is solved through evasion, environmental manipulation, luring, or puzzle-resolution. The player's power is entirely intellectual. This is the Aline Cedrac rule. Ref: AitD: The New Nightmare.

III
The Game Watches Without Speaking.

No indicator, prompt, or UI element communicates that the player's behavior is being tracked toward an ending. No karma meter. No relationship gauge. No dialogue branch flags. The behavioral tracking system is entirely invisible. Ref: Silent Hill 2's ending criteria.

IV
Every Saved Resource Belongs to the Vanguard.

Items the Scholar refuses to give do not disappear — they degrade, attract creatures, or become the sole tools available in the Lion Phase. Hoarding is not safety. It is a different kind of investment in a different outcome. There is no neutral choice. Ref: SH4's haunted items mechanic.

V
The Vanguard Is Always Human.

The Vanguard never transforms into a supernatural entity. Her ascension in the Child Phase is entirely psychological — the evolution of a superior mind that has absorbed the full logic of the Archive. No wings, no glowing form, no second anatomy. She puts her coat on. She straightens her blade. That is enough. Ref: Core design doctrine established in conversation.

VI
The Safe Room is Never Truly Safe.

The Scholar establishes a base camp (the Observatory) in the Camel Phase, exactly as the player establishes safe rooms in the genre they trust. Over time, this space is contaminated by the Scholar's own choices. Items left there develop a corruption state. The Vanguard can track it through incense residue. Safety is a resource that depletes. Ref: Silent Hill 4: The Room. AitD safe-room structure.

Literary Sources

Historical Novel · Literature
Chronicles of Eastern Zhou Kingdoms
東周列國志 · Dōng Zhōu Liè Guó Zhì · Feng Menglong, 17th century (events 770–221 BC)
770–221 BC
Spring & Autumn /
Warring States

A 108-chapter historical novel covering the fragmentation of the Zhou Dynasty into warring kingdoms. The recurring tragedy is the 士 (Shì) — wandering scholar-strategists who gave their brilliance to rulers who used them until the war was won, then discarded or executed them. Wu Qi reformed the Chu army and was torn apart by ministers after the king died. Shang Yang wrote the laws that made Qin dominant and was executed under those very laws. Su Qin held six kingdoms in alliance simultaneously and was assassinated. Fan Li survived only by leaving before victory was complete.

The 合縱連橫 (Vertical and Horizontal Alliances) strategy describes kingdoms simultaneously allied with, and undermining, one another — every party serving their own interest while appearing cooperative. This is the dual-Vanguard political structure made historical fact.

→ Kenoma Integration Points
The 士 Archetype
The Scholar IS a 士. Brilliant, transactional, constitutionally unable to stop serving the system that will kill them. Their fate is not tragedy — it is historical law.
Fan Li's Aphorism
The game's thesis, spoken in the opening cinematic as hope. Understood in the ending as its true meaning. The Scholar is the bow. The bow is put away.
合縱連橫
The dual-Vanguard structure: both are simultaneously allied with the Scholar and working against each other. The Scholar is the small state caught between two great powers — essential to both, protected by neither.
Chu Kingdom Shamanism
World B's entire aesthetic. The Chu people's shamanistic rituals, dark cosmology, and supernatural folklore traditions source every creature design, ritual puzzle, and environmental element in the Sunken Archive.
The Dark Voice
Mid-Camel Phase: the Scholar begins hearing Sun Tzu and Fan Li aphorisms as a second internal voice — their own strategic intelligence warning them. Inspired by the Warring States tradition of self-counsel. Maps to PoP Two Thrones' Dark Prince voice.
Philosophical Novel · Literature
Thus Spoke Zarathustra
Also sprach Zarathustra · Friedrich Nietzsche · 1883–1885
1883–1885
German
Philosophical

Nietzsche's most complete philosophical work, structured as prophetic narrative. The Three Metamorphoses (Camel, Lion, Child) describe the evolution of the human spirit from burden-bearer to rebel to creator. The Tightrope Walker parable illustrates the precise mechanism of the Scholar's betrayal — the Camel's careful progress is simply bypassed by the Lion's leap, and the Camel falls without malice being involved. The Übermensch/Last Man axis defines the quality of the Vanguard's final ascension.

→ Kenoma Integration Points
Three Metamorphoses
Literal game phase structure. Camel = cooperative arc. Lion = betrayal/survival arc. Child = the execution arc. Not metaphor. Mechanical law.
Tightrope Walker Parable
The specific mechanism of betrayal: the Vanguard does not turn evil. She leaps over the Scholar's careful progress because she has outgrown its pace. The Scholar's death is the natural consequence of her ascension — not its cause.
Übermensch vs. Last Man
The ending spectrum's philosophical axis. A Colorful Vanguard achieves Übermensch status — terrifying, complete, creative. A Poor Vanguard collapses into the Last Man: hollow, broken, unable to create, ruling a dead world.
"Man is a rope"
The Scholar's core monologue. They quote this as self-definition — "I am the bridge." The horror is that they are correct. Bridges are not destinations. The Scholar is a means, not an end.

Game Sources

Video Game Series
Alone in the Dark — Trilogy & The New Nightmare
Infogrames · 1992 (AitD 1) · 1993 (AitD 2) · 1994 (AitD 3) · 2001 (The New Nightmare)
1992–2001
Survival Horror
PC / PS1

AitD 1 (1992): The genre origin. Pre-rendered backgrounds, fixed camera angles, Edward Carnby, the Derceto mansion. Critical rule: some creatures cannot be defeated in combat — the player must find puzzle-based solutions or environmental workarounds. Established fixed-camera as authorial composition rather than technical limitation. The 15-slot inventory grid and document-reading mechanic. The first use of pre-rendered camera angles as psychological constriction.

AitD 2 (1993): Jack is the pirate captain-villain who has assumed a supernatural authority — a being of stolen power and false identity. Maps to the Vanguard's relationship with the Archive's knowledge: she is absorbing a power that isn't native to her yet.

AitD 3 (1994): The gold mines setting — greed and ambition as corruption vectors. The Vanguard's hunger for the Traversal Skill follows this logic: the Archive's knowledge is the gold, and its lure has already made her dangerous long before she acts.

AitD: The New Nightmare (2001): The most directly relevant entry. Two playable characters — Edward Carnby (combat-specialist) and Aline Cedrac (researcher/lore-specialist). The player chooses one; the other is AI-controlled. They navigate the same island from different paths, communicate via radio, and each possess knowledge the other lacks. Carnby without Aline misses critical lore. Aline without Carnby is defenseless. The partnership is mandatory and temporary. A shadow creature can only be harmed by light — flashlight as weapon. Aline reads documents to unlock narrative; document-reading has a mechanical cost.

→ Kenoma Integration Points
AitD:TNN Dual Structure
Scholar (Aline) and Vanguard (Carnby) as the fundamental character architecture. The player is ALWAYS Aline — the researcher who cannot fight. The Vanguard is ALWAYS the AI Carnby — the fighter who cannot read the Archive without the Scholar's translation.
Radio Communication
The Scholar and Vanguard communicate across dimensional boundaries via a brass resonance instrument — a Zhou-era astronomical tuning device. It can be used to request the Vanguard's position, share a puzzle solution, or warn of approaching threats. In the Lion Phase, it goes silent on the Vanguard's end.
AitD 1 — No-Combat Rule
The Scholar NEVER fights. Every creature encounter is a puzzle, an evasion, an environmental solution. Some creatures cannot be stopped at all — only outrun or lured away from critical paths. This is the AitD 1 doctrine applied absolutely.
AitD:TNN — Light as Weapon
The Scholar's jade lamp (their only carried item with offensive utility) harms shadow-class creatures from World B that manifest in World A. Not a weapon — a tool that happens to deter specific threats. It also burns incense at altar-saves.
AitD 1 — 15-Slot Inventory Grid
The Scholar carries a 15-slot physical inventory. Items exist as 3D objects that can be examined and rotated. Combining items requires physical adjacency in the grid. The slot structure is a hard constraint — nothing auto-sorts. Combined with TS1's 3D manipulation interface.
AitD 1 — Fixed Camera as Authorship
Every camera angle is a composed painting. The Vanguard appears in the background of Scholar-focused shots throughout the Camel Phase — always watching, always still. The player builds dread simply by noticing her attention. Camera cuts are zone-triggered, not player-controlled.
Video Game Series
Silent Hill 1 · 2 · 3 · 4: The Room
Konami / Team Silent · 1999 (SH1) · 2001 (SH2) · 2003 (SH3) · 2004 (SH4)
1999–2004
Psychological Horror
PlayStation

SH1 (1999): Harry Mason searching for Cheryl in a fog-covered town. The Otherworld manifests from Alessa's trauma — the monsters are her fear made physical. The radio crackles with static when creatures approach (proximity system). Fog as visibility control. The town is psychologically specific to its source trauma.

SH2 (2001): The masterpiece. James Sunderland receives a letter from his dead wife and goes to Silent Hill. The Otherworld is James's own guilt manifested. Pyramid Head exists because James wants to be punished. Endings are determined by silent behavioral tracking: how many times James reads Mary's letter, whether he examines Maria obsessively, whether he keeps his health intentionally low. No UI element communicates this tracking. The player finds out only at the ending. Multiple routes to several endings, each reflecting a psychological truth about the player's unconscious behavior.

SH3 (2003): Heather/Alessa — the protagonist carries within her the origin of the Otherworld. The transition between Normal and Otherworld states happens mid-level. Mirror mechanic: mirrors show the Otherworld state even in the Normal world. The transformation is reactive to Heather's internal state, not to external events.

SH4: The Room (2004): Henry Townshend is trapped in his apartment (Room 302). He can only leave through a hole in the wall. The Room starts as a safe haven but progressively becomes haunted as Henry returns between levels — objects accumulate corruption, ghost-victims follow him through every world, haunted items left in the Room drain his max health. The hole in the wall IS the traversal mechanic made literal.

→ Kenoma Integration Points
SH4 — The Room / The Observatory
The Scholar's Observatory (base camp) is The Room. Starts safe. Progressively haunted as items touched by the Vanguard are returned. Items with Vanguard-contact accumulate a corruption state — carried, they drain health. The Scholar must spend incense to cleanse them or leave them behind.
SH4 — The Hole / Traversal
Traversal focal points ARE holes in the world's fabric — astronomical instruments that function as dimensional apertures. The Scholar observes through the instrument before committing to the cross (SH4's peering through the hole before entering). The crossing has a physical cost: a brief animation in which the Scholar appears to fold through space.
SH2 — Silent Behavior Tracking
The game silently tracks: how fully the Scholar reads each forbidden text (depth), how often they check the resonance instrument, average health maintained, items donated vs. hoarded, traps set vs. left disarmed. This feeds the 0–100 spectrum score per world. No UI element ever acknowledges the tracking.
SH1/2 — Monsters as Complicity
Archive creatures are not generic. Each creature type manifests a specific category of Scholar decision. The creature that appears after the Scholar gives a weapon to the Vanguard is different from the one that appears after they hoard medicine. Players who engage deeply with the pattern begin to read their own behavioral history in the monster design.
SH1 — Radio Static
The Scholar's brass compass buzzes with static when hostile entities approach. In World B, static intensity correlates to both distance AND the emotional significance of the encounter — it buzzes harder near creatures that manifest the Scholar's most consequential acts. The compass is a conscience made audible.
SH3 — Mid-Level World Shift
Both worlds have a Normal and Otherworld state. The Otherworld shift in World A transforms bronze halls into biological clockwork. In World B, submerged spaces shift into total darkness broken only by the Scholar's lamp. Triggers are based on accumulated Scholar decisions, not scripted story moments.
SH2 — Pyramid Head Logic
The Archive Guardian (the game's unskippable pursuer entity) exists because the Scholar is subconsciously aware they are building their executioner and cannot stop. It appears more frequently as Camel Phase generosity increases — the more the Scholar gives, the more the Guardian pursues. It represents their borrowed time, made visible.
Video Game Series
Prince of Persia — II · Sands of Time · Warrior Within · The Two Thrones
Jordan Mechner · 1993 (II) / Ubisoft Montreal · 2003 · 2004 · 2005
1993–2005
Action / Adventure
PC / PS2

PoP II: The Shadow and the Flame (1993): The Prince finds his palace conquered and his identity stolen by a disguised Jaffar. His own shadow pursues him as a doppelganger. The Shadow and the Flame are two supernatural forces in opposition. The Prince must reclaim his own identity by the end. This maps directly to the two Vanguards as "Shadow" (World B, Sunken Archive) and "Flame" (World A, Waking Archive) — two aspects of one consuming force, opposed and complementary.

Sands of Time (2003): Farah is the companion whose agenda is never fully aligned with the Prince's. She steals the Dagger of Time briefly because she calculates that her solution is better — not out of betrayal but out of cold, independent reasoning. She does not apologize. The ending: the Prince reverses time entirely. Farah never knows any of it happened. Only the Prince carries the memory of everything that was built and lost.

Warrior Within (2004): The Dahaka is an unstoppable entity that hunts the Prince because he altered time. It CANNOT be defeated in combat through most of the game — it must be outrun. Kaileena (the Empress of Time) is an antagonist trying to prevent her own death — sympathetic, calculating, using the Prince as a means to her own survival. The Sand Wraiths are shadow-versions of the Prince from corrupted timelines.

The Two Thrones (2005): The Dark Prince is the Prince's corrupted alternate self, speaking in his ear, offering shortcuts, demanding sand to stay alive. The Prince must choose between integration and rejection of this internal voice.

→ Kenoma Integration Points
PoP II — Shadow & Flame
The two Vanguards ARE the Shadow and Flame. Vanguard A (Waking / Systemic) = The Flame — ordered, consuming, reducing old knowledge to clean architecture. Vanguard B (Sunken / Folkloric) = The Shadow — patient, possessive, claiming what she touches without violence. Each Vanguard is incomplete without the other's quality. The one who absorbs the Traversal Skill gains both.
SoT — Farah's Logic
The Vanguard's betrayal follows Farah's exact reasoning: she does not act out of malice. She calculates that the Scholar's continued existence is the largest remaining inefficiency in her system. She is not wrong. The game never frames this as a moral failure. She is correct within her logic.
SoT — Environmental Traps
During the Camel Phase, the Scholar arms environmental mechanisms: pressure plates, spike corridors, chain-weighted portcullises, oil-vessel pressure systems. These are presented as optional puzzle elements. In the Lion Phase, they become the Scholar's only defensive tools. The Vanguard circumvents traps she observed being set. Only traps she never saw activated can catch her — and only once.
WW — The Dahaka System
The Archive Guardian is the Dahaka. It appears periodically and cannot be fought — only outrun. Its frequency of appearance is tied to the Scholar's use of the Traversal Skill: every dimensional crossing accumulates a "debt," and the Guardian is the debt collector. Heavy Traversal use = more frequent, faster Guardian pursuit. This disincentivizes careless dimensional hopping and makes every crossing a deliberate risk calculation.
WW — Kaileena's Sympathy
The Vanguard is designed with Kaileena's emotional logic. She is not a villain. She is a person who made a completely rational decision given the information she had. Players who understand her fully — and many will — will feel the tragedy of her correctness more than the horror of her power.
TT — The Dark Voice
Mid-Camel Phase, the Scholar develops a second internal voice — their own strategic mind, finally articulating what their academic training has known for hours. It speaks in Eastern Zhou aphorisms. It warns. The player can LISTEN (triggering early sabotage options) or IGNORE (extending cooperation, intensifying Lion Phase). Neither is clearly correct. Both have costs.
SoT — The Erased Memory Ending
In SoT's ending, the Prince rewinds time and Farah remembers nothing. In Kenoma, the Scholar's memory is erased differently: the Vanguard, having achieved the Child Phase, deletes the Scholar from the Archive's history. The lore fragments the Scholar translated now attribute the work to an anonymous source. The Scholar did not exist. They were a bridge. Bridges are not credited.
Video Game Series
Tormented Souls 1 & 2
Dual Effect / Abstract Digital · 2021 (TS1 — Caroline Walker) · 2023 (TS2 — Justine)
2021–2023
Survival Horror
PC / Console

TS1 (2021): Caroline Walker arrives at Winterlake Hospital to investigate a missing twin (again: the duality theme). The game is the most successful modern revival of classic fixed-camera survival horror, featuring: fully 3D inventory manipulation (items are physical objects that can be rotated and examined), a candle-save system (place candles at specific floor positions — limited supply, each position unlocks once per run), health communicated through visible body-horror to the player character rather than a numerical gauge, and a document-reading animation that costs time and leaves Caroline vulnerable. The "combination crafting" system requires items to be physically compatible in the 3D space before combining.

TS2 (2023): Justine as protagonist. Expanded outdoor environments, more complex multi-step puzzle chains, improved item interaction physics, and a wider variety of enemies requiring specific environmental approaches. Proves the format can scale and iterate. TS1+2 together constitute the strongest modern proof-of-concept that this genre format is commercially viable.

→ Kenoma Integration Points
TS1 — 3D Inventory + AitD Grid
The Scholar's inventory combines both systems: AitD's 15-slot grid structure with TS1's 3D physical manipulation interface. Items are placed in grid slots but can be removed and physically examined/rotated before use or combination. The interface is diegetic — the Scholar physically opens their satchel.
TS1 — Candle-Save System
Saves require burning incense at Eastern Zhou bronze altar vessels. Limited supply (~14–18 per run depending on exploration depth). Each altar locks after one use per session. In the Lion Phase, recently used altars emit detectable incense residue. The Vanguard can locate hot altars and position near them — saving becomes a tactical risk.
TS1 — Body Horror Health
The Scholar's health state is communicated entirely through animation and appearance: slowing movement, hunching posture, hand tremor during puzzle interaction, increasing irregularity of breathing sounds. No numerical display. Inspired by TS1's visible degradation approach. The player learns to read the Scholar's body language as a health gauge.
TS1 — Document Reading Cost
Reading forbidden texts (bamboo scrolls, oracle bone inscriptions, bronze inscription plates) triggers a reading animation during which the Scholar cannot move. Full-depth reading provides maximum lore (feeding the behavioral tracking toward Colorful endings) but costs time and leaves the Scholar exposed to the Guardian's timed appearances. Partial reading reduces knowledge gain. TS2's expanded puzzle chains inform how lore fragments combine into full Archive knowledge.
TS1 — Twin/Duality Theme
Caroline searches for her missing twin — the duality motif runs through both TS games. In Kenoma, the Vanguard's "twin" nature (Shadow/Flame, two Vanguards as two aspects of one strategic mind) echoes this. The Scholar's dead dimensional counterpart (Narrator B, whose death enables Traversal) is the "missing twin" they can never find.
TS2 — Scalable Puzzle Chains
TS2's multi-step puzzle chains inform Kenoma's cross-dimensional puzzle design: a solution in World A may require gathering three components across both worlds in sequence. The puzzle grammar scales from single-item solutions in the early Camel Phase to elaborate 5-step cross-dimensional chains in the late Camel Phase.

The Scholar is the Aline Cedrac of this world: the researcher whose presence makes the Archive legible. They are a 士 — a Warring States intellectual mercenary, constitutionally driven to serve the system they inhabit. They cannot fight. Their power is translation, puzzle-resolution, and the unique dimensional privilege of the Traversal Skill.

They carry a leather satchel containing: a jade lamp (their only threat-deterrent), a brass compass (static proximity alarm, per SH1), a dimensional resonance instrument (cross-world communication, per AitD:TNN's radio), bamboo scroll fragments, calibration tools, and available inventory slots for Archive items.

The Dark Voice

Mid-Camel Phase — after the third major puzzle sequence — the Scholar begins hearing a second internal voice. Their own strategic consciousness, finally overriding their academic servility. It speaks in the language of Sun Tzu and Fan Li. "The general who wins a battle makes many calculations before the fight. The general who loses makes few." The player can choose to listen (early sabotage), answer back (escalate toward Lion Phase), or suppress it (maintain cooperation). Drawn from PoP Two Thrones' Dark Prince voice system.

Health Communication

No health bar. The Scholar's condition is read entirely from their body: movement speed, postural degradation, puzzle interface tremor, breath audio irregularity. Drawn from TS1's Caroline Walker approach — the player learns to read the Scholar the way the Scholar reads the Archive.

The Vanguard is the Edward Carnby of this partnership: the fighter who makes the Scholar's research survivable. She protects the Scholar not out of loyalty but because they are the most efficient available key to the Archive's locked systems. She is Edward Carnby filtered through Farah (SoT), Kaileena (WW), and the female military commanders of the Eastern Zhou period — brilliant, calculating, operating under a rational agenda that will eventually supersede the Scholar's utility.

She never transforms. She never glows. She never becomes a monster. Her evolution is entirely in her eyes — in the way she studies the Scholar's hands during a puzzle sequence, the way she stops asking for clarification mid-Camel Phase and simply acts on what she's already deduced. The horror is in watching someone gradually no longer need you.

Vanguard A vs. Vanguard B — Shadow and Flame (PoP II)

Vanguard A (Waking Archive — The Flame): Ordered, consuming, architectural. She fights with clean precision — each strike finding the structural weakness, each movement a tactical calculation. Her voice is quiet, measured. When she absorbs the Traversal Skill, she becomes the Flame: a force that reduces everything it touches to clean, structured ash, ready to build on.

Vanguard B (Sunken Archive — The Shadow): Patient, possessive, mythic. She moves differently — slower, more deliberate, using the Archive's dark spaces as extensions of her own patience. Her voice carries the rhythm of Chu ceremonial speech. When she absorbs the Traversal Skill, she becomes the Shadow: she claims ownership of everything she passes through, silently, without declaration.

The two Vanguards share a single underlying tactical architecture expressed through two aesthetic registers. They are the same intelligence, divided by the dimensional threshold — which is why they cannot coexist once the Traversal Skill is available. Two instances of the same system cannot share the same administrative privileges.

The Archive Guardian — The Dahaka of Kenoma

Drawn from PoP Warrior Within's Dahaka and SH2's Pyramid Head: an entity that cannot be defeated in combat (until the final moments of the Camel Phase, as a deliberate structural feint) and must only be outrun or evaded. The Guardian represents the Scholar's borrowed time — the temporal debt accumulated by a Narrator who should not be traversing dimensions at all.

It appears with increasing frequency as the Scholar uses Traversal more. Its speed increases with each manifestation. Its design is drawn from the Chu cosmological tradition — not a mechanized creature, not a gore-construct, but an entity that appears fundamentally wrong within the physical logic of the Archive. It moves like something that does not belong in three-dimensional space. It is not hostile toward the Vanguard. Only the Scholar. This is not a coincidence the player will miss.

Chapter 04
World Architecture — Waking & Sunken Archive
世界架構 · 醒界與沉界

The Twin Archives — Design Identity

Attribute
★ World A — The Waking Archive
◈ World B — The Sunken Archive
Historical Source
Northern Zhou court architecture: bronze halls, jade-inlaid mechanisms, astronomical instruments, Confucian administrative order imposed on ancient ruins.
Chu Kingdom shamanic tradition: submerged ceremonial spaces, dark ritual chambers, waterlogged bamboo forests, oracle bone shrines.
Visual Identity
Gothic clockwork fused with Eastern Zhou bronze architecture. Iron mechanisms with jade inlays. AitD 1-style fixed-camera compositions — high angles dwarfing the Scholar against massive bronze halls.
Silent Hill Otherworld logic applied to Chu ritual spaces. Black water, bioluminescent algae, dark thorns, submerged statuary. SH3-style mid-level Otherworld shifts into total darkness.
Camera Style
Dutch-angle and high-angle fixed compositions — the AitD 1 approach. Vanguard A visible in background of Scholar shots throughout Camel Phase.
Closer, more intimate fixed angles. Fog limits depth — Silent Hill's fog-as-depth-control. The compass static begins before threats are visible.
Save System
Burning incense at Zhou bronze altar vessels. Limited supply. Post-burn residue is detectable by Vanguard A in Lion Phase.
Placing candles at Chu ritual floor markings. Same resource pool as World A incense — shared constraint. TS1 candle-save system applied to shamanistic context.
Otherworld State
Bronze halls become biological clockwork — organic tissue threaded through iron mechanisms. Triggered by accumulated Scholar decisions (SH3 trigger logic).
Submerged spaces become total darkness broken only by the Scholar's jade lamp. The lamp's light reveals things that only exist when observed — a quantum horror principle.
Creature Logic
Manifestations of systemic complicity — creatures that move like failed mechanisms, like clockwork given corrupted purpose. The Scholar reads their origin in Archive texts.
Manifestations of folkloric guilt — drowned memories, suppressed knowledge, Chu ceremonial figures distorted by the Scholar's refusal to translate their full meaning. SH2 monster-as-guilt principle.
Sound Design
Resonating bronze zhong bells, grinding iron, mechanical rhythm. Compass static is sharp, electrical — SH1 radio crackle applied to brass instrument.
Water movement, distant Chu ceremonial chanting, waterlogged wood creak. Compass static is lower, more organic — almost biological. SH2's industrial ambient applied to underwater spaces.
Vanguard Presence
Vanguard A: appears in background of fixed shots studying mechanisms, testing trap resistance, calibrating her behavioral model of the Scholar.
Vanguard B: harder to locate in fixed shots — she is present but uses the fog and dark water to appear at the edge of compositions, watching from distance.

The Traversal Focal Points — The Holes in the Wall

Directly drawn from SH4: The Room's "hole in the wall" mechanic. Traversal focal points are Eastern Zhou astronomical instruments — bronze armillary spheres, jade orreries, celestial alignment chambers — that function as apertures between dimensions. The Scholar observes through the instrument before crossing (SH4's peering-through-the-hole interaction), and the crossing has a physical cost: the Scholar appears to fold through space, emerging on the other side displaced slightly in time.

SH4: The Room — Integrated System The Observatory (Scholar's base camp) begins as a safe haven, identical to SH4's Room 302. As the run progresses: items given to the Vanguard return to the Observatory in a corrupted state after the Camel Phase ends. Corrupted items carried drain health. Items left in the Observatory long-term accumulate a "haunting" value — above a threshold, the Observatory itself begins spawning environmental hazards. The Scholar must spend incense (save resources) to perform a cleansing ritual, removing corruption at the cost of save capacity.

The Archive Guardian — Traversal Debt System (PoP Warrior Within)

Each Traversal crossing increments a hidden debt counter. The Guardian's spawn frequency is a direct function of this counter. Light use of Traversal (crossing only when necessary) keeps the Guardian rare and predictable. Overuse of Traversal (frequent dimensional hopping to solve problems efficiently) turns the Guardian into a near-constant threat. This is the Dahaka system applied to dimensional mechanics — freedom of traversal has an invisible cost that compounds.

Chapter 05
The Six Core Mechanics — Complete Specification
六大核心機制 · 完整規格

Mechanic 1 — The Tactile Inventory (AitD 1 Grid + TS1 3D Manipulation)

The Scholar's satchel contains a 15-slot grid (AitD 1's slot count) where items are stored as 3D physical objects. When examining an item, the player removes it from the slot and can rotate it freely in a 3D space (TS1's interface). Items have physical properties: weight (affects Scholar movement speed when carried), compatibility (TS2's combination rules — volatile + ignition source = prohibited adjacency), and wear state.

Giving an item to the Vanguard requires physically placing it into her hands in the game world — not through a menu. This is a deliberate design choice: the act of relinquishing a resource is a physical, first-person action with spatial weight. You reach into your satchel, take the item out, and hold it out. She takes it without looking at you. She is already examining it.

AitD:TNN — Asymmetric Information Items given to the Vanguard disappear from the Scholar's inventory — but the player never sees what she does with them. In the Camel Phase, this is fine. In the Lion Phase, the Scholar begins encountering Vanguard-optimized weapons and environmental modifications she has made using the Scholar's donated resources. The donated items have been weaponized against their donor.

Mechanic 2 — The Candle-Incense Save System (TS1 + AitD Safe Room)

Incense sticks (~14–18 per run, exploration-dependent) are burned at bronze altar vessels in World A and placed as candles at Chu ritual floor markings in World B. Both consume from the same resource pool. Each altar/marking locks after one use per session. The Scholar cannot save in the same place twice in one session.

Lion Phase complication: Incense burns leave detectable residue for 15–20 minutes of play time. Vanguard A can detect a hot altar by examining it — if she finds residue, she will position near it or near neighboring altars, anticipating the Scholar's return. The save system becomes a tactical intelligence problem.

Mechanic 3 — The Compass / Static System (SH1 Radio + SH2 Guilt Frequency)

The Scholar's brass compass emits static from its resonance chamber when hostile entities approach. Static intensity encodes two variables: proximity (louder = closer) AND emotional significance (the static changes quality — sharper for creatures manifesting the Scholar's Camel Phase generosity, lower and more organic for creatures manifesting their hoarding decisions). Players who engage with the system develop an intuitive read of their own behavioral history through audio.

Mechanic 4 — Forbidden Text Reading (TS1 + AitD 1 Documents)

Reading bamboo scrolls, oracle bone inscriptions, and bronze plate inscriptions triggers a reading animation (the Scholar unfurls the scroll or holds the plate to their lamp) during which movement is locked. Reading depths:

Skim read (2 seconds): Scholar notes the document exists. No lore gain. No behavioral tracking impact. Standard read (8–12 seconds): Extracts the document's surface content. Moderate lore gain. Adds to behavioral tracking. Deep read (25–40 seconds): Full translation including esoteric content hidden in archaic script. Maximum lore gain. Maximum behavioral tracking. High Guardian spawn probability during the read window. The most valuable texts are the most dangerous to translate fully. The Scholar must decide, in real time, how much they want to know.

Mechanic 5 — Environmental Trap Architecture (PoP Sands of Time + PoP II)

Throughout the Camel Phase, the Scholar discovers and can arm environmental mechanisms. These exist as optional puzzle elements presented without any indication of their Lion Phase utility — a first playthrough player may activate them for puzzle rewards, not knowing they are establishing a defensive infrastructure.

Trap Types: Pressure plate corridors (floor-spike systems from PoP's classic trap vocabulary), chain-weighted portcullises (can seal a room or create a temporary barrier), oil vessel pressure systems (spray flammable residue — activates with the jade lamp), structural weight traps (destabilized ceiling sections that fall on a trigger).

Vanguard Intelligence: Any trap the Vanguard observed the Scholar interact with is flagged in her behavioral model. She will not trigger a flagged trap. She will reset an observed pressure plate so the Scholar triggers it when fleeing. She makes the same mistake only once. Traps she never watched being armed remain effective — and she will study the mechanism after encountering it, never being caught by it again.

Mechanic 6 — The Silent Behavioral Tracking (SH2 Ending Criteria)

The game maintains two hidden accumulating scores (0–100), one per world. Every Scholar decision contributes to both scores. No UI element acknowledges the tracking system's existence. The scores determine which of the 10 ending scenarios triggers at the Child Phase. Tracked variables include:

Document depth rates, item donation frequency and tier, average health maintained (high health = hoarding instinct, low health = sacrifice pattern), compass check frequency (how often the Scholar monitors threat proximity), traps set vs. left disarmed, Traversal debt (Guardian spawn frequency), Dark Voice — listened vs. ignored, incense conservation vs. liberal use, whether the Scholar attempted to decode Vanguard B's motivations in World B lore before the betrayal, time spent in the Observatory after items began showing corruption.

These inputs produce a behavioral signature that maps cleanly to one of five spectrum levels per world without the player ever understanding the system they're being evaluated by. This is exactly Silent Hill 2's ending design — the player discovers they were being profiled only when the ending reveals what their behavior meant.

Three-Act Structure — Camel · Lion · Child
三幕結構 · 尼采的三種蛻變
駱駝
The Camel
Nietzsche: "Thou Shalt" / AitD:TNN Cooperation / TS1 Exploration

The burden-bearer. You serve, solve, give, translate. The Vanguard watches. You establish the Observatory, arm the Archive's mechanisms, build the infrastructure of a civilization you will never inhabit. Every correct decision here is a decision being added to her tactical model.

獅子
The Lion
Nietzsche: "I Will" / PoP SoT Trap Phase / SH2 Guilt Manifested

The rebel. The Vanguards turn on each other, using the Scholar as their weapon. You must use the traps you set, the environmental knowledge you accumulated, and every remaining incense stick with precision. Safe rooms are compromised. The radio is silent. The compass buzzes constantly.

孩童
The Child
Nietzsche: "Sacred Yes" / PoP SoT False Victory / SH2 Endings

The creator. The False Victory. One Vanguard falls. The achievement pops. The music resolves. Then the dimensional tear opens. What follows is determined entirely by the world you built over the preceding four to seven hours.

Act I — The Camel Phase (Cooperative Architecture)

The longest act (~40–50% of total playtime). The Scholar and both Vanguards navigate the twin Archives in a state of functional cooperation. The puzzle grammar scales from single-item solutions to 5-step cross-dimensional chains (TS2). The Scholar opens the Observatory in the first hour and establishes it as a safe, warm space — save altars nearby, items organized, the resonance instrument on the central table for communication with both Vanguards.

The Vanguards are present throughout the Camel Phase as observable AI entities: Vanguard A works visibly through World A's mechanisms, and the Scholar can watch her through traversal focal points. Vanguard B is rarely visible directly but her presence is felt — items in World B are organized after she's passed through, doors she opened remain open, her footprints are in the silt.

The Dark Voice emerges after the third major puzzle sequence. Its first words are always Fan Li's aphorism, spoken in the Scholar's own register. The player who knows Eastern Zhou history will recognize the warning instantly. The player who doesn't will interpret it as an intrusive thought and ignore it. Both responses are valid. Both have consequences.

Act II — The Lion Phase (Environmental Chess)

The turn is not a cutscene. It is a behavioral shift. The Scholar attempts to use the resonance instrument to coordinate with Vanguard A. There is a long silence. When the response comes, it is not a request for assistance. It is a position report. Vanguard A communicates where Vanguard B was last located. The Scholar understands immediately.

Vanguard A demands the Scholar traverse to World B and compromise Vanguard B's position — flood a corridor, collapse a mechanism, corrupt a save altar. Vanguard B makes the reciprocal demand through a separate instrument message. If the Scholar delays responding to either demand, that Vanguard begins moving toward the Scholar's last known location.

The trap vocabulary (PoP SoT) now activates. The Scholar's only available tools: environmental traps they armed during the Camel Phase, their jade lamp (deterrent against shadow-class entities), the Observatory (compromised but still providing minimal safe space), and their dimensional traversal — with its attendant Guardian cost (PoP WW Dahaka debt).

"The player's Camel Phase behavior determines the quality of their Lion Phase toolkit. A Scholar who set every optional trap, maintained their incense supply, and kept the Observatory clean has a rich defensive infrastructure. A Scholar who gave everything away, never armed a trap, and spent all their incense finds the Lion Phase is simply a countdown to a death they cannot slow."
— Design note: both outcomes are valid. The game does not punish the generous Scholar with a bad experience — it rewards them with a different quality of tragedy.

Act III — The Child Phase (False Victory Architecture)

After the Scholar orchestrates one Vanguard's elimination through environmental manipulation, the combat music resolves to a quiet, sustained note. The "Area Cleared" notification appears in the format of the genre's most familiar success prompt — the same font, the same sound, the same contextual position in the screen. A save prompt offers itself. The scholar's breathing normalizes.

The air tears. Not dramatically — a quiet seam in the air, like fabric parting. The surviving Vanguard steps through, her coat dry, her blade sheathed. She does not look at the Scholar. She walks to her counterpart's body, kneels, and performs a single-gesture ritual drawn from Chu ceremonial tradition (World B survivor) or adjusts the dead Vanguard's equipment with mechanical precision (World A survivor). She absorbs the Traversal Skill. The Scholar's compass goes completely silent — for the first time in the entire game. There are no threats to warn against. There is only her.

The UI disappears. The Traversal Skill is stripped from the Scholar's inventory. She straightens, turns, and looks at the Scholar for the first time in the entire sequence. What follows is determined by the behavioral score accumulated over the entire run.

Chapter 08
The False Victory & 10-Level Ending Spectrum
虛假勝利 · 十種終結的光譜

The False Victory Architecture

The False Victory is the game's final act of subversion — weaponizing every piece of accumulated player-literacy against the player simultaneously. At the moment of apparent victory, the game mimics the exact UI, audio, and pacing of a classic survival horror boss clear: the combat music resolves to a major key cadence, the achievement notification appears with the correct sound, the save prompt manifests. The player exhales.

The dimensional tear opens quietly. Not a dramatic portal — a fold in the air, like a page turning. The surviving Vanguard steps through. She does not look at the Scholar. She is already past them in every sense that matters. She kneels by her counterpart, absorbs the Traversal Skill in a single gesture, and stands. The UI goes dark. The Scholar's compass is silent — permanently. There are no more threats for it to warn against. There is only one entity in this Archive now, and she is not a threat. She is the new law.

10-Level Execution Spectrum — Human Ascension (No Physical Transformation)

Per Design Rule V: The Vanguard never transforms. Her final form is human. Her evolution is entirely psychological — the expression of a superior mind that has consumed the full logic of the Archive and no longer needs to share it.

Chapter 10
Aesthetic, Audio & UI Direction
美學方向 · 音頻設計 · 介面哲學

Visual Identity

The game's visual identity is the structural collision of two architectural traditions: Eastern Zhou bronze-hall construction (jade inlays, astronomical instruments, bamboo-and-stone) and European gothic horror (iron mechanisms, vaulted stone, the pre-rendered compositional authority of Alone in the Dark 1). Neither tradition dominates. Every room of World A expresses both simultaneously.

Camera work follows AitD 1's compositional doctrine absolutely: every angle is a deliberate painting. High angles dwarf the Scholar against the Archive's scale. Dutch angles in the Lion Phase destabilize the Scholar's world spatially. The Vanguard appears in the background of Scholar-focused shots throughout the Camel Phase — always watching, always still, slightly out of focus but never gone from the frame. The player builds dread from her background presence alone.

World B's visual identity applies SH3's Otherworld design logic to Chu Kingdom shamanic architecture: submerged ceremonial spaces, bioluminescent algae on oracle bone shrines, dark thorns growing through waterlogged bamboo. The fog from SH1 is adapted to water: World B's "fog" is a diffused opacity of silt and darkness that limits the Scholar's visible depth to 4–6 meters at the default lamp intensity.

Score Architecture

Built on two instruments: a cello (the Scholar) and a tuned bronze zhong bell (the Vanguard). The Camel Phase score is their quiet counterpoint — cautious harmonics that complement without fully resolving. In the Lion Phase, the bell's theme begins incorporating dissonance, irregular time, and the interference patterns of two incompatible tonal systems. The cello is stripped from the mix gradually — its absence is not announced, it is simply noticed one room too late.

The Child Phase score is determined by ending level. Poor endings: only a broken bell figure, decaying and distorted. Colorful endings: the bell has absorbed the cello's melodic line — a single instrument now playing both themes as one. The Scholar's voice is still present in the Vanguard's theme. It was always going to be. She learned music from the Scholar too.

UI Philosophy — The Invisible Game

Zero heads-up display during gameplay. Health: Scholar animation state. Threat proximity: compass static timbre. Vanguard's status: the brass resonance instrument's readout panel (three phases: human vitals → encrypted data streams → architectural code). Inventory: opened by a physical satchel interaction. Save system: diegetic incense/candle placement. No map (SH2's map system is retained as a manual, discovered-through-exploration diagram that the Scholar draws themselves in a notebook).

The behavioral tracking system (Rule III) is completely invisible. Its score does not appear anywhere in the game's UI. It is not referenced in any dialogue, document, or environmental element. The player discovers they were profiled at the ending — and only then, through the Vanguard's specific execution method, do they understand what their behavior meant.

Chapter 11
Pros & Cons — Expert Design Analysis
優缺點分析 · 專家設計評估

Design Strengths

Structural Strengths
◆
De-Risked Format

Tormented Souls 1 & 2 (2021/2023) have proven the fixed-camera survival horror format is commercially viable in the modern market, eliminating the primary investor objection to the genre. Kenoma is the next evolution of what TS validated, with a dramatically more ambitious narrative and world design.

◆
Original Market Position

No current game combines fixed-camera horror, dual-world synchronization, an AI antagonist whose evolution is systemic, an unwinnable ending, and behavioral-score-based outcome selection. The pitch comps are clear (AitD:TNN + SH2 + PoP SoT) but the synthesis is genuinely new.

◆
Cross-Cultural Resonance

Fan Li's aphorism is foundational in East Asian literary tradition; Nietzsche's Zarathustra is cornerstone Western philosophy. Using both expands critical reception and marketing reach across Eastern and Western markets simultaneously without pandering to either.

◆
Modular Ending Budget Efficiency

The 10-ending spectrum is not 10 independently constructed environments. Each ending level is primarily shader variation, audio composition, and behavioral AI parameter changes on a single base arena. The production cost of 10 endings is approximately 1.5–2× the cost of a single ending. High perceived value, manageable production scope.

◆
The AitD:TNN Pitch Comp

AitD: The New Nightmare's dual-character structure provides the clearest existing game as pitch comparison. "It is AitD: The New Nightmare if you could only play Aline — and Carnby is a genius who is studying you" is immediately understandable to any survival horror veteran or publisher with genre literacy.

Narrative & Mechanical Strengths
◆
The False Victory — Accumulated Trust

The False Victory only works because the entire game spends its runtime building the player's trust in survival horror genre conventions. Every familiar cue is present and respected until the critical moment — then weaponized simultaneously. This requires no additional content. It is architectural, not additive.

◆
SH2 Behavior Tracking Reapplied

Silent Hill 2's ending system is widely considered one of the most profound behavioral feedback loops in horror game history. Kenoma applies the same principle at a larger scale: not 4–6 endings, but 10, and determined by richer behavioral data. Players will replay specifically to understand what their behavior meant.

◆
Vanguard as Non-Monster

Designing the Vanguard's ascension as psychological rather than physical is the game's most distinctive horror choice. The monster that stares at you from across the room with complete composure, speaks your fate in a calm voice, and executes you with one unhurried movement is permanently more disturbing than any transformation sequence.

◆
PoP Trap Retroactivity

The Camel Phase trap-setting mechanic (PoP SoT) creates a rare design achievement: meaningful first-playthrough decisions that most players don't understand are decisions until their second run. The Lion Phase reveals what every optional Camel Phase mechanic was for. This drives replay without requiring new content.

◆
SH4 Observatory Lifecycle

The Observatory's corruption lifecycle transforms the safe space from a passive resource (classic genre convention) into an active mechanical system requiring management. The horror of watching your only safe space decay is among the most psychologically effective design moves available in the genre.

Design Risks & Mitigations

Structural Risks
✖
Tank Controls — Modern Friction

Tank controls are authentic to the format but alienating to players who entered gaming after the PS2 era. Mitigation: Offer a modern directional control option as a toggle in the options menu. The camera system's integrity is unaffected by control scheme — the player always looks where the camera looks, not where they point the stick.

✖
Dual-World Sync Complexity

Synchronizing two worlds' states across all puzzle solutions, trap activations, corruption states, and Guardian debt requires extremely disciplined technical architecture. Mitigation: The WorldSyncManager (Observer/Event Bus) must be designed first, before any content is built. All world-state changes are events, never direct calls. See Chapter 12.

✖
Zero-Combat Scholar — Frustration Risk

Some action-horror players will find the Scholar's complete inability to fight deeply frustrating. Mitigation: AitD 1 established the no-combat-encounter precedent. TS1 reinforced it. The marketing must communicate this clearly — "You do not fight. You think" is a compelling differentiator, not a weakness, if framed correctly from day one.

✖
False Victory — Disappointment vs. Horror

The False Victory only works if the Camel Phase has built genuine attachment between player and Vanguard. If players spend the Camel Phase treating the Vanguard as a utility object, the False Victory is frustrating rather than devastating. Mitigation: The Camel Phase must engineer 3–4 moments of genuine emotional resonance between Scholar and Vanguard — moments where the player forgets they know the ending.

Production & Narrative Risks
✖
Cultural Synthesis — Tokenism Risk

Eastern Zhou + Nietzsche + gothic horror is a volatile cultural mixture. Without a lead writer with genuine fluency in Eastern Zhou history (not just aesthetic familiarity), the Chinese elements risk becoming decorative rather than structural. Mitigation: The Eastern Zhou elements are load-bearing — Fan Li's aphorism IS the thesis, the 士 archetype IS the character foundation. If the writer can't engage with the primary texts, they can't write this game.

✖
Silent Hill Comparisons

A fixed-camera psychological horror game with an Otherworld-style secondary dimension will attract Silent Hill comparisons in marketing regardless of differentiation. Mitigation: Lean into the comparison initially as a credibility signal, then differentiate: "Like Silent Hill, but your guilt isn't about the past — it's about what you're building right now." The Eastern Zhou visual identity makes the distinction visceral and immediate.

✖
Behavioral Tracking — Playthrough Length

The 10-level ending system requires a full playthrough (~6–8 hours) to reveal its meaning. Players who drop the game before the False Victory never discover the system. Mitigation: The game must earn every hour of its runtime in the Camel Phase through puzzle quality, atmosphere, and Vanguard characterization. The Camel Phase's quality is the game's commercial survival mechanism.

✖
Dark Voice Decision Branch

The Dark Voice (PoP TT system) adds a complex early decision branch that some players may engage with accidentally (following the aphorisms without understanding they are triggering mechanical effects) or ignore entirely (missing a layer of the game's thematic depth). Mitigation: The Dark Voice's mechanical effects should be subtle enough not to confuse, but significant enough to reward on second playthrough. It should feel like an Easter egg the first time and a design revelation the second.

Engine & Core Architecture

Engine: Unity (URP). The pre-rendered background system, fixed-camera zone management, and component-based AI architecture are all well-supported. The dual-world synchronization system and Vanguard behavioral model are the two most technically complex systems; both require clean, modular design before content production begins.

Critical Systems

SystemDesign PatternSource RefCritical Requirement
WorldSyncManager Observer / Event Bus SH4 All state changes in World A broadcast events that World B subscribes to. A broken mechanism in World A must register in World B's mirrored space within one frame. No direct cross-world calls — only events. This is the architectural load-bearing system of the entire game.
VanguardBehaviorModel Behavior Tree + Runtime Profile AitD:TNN SH2 Continuously ingests Scholar behavior data: movement routes, item usage, document reading depth, save altar positions. In the Lion Phase, this profile feeds the Vanguard's pathing, ambush positioning, and trap-circumvention logic. The Vanguard's intelligence must feel earned, not scripted.
EndingSpectrumCalculator Weighted Score Accumulator SH2 Endings Maintains two hidden 0–100 scores (one per world). Each Scholar decision contributes weighted values. At Child Phase trigger, scores determine ending level per world. No UI output at any point. The system is completely invisible until the ending makes it visible retroactively.
TraversalDebtManager Counter + Event Dispatcher PoP WW Dahaka Increments a hidden debt value on every Traversal crossing. The GuardianSpawnSystem subscribes to debt thresholds and adjusts spawn frequency and movement speed accordingly. The Scholar never sees this counter. They only feel its effect.
ObservatoryCorruptionSystem State Machine per Item SH4 Room Each item in the Observatory that has been touched by the Vanguard accumulates a corruption value over time. Items above threshold: carried = health drain. The Scholar can perform a cleansing ritual (costs incense) to reset. The Observatory itself tracks an aggregate corruption level that affects spawn rates within it above a threshold.
TrapRegistryManager Dictionary + Observer PoP SoT Logs every trap the Scholar places (type, position, arming state) and a boolean: was the Vanguard present when this trap was armed? Traps with observed=true are flagged in VanguardBehaviorModel as known — she will circumvent, not trigger them. Critical to the integrity of the Lion Phase's strategic balance.
FalseVictorySequencer Timeline / Cutscene Controller SH2 SoT Triggers the False Victory UI suite (achievement notification with correct audio signature, save prompt, combat music resolution cadence) at a precisely calibrated interval after the boss-kill event. The timing must maximize the player's relaxation before the dimensional tear event. Requires playtesting across player types to calibrate the exact window.
DarkVoiceController State Machine + Audio Sequencer PoP TT Eastern Zhou Tracks the Scholar's engagement with the Dark Voice across the Camel Phase. Three states: Suppressed, Acknowledged, Followed. Each state modifies available Scholar actions in the mid-to-late Camel Phase. Followed state unlocks early sabotage mechanics. No state is labeled or indicated in UI.

Pre-Rendered Camera Architecture

Following AitD 1's approach and Resident Evil's camera system: backgrounds are pre-rendered at high visual quality and composited with real-time character models. Camera transitions are zone-triggered, not player-controlled. Each camera angle is authored as a deliberate composition. This architectural choice reduces real-time rendering complexity while enabling art-quality environments that real-time rendering cannot match at equivalent budget for an AA-scale production.

Production advantage: Pre-rendered backgrounds also provide a technical benefit for the VanguardBehaviorModel. Camera zone transitions are predictable events, allowing the Vanguard AI to calculate optimal positioning relative to the Scholar's anticipated arrival point. She appears prescient without requiring omniscient pathfinding — because the fixed-camera system makes her prescience architecturally possible.

Production Scope Classification

Scope: AA with ambition. The fixed-camera pre-rendered format, single-player linear-with-branching structure, and modular ending system make Kenoma achievable below AAA budget. Key scope discipline required: dual-world synchronization must be engineered as a modular event system from day one, before any content is built — attempting to retrofit WorldSync into an existing codebase would be project-threatening.

The 10 endings are modular variations on a single base arena, not 10 independent environments. The Vanguard behavioral AI has clearly defined parameters rather than machine-learning systems, making it producible and testable. The pre-rendered camera system reduces per-environment GPU complexity dramatically.

Recommended team: 20–35 people. 24–30 months production. PC + console. The game Tormented Souls was made with approximately 8 people — Kenoma at 3–4× that team size produces a dramatically more ambitious project while remaining fundable as a passion-driven AA title.
