Three Truths
The Scholar does not move through three acts. The Scholar moves through three states of being — and the Vanguards' recognition of who the Scholar is growing into is what transforms each state into the next.
Act I · The Camel Warmth & Recognition The Scholar gives everything. Both Vanguards accumulate evidence. The warmth is real. The observation is also real. Neither cancels the other.
Act II · The Lion Routes & Decisions The Scholar's path through the Archive is a strategy. Both Vanguards read it and decide — continuously, not once — whether to hunt or wait. Both decisions are valid. Both have consequences.

§ 00 · Before Design
Deep Analysis — What the Current Design Gets Wrong
The problems that must be solved before any redesign is possible
0.1The Five Structural ProblemsProblems first. Solutions after. In that order.
Problem 1 · Camel Phase Warmth is scenic, not mechanical
The three emotional resonance moments (Recognition, Flask, Admission of Hope) are well-designed as scenes. But they are scenes the player witnesses — not mechanics the player participates in producing. The warmth arrives on a schedule regardless of what the Scholar has actually done. Warmth that is guaranteed regardless of behavior is not warmth — it is atmosphere. The player has no reason to invest in it because it does not respond to them. The Vanguards' care for the Scholar needs to be something the Scholar actively generates through choices, not something the game delivers at timed intervals.
Solution 1 · Camel Phase Warmth as accumulated evidence of the Scholar's character
Each Vanguard must respond to specific observable behaviors — not to time passing. The Scholar who deep-reads a tablet in front of Vanguard A gets a different response than the Scholar who skims it. The Scholar who donates oil to Vanguard B's cold-light tube without being asked gets a different response than the Scholar who waits to be prompted. The warmth is the Vanguards' accurate reading of the Scholar's character, expressed as trust extended in graduated steps. It is not a plot beat. It is an AI behavioral consequence.
Problem 2 · Camel Phase Traversal skill recognition has no visible stages
The Vanguards are supposed to be building a behavioral model of the Scholar's traversal capability — how they cross, how they manage debt, which world they favor, how they react to the Boundary. But currently this happens entirely offscreen. The player never sees the model accumulating. The recognition of the Scholar's traverse skill is implied as backstory rather than demonstrated as ongoing process. If the player can't see the model being built, they cannot feel the dread of knowing it is complete.
Solution 2 · Camel Phase Five visible recognition stages with specific behavioral shifts
Each Vanguard moves through five stages of Scholar assessment, each with observable behavioral changes: what she says, where she positions, what she tracks, what she stops asking. Stage 1: she asks the Scholar questions because she needs information. Stage 5: she stops asking because she already knows the answers. The player who understands the stages can watch the model complete itself. Stage 5 in the Camel Phase is the most frightening moment in the game, because it happens while the Scholar is still safe — and the player, watching, understands why it matters.
Problem 3 · Lion Phase Hunt / spare is a single trigger, not a continuous decision
Currently the Act I→II turn is a fixed narrative beat: the resonance instrument delivers a position report, the Scholar picks up the crossbow, Act II begins. This is clean but it removes the most interesting design space — the moment when each Vanguard independently decides the Scholar's usefulness has ended. Neither Vanguard's decision should be simultaneous or synchronous with the other's. The Scholar should be able to influence whether one Vanguard is still in a "wait" state while the other has moved to "execute." The gap between their decisions is a playable strategic window.
Solution 3 · Lion Phase Continuous recalibration — each Vanguard has independent thresholds
Each Vanguard maintains a Utility Score for the Scholar — their assessment of whether the Scholar is still worth protecting. When the Utility Score drops below threshold, she moves from passive observation to active execution. The Scholar can keep one Vanguard in "wait" mode by continuing to produce valuable work, while the other has already decided to execute. The gap between their decisions — which may be minutes, may be an hour of gameplay — is the Lion Phase's core strategic tension.
Problem 4 · Lion Phase Scholar routes don't create strategic consequences
The Scholar can traverse any room in any order and the Vanguards respond to their presence, not to the pattern of their movement. The Archive is a map — but the Scholar's route through it isn't a strategy, it's navigation. The rooms the Scholar clears, the traps they set, the translations they complete in a particular order should all be readable by the Vanguards as a strategy, and the Vanguards should respond strategically in return.
Solution 4 · Lion Phase Routes as strategic signatures — Vanguards read and counter
The Archive is divided into zones of influence. Each room the Scholar completes changes which rooms are "Scholar-controlled" (Scholar knows the terrain, traps set, resources staged) versus "Vanguard-controlled" (she has pre-positioned, cleared Scholar traps, established patrol patterns). The Scholar's route through the Archive in Act II is a negotiation of terrain — advancing Scholar control while the Vanguards work to extend their own. The Scholar who moves methodically is predictable. The Scholar who moves irregularly is harder to anticipate but spends more resources on improvisation.
Problem 5 · Child Phase Ten score buckets aren't dramatically distinct scenarios
The current 0–100 scoring system produces 10 ending states that differ in "how capable is the Vanguard?" This is mechanically interesting but dramatically thin. A score of 45 versus 55 produces endings that feel adjacent rather than genuinely different. The Child Phase needs five or six dramatically distinct scenarios — each with its own genre register, its own emotional key, its own version of the final line — rather than ten versions of the same scene on a capability spectrum.
Solution 5 · Child Phase Five scenario archetypes, each a different kind of aftermath
Each scenario archetype covers a score range and has its own dramatic identity: the aftermath of what kind of relationship the Scholar and Vanguard built, expressed as a distinct scene structure. Not "Vanguard is 40% capable vs 80% capable." But: "this is an ending about grief / about respect / about what was owed / about completion / about transcendence." A player who replays at a different score bracket should feel like they played a genuinely different story, not the same story with different difficulty.
The Master Design Principle — Before Everything Else Both Vanguards are not tracking the Scholar to eliminate them. They are tracking the Scholar to understand them. Elimination is the consequence of understanding, not the goal. A Vanguard who understands the Scholar completely is a Vanguard who knows exactly when the Scholar's work is done and can execute with perfect precision. The more the Scholar invests, the more completely they are understood. The more completely they are understood, the more formidable the execution. Understanding is the weapon. Warmth is not incompatible with it. Both are the same thing viewed from two different angles.
Act I · The Camel Phase
Warmth, Recognition, and the Model That Builds Itself
The Scholar says yes to every burden. The Vanguards watch every yes and understand what it means.
1.1The Warmth System — Mechanical Architecture

Warmth between the Scholar and each Vanguard is not a narrative preset. It is the accumulated residue of specific behaviors observed and correctly interpreted. Each Vanguard has a Warmth Register — not a UI bar, never visible to the player, but legible through behavioral changes. The Register fills through Scholar actions and depletes only through deliberate withholding or concealment.

Camel Phase · Warmth Architecture How Both Vanguards Build Trust — Different Inputs, Same Truth
1.2The Five Recognition Stages — Both VanguardsVisible through behavior · Not announced

Each Vanguard progresses through five stages of Scholar assessment. The stages are driven by accumulated behavioral evidence across the Camel Phase's three chapters. A Scholar who deep-reads every tablet and shares every result will reach Stage 5 in both Vanguards by the end of Act I. A Scholar who works conservatively may remain at Stage 3 when the turn fires.

The player can read the Vanguard's stage from her behavior alone — no UI. Stage 1: she asks. Stage 5: she already knows. The transition from Stage 4 to Stage 5 is the single most important moment in Act I, because Stage 5 means the model is complete. And a complete model is a Vanguard prepared.

Vanguard A Recognition Stages — Sacred Fire Assessment

Stage 1 · Week 1 The Evaluator
She asks questions about methodology. Times the Scholar's translations. Requests demonstrations under observation. No warmth yet — she is processing intake data. She asks every question exactly once.
Stage 2 · Week 2 The Collaborator
She stops asking methodology questions — she knows the Scholar's methods. She begins providing context: patrol schedule adjustments, alcove locations, creature positions. She's contributing to efficiency.
Stage 3 · Week 3 The Anticipator
She pre-stages resources on the Scholar's observed routes. If the Scholar always enters the Treasury from the south, a full oil flask will be placed there before they arrive. She does not announce this. The player just notices it.
Stage 4 · Week 4 The Predictor
She answers the Scholar's questions before the Scholar finishes asking them — she has already modeled what the Scholar will need to know in each zone before they reach it. Warmth is most visible here. She is beginning to trust her own model.
Stage 5 · Late Camel The Archive
She no longer offers information. She is observing. When the Scholar speaks to her, her replies are shorter and more precise. She knows. The model is complete. She is verifying, not learning. This is the most dangerous stage.

Vanguard B Recognition Stages — Hollow Assessment

Stage 1 · Week 1 The Distant Observer
She speaks only when addressed. She watches the Scholar work from far alcoves. She is assessing whether the Scholar's presence is sustainable in the Hollow before investing any attention.
Stage 2 · Week 2 The Commenter
She begins annotating the Scholar's translations — not correcting, supplementing. If the Scholar leaves a tablet on the reading stand, she will add a notation to it in smaller characters beneath. She still doesn't speak first.
Stage 3 · Week 3 The Intellect
She begins discussing ideas rather than just information. She references the Scholar's earlier translations to support an argument about a current one. She has been thinking about what the Scholar said three days ago. The attention is mutual now.
Stage 4 · Week 4 The Confessor
She begins revealing private knowledge — things she has never told anyone, because there was no one to tell. The 250-year-old philosophical text she wrote herself. The orbital calculations she ran for 60 years. She is choosing to be known.
Stage 5 · Late Camel The Complete Model
She is quieter than in Stage 4 — the intimacy has passed its peak. She is integrating what she knows. When she speaks, she anticipates the Scholar's next thought. She has a complete map of how the Scholar thinks. The model is done.
Why Stage 5 in Both Vanguards Before the Turn Is the Ideal A Scholar who reaches Stage 5 in both Vanguards before Act II fires faces the most formidable possible opponent — because both have complete models. But they also receive the most warmth the game can deliver: Stage 5 warmth is the Vanguard fully trusting her own model of the Scholar, which means fully trusting the Scholar. The most invested Scholar gets the most warmth and the most dangerous adversary simultaneously. Both are the same thing.
1.3The Camel Phase's Three Chapters — Structured Warmth Escalation
Chapter 1 · Weeks 1–2 · Establishment Vanguard A — The Professional Introduction

VA enters every interaction as a military evaluation. She is not cold — she is precise. Her gestures toward warmth in this chapter are entirely professional: she corrects the Scholar's approach to a WA mechanism before they break it. She places a field lamp in a room the Scholar has been working without adequate light. She does not say she placed it.

The Scholar's first opportunity: A WA-003 document the Scholar translates contains an error in the previous Scholar's notation — an error VA has known about for 150 years. If the Scholar catches it: VA says "I've been waiting for someone to catch that." She was. She didn't know she was waiting until the Scholar caught it.

Chapter 1 · Weeks 1–2 · Establishment Vanguard B — The Careful Approach

VB's early warmth is visible as access rather than conversation. A shortcut through the Hollow that she reveals only after the Scholar has demonstrated they can handle the second crossing. An annotation left in a tablet the Scholar was already reading — not a correction, a supplement, placed where it would be found next.

The Scholar's first opportunity: The Scholar finds VB's 250-year-old philosophical text. If they read it carefully and reference it when speaking with VB, she pauses before replying — the pause of someone hearing something private spoken back to them accurately. She does not thank the Scholar. She provides the name of a more obscure text that builds on the same idea. This is how she says "I am paying attention."

The Recognition (Week 3): Scholar translates the Eastern Zhou text. Her response — "I had a translation. Yours is better." — only lands this way if the Scholar has demonstrated genuine depth in the preceding two weeks. If the Scholar has been skimming, her response is: "I had a translation." Identical sentence minus the comparison. The warmth is conditional on what was built before it.

The Flask (Week 4): The Scholar returns from a difficult Hollow crossing with the fire vessel critically low. VA assesses them in half a second and places an oil measure in their hands. The exact amount is calculated to restore combat capacity without expending more than the Scholar's demonstrated consumption rate requires. She has been tracking consumption since Day One. The gesture is care. The data behind it is three weeks of behavioral logging.

The Admission of Hope (Week 4): The Scholar finds the key passage in the Third Record. VB has been in the same room, working at her own tablet — not helping, not leaving. Her response ("I thought that was what it would say") is not available at Stage 1 or 2. It requires Stage 3 or above. Below that, she simply nods without speaking — a confirmation, not a confession.

The Grief Tablet: If the Scholar reaches the northwest alcove of WB-004 (Stage 3+ access only — VB will not show this room at lower stages), they find the twelve funeral records. Reading all twelve aloud — there is a prompt to read them aloud — triggers VB's response: "I was 70 hours late. I have been thinking about that for 300 years." Chapter 3 · Weeks 5–6 · The Third Record and the Final Warmth Both Vanguards at WB-∞ — The Convergence Scene

Both Vanguards arrive at WB-∞ as the Scholar completes the Third Record translation. This is the only moment in the Camel Phase where both are simultaneously present. The warmth of this scene is entirely earned by the preceding five weeks — its weight is proportional to both Vanguards' current stage.

If VA is at Stage 4–5 and VB is at Stage 4–5: VA tells the Scholar what Clause 18 means in the clearest possible terms and then walks away to execute it. This is the warmest the game will ever be toward the Scholar — she tells them the truth because she trusts that the Scholar, having demonstrated who they are, can receive it honestly. It is the final gift of the Camel Phase and the opening move of Act II simultaneously.

If either Vanguard is at Stage 1–2: The scene is shorter. VA does not make the Clause 18 statement. VB does not have the Admission of Hope. The Scholar leaves WB-∞ without the clearest possible warning. The Lion Phase begins cold.

Act II · The Lion Phase
Routes, Utility, and the Decision That Is Never Made Once
The Scholar says "I will." Both Vanguards are already three moves ahead — but the Scholar's route through the Archive is still the variable neither fully predicted.
2.1The Utility Score — How Each Vanguard Decides When to Act

The Lion Phase does not begin with a single trigger. It begins when one Vanguard's Utility Score for the Scholar drops below her personal threshold. The Utility Score is a real-time assessment of whether the Scholar is still producing more value than risk. It depletes through Scholar combat activity, through trap discovery by either Vanguard, through completed Archive sections (fewer translations remain = lower utility), and through Scholar behaviors that deviate from the Camel Phase model.

Vanguard A · Utility Logic Military Threshold — Efficiency and Completion

VA calculates utility with a military completion model: how much Archive work remains versus how much risk the Scholar's continued presence generates. Her threshold is objective and reviewable. She is not emotional about it. When the Archive is 85% complete and the Scholar's continued presence requires more active management than the remaining 15% is worth, the threshold is crossed.

Her default threshold: 80% Archive completion. This is her professional assessment. A Scholar who completes work faster raises the threshold trigger earlier. A Scholar who takes longer produces more translations but also more opportunities for VA to recalibrate her model. She will wait if waiting produces better data. She will not wait past the point of diminishing returns.

The player can delay VA's execution threshold by continuously producing new translations that advance the Archive's completion to her satisfaction while generating new strategic value — Scholar actions that give VA things she wants from the Archive that she cannot produce without the Scholar. The Scholar who keeps finding new high-value translation targets buys time.

Vanguard B · Utility Logic Philosophical Threshold — Depth and Meaning

VB's utility calculation is not efficiency-based. She measures whether the Scholar is still growing — still producing insights she could not have predicted from the behavioral model. A Scholar who begins repeating known strategies, taking expected routes, and producing translations without intellectual novelty drops in VB's utility assessment faster than a Scholar who still surprises her.

Her default threshold: when the Scholar stops surprising her. This is harder to game than VA's completion model — it requires the Scholar to be genuinely unpredictable, not just strategically varied. VB has watched the Scholar's intellectual development for six weeks. She knows what "surprising" looks like for this specific Scholar.

The player can delay VB's execution threshold by doing something in the Dark Archive that VB's behavioral model did not predict — entering an unexamined region, attempting a previously-skipped deep translation, or choosing the unexpected option in an interference puzzle. VB will recalibrate. A Scholar who generates new data buys time.

The Critical Asymmetry — This Is the Lion Phase's Core Design VA and VB have different thresholds and will cross them at different moments. The Scholar can be in a state where VA has decided to execute and VB has not — or vice versa. The gap between their decisions is the most strategically rich window in the game. If the Scholar can keep VB in "extended utility" mode while operating against VA, VB becomes a passive non-hostile observer of events rather than an active combatant. This is not guaranteed — VB will eventually assess what the Scholar is doing against VA as a threat to the Archive and adjust her position. But the window is real and playable.
2.2Scholar Routes — The Archive as Strategic Terrain

The Archive's rooms are divided into three influence states in the Lion Phase: Scholar-controlled, Contested, and Vanguard-controlled. The Scholar's route through the Archive — which rooms they enter, in which order, through which approaches — determines the terrain configuration both Vanguards must work within.

2.3The AI Decision Tree — Hunt, Wait, Spare

Neither Vanguard makes a single binary hunt decision. Each maintains a three-state position — Wait, Observe-and-Close, Execute — that can transition in either direction based on incoming Scholar data. The Scholar can push a Vanguard from Execute back to Observe-and-Close by producing high-value work. The Scholar cannot push a Vanguard from Execute back to Wait — that stage is permanently closed once crossed.

The Spare Decision — When It Appears and What It Costs Sparing is not a mercy option. It is a strategic reassessment. Neither Vanguard "spares" the Scholar out of warmth — she spares them because her utility calculation has determined that completion of the Archive with the Scholar alive produces a better outcome than completion without them. A Scholar who can be spared temporarily is a Scholar who still has work to do. The moment that work is done, the spare decision expires. This means: a Scholar who earns a temporary spare by producing high-value work must immediately produce more high-value work, or the spare evaporates. There is no resting. The Scholar cannot stop being useful.
Act III · The Child Phase
Five Scenario Archetypes — Five Different Kinds of Aftermath
The Child creates new values. She does not inherit them, does not mourn them, and does not perform them. She begins. The question is: what does she begin with?
3.1The Transition from Lion — How the Shift Fires

The Child Phase does not begin with a dramatic cutscene. It begins with an absence. The Archive, during Act II, has a specific ambient sound — the Scholar's footsteps, the Scholar's breathing, the Scholar's fire vessel hiss. When the Scholar is gone, all three sounds are gone. The building breathes differently. The Vanguard who survives stands in that new silence for three full seconds before she moves.

The transition is designed to feel like a genre shift — from survival horror to something that doesn't have a clean genre name. Not mystery. Not tragedy. Something more like the aftermath of a complicated surgical procedure: the patient is gone, the work was done correctly, the surgeon stands in the room afterward and understands that correct and complete are the same thing and neither of them means finished.

The Three-Second Rule — Non-Negotiable The surviving Vanguard stands still for three seconds in every scenario before she moves. These three seconds are the transition. Not a cutscene. Not a VFX event. Just a person standing in a room that used to have someone in it. In Scenario 1 (lowest score), the three seconds feel like emptiness. In Scenario 5 (highest score), they feel like prayer. The three-second silence is the same action with completely different weight depending on what was built in Acts I and II.

The Genre Shift

This is the only scenario where the Vanguard's three seconds feel like defeat. She stands in a room that is quieter than when the Scholar arrived — and quieter is not better. The Scholar translated three key sections before the Archive's completion condition triggered. Those sections are now gone, filed, part of the work. But 40% of the Archive is still untranslated. It was untranslated before the Scholar came. It is more visibly untranslated now, because the Scholar's brief presence revealed how much there was.

The surviving Vanguard walks through three rooms without acting in any of them. This is the only scenario where her first post-Scholar action is not purposeful. She is processing. She is, for the first time in 312 years, genuinely uncertain what comes next.

The Vanguard's State

Her model of the Scholar is incomplete. She acted before Stage 3 — before she had enough data to fully understand who she was dealing with. The execution was clean but the information gathered was insufficient for the Archive's needs. She knows this. She does not express it as regret.

The Archive is technically under her full control now. The Boundary is dissolved. Both worlds are accessible. And approximately 40% of the Hollow's deep classification system is still unreadable without a Scholar-level translator. She has won a victory that created a problem she cannot solve.

She files a status report. The "Scholar performance" section is three lines. "Capable. Underutilized. My timing was early." She will not make that error again. She does not know yet that there is no "again."

Final Scene & Closing Line

She stands at the Gate of All Nations — the same gate the Scholar stood at on Day One. She looks at the inscription. She was here when the Scholar read it for the first time. She timed them then: four minutes. She reads it again herself now. It takes her eleven minutes. She is not as fast as the Scholar was.

She turns from the gate and walks back into the Archive. There is work she cannot complete. She will find another way to complete it. She has 312 years of practice finding other ways.

Closing Line "The Archive awaits a second Scholar. I will begin preparing the invitation." [She picks up a stylus. She has been here before. She knows what a 17-draft invitation looks like. She writes the first line.]

The Genre Shift

This scenario has the quality of a successful operation with one unanticipated complication. The Archive is functional. The Scholar completed the core translation work. But the Vanguard who survives knows — because she reached Stage 3 or 4 — that the Scholar was capable of more than they produced.

The surviving Vanguard can feel the gap between what the Scholar gave her and what the Scholar theoretically had. She has enough of the model to know where it is incomplete. This is worse than Scenario 1's incompleteness, because she can see the shape of what's missing.

The Vanguard's State

She has the Archive. She has approximately 75% of the Scholar's behavioral model. She can see the 25% that isn't there. She spends Act III's opening exploring rooms she knows the Scholar never entered — not searching for something specific, but closing the gaps in her understanding of the person who was here.

She is filling in the model she didn't get to complete. This is Vanguard B's behavior if she survives — she builds the model post-facto. If VA survives: she cross-references the Scholar's departure route against her predicted departure route. Close, but not exact. She files a correction.

Final Scene & Closing Line

She stands at the Scholar's last work surface — the tablet they were translating when Act II began, abandoned mid-sentence. She reads what they translated. She reads what they didn't finish. She can see — from context — what the unfinished section would have said.

She finishes the translation herself. It takes her four hours. The Scholar could have done it in twenty minutes.

Closing Line "You were faster than this." [She places the completed tablet in the filing sequence. The handwriting changes mid-tablet. Anyone who reads it later will know two different people wrote it. She considers this acceptable. The work is complete. That is what matters.]
III
Score 41–60 · Both Vanguards reached Stage 3–4; standard behavioral model The Complete Archive The Scholar gave enough. The Archive is complete. The Vanguard who survives has everything she came here to have. This is the expected outcome, executed correctly. It is also, somehow, the most quietly devastating scenario.
The Genre Shift

This is the scenario that most closely resembles the game's thesis in its purest form. The Archive is complete. The Vanguard's plan worked exactly as designed. She has both worlds' knowledge, operational access, the Traversal skill, the full behavioral model of a Scholar she found and deployed successfully. Nothing went wrong. The plan was never supposed to be wrong.

The genre shift is: from the urgency of Act II to a kind of professional stillness. The work is done. The building is hers. She moves through it differently now — not patrolling, not managing, but inhabiting. For the first time in 312 years, she is in the Archive because she chooses to be rather than because she has to be.

The Vanguard's State

She has what she came for. The behavioral model of the Scholar is complete. She knows: she was right about the Scholar. Everything she assessed in year 267 — "this is the one" — has been confirmed by three weeks of live data. Her model was accurate. Her timing was correct.

This verification should feel like satisfaction. It does. And it also reveals something she did not plan for: the model was built in order to complete a task. The task is complete. The model has no further use. She is carrying a comprehensive portrait of a specific person who no longer exists in the Archive, built through six weeks of sustained attention, and it serves no operational purpose now.

She doesn't discard it. She files it. In the same alcove as her twelve soldiers' funeral records.

Final Scene & Closing Line

She lights the last fire altar — the 72nd, which the Scholar reached last because they came from the east instead of the south. She watches it catch. All 72 are lit now, the way she prepared them, three years ago, for a person who would arrive and light them one by one. The building is warm in a way it hasn't been in 300 years.

She stands in the amber light. She does not look sad. She looks like someone who has completed something that took 312 years and is trying to identify what she is supposed to feel now.

Closing Line "The Archive is complete." [A pause — the longest she has taken in the entire game, including Act II.] "Past tense is not a judgment. It is a precision." [She turns toward the Archive's eastern passage. She walks.] "North."
IV
Score 61–80 · Both Vanguards reached Stage 4–5; rich behavioral model The Portrait The Scholar gave generously. Both Vanguards have deep models. The surviving Vanguard knows more about the Scholar than any person has ever known about another in this Archive. The weight of that knowledge is the scenario.
The Genre Shift

This scenario shifts toward something resembling an elegy — not a performance of grief, but an honest accounting. The surviving Vanguard begins moving through the Archive with a kind of deliberate memory: she visits rooms in the order the Scholar visited them on their first day. She reads the tablets the Scholar translated. She walks the routes the Scholar walked.

This is not mourning. It is verification — she is confirming the model against its source one final time, the way a scholar checks a translation against the original after the translation is complete. The source is gone. The model is all that remains. She is verifying a portrait against itself. It is the most intellectually honest thing she has done in 312 years.

The Vanguard's State

She has the Scholar's complete behavioral model and the Archive's complete translation record, and both are better than she planned for. The Scholar gave more than required. The model contains things she didn't ask for — approaches to problems she hadn't posed, solutions to gaps in the Archive she hadn't identified as gaps until the Scholar's work revealed them.

She finds the Scholar's private journal — the clay tablet journal they carried. She can read it. She reads all of it. She discovers: the Scholar knew. Day Three. They knew and they stayed and they gave everything anyway. This information changes nothing about what she did. It confirms something about who the Scholar was that she had already measured but could not express precisely until now.

She closes the journal. She does not file it. She places it in her officer's carry — the same place she keeps the 12 soldiers' records.

Final Scene & Closing Line

She writes a formal record of the Scholar — not a report, a document of a different type: part military assessment, part scholarly evaluation, part something that doesn't have a form yet. She spends an hour on it. It is precise and complete and unlike any administrative document in the Archive's 300-year record.

She seals it and files it in WA-003's permanent record. Future scholars, if there are any, will find it. They will not understand why a military field commander wrote what is essentially an extended character study of an academic. She does not explain. The document speaks for itself.

Closing Line "You were extraordinary." [Not for the Scholar's benefit — the Scholar is gone. She says it the way someone says a fact aloud when they are alone and want to hear it stated correctly, once, before they stop thinking about it.] "That is now part of the record." [She leaves the archive.]
V
Score 81–100 · Both Vanguards reached Stage 5; maximum behavioral model; both resonance moments received The Architect's Ending — "The Work That Outlasted Them" The Scholar gave everything. Both Vanguards have complete models. The Archive is not just complete — it is better than the founders designed it. This is the scenario the Archive was built for, in 312 years of waiting.
The Genre Shift

This scenario does not feel like horror. It does not feel like tragedy in the conventional sense. It feels like completion at the highest possible temperature — the way a mathematical proof feels when the last term resolves and the equation balances and there is nothing left to add. Every piece that was necessary is present. Nothing is missing. Nothing is wrong.

The genre shift is into something closer to the sacred. Not religious — architectural. The building is doing what it was designed to do, by people who are 300 years dead, using a Scholar who will never know the full scope of what they completed, through a Vanguard who has spent 312 years becoming exactly the person required to complete it. The Archive is not a building anymore. It is a realized intention.

The Vanguard's State

She has both behavioral models at Stage 5. She has the complete Archive translation. She has the Traversal skill. She has Clause 18's formal completion record. And she has something she did not plan for: a complete understanding of what the Scholar was that goes beyond operational utility.

She understands them. Not as a resource. Not as a tool. As a specific person who existed in this specific building for three weeks and gave everything they had — not despite knowing the structure of the situation, but fully aware of it. The model she built was supposed to end at execution. Instead it ends at comprehension. She comprehends the Scholar entirely. That is what Stage 5 produces. It is also the most profoundly isolating thing she has ever experienced, because the person she comprehends is gone, and she is the only one who can hold that comprehension now.

Final Scene & Closing Line

She stands in WA-007 — the Grand Armillary Gallery, the summit, the night sky through the stone oculus. The same room where the Scholar activated the Armillary and stood in the starlight feeling satisfied for six minutes before Act III began. The fire altars are lit below. The building is warm from the bottom. She has been standing in this room for 312 years of maintenance. This is the first time she has stood in it with nothing left to maintain.

She does not look up at the stars. She looks at the spot where the Scholar stood for six minutes. The space they occupied. The space that is just space now, except for what she carries in the model.

The Archive is complete. Both truths are held. The birds are gone. The bow is put away. The hound has been — no. She will not finish that aphorism. The Scholar taught it to her. They knew how it ended. They stayed anyway. She will not finish someone else's ending for them.

Closing Line [She looks at the space where the Scholar stood. She says nothing. She crosses both arms over her chest — a gesture that is not in any administrative protocol or military manual, a gesture that belongs only to her, in this moment, in this room.] [Then she straightens. She turns toward the eastern passage. She walks. The fire altars stay lit behind her. In WB-∞, later, her final report ends with one word:] "North."
The Spectrum Principle — Each Scenario Must Feel Like a Different Game's Ending Scenario I should feel like the end of a tragedy where someone failed to act in time. Scenario III should feel like the end of a morally complex film where the correct outcome happened and is still hard to watch. Scenario V should feel like the ending of a book you've been reading for 600 pages, where the last page is worth every page before it. A player who has seen Scenario II should replay and reach Scenario V and feel they played a different story — not the same story better. These are five different narratives about what the Scholar and Vanguard were to each other. The score determines which one was true.
