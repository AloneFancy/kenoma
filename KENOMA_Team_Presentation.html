§ 01 · Foundation
The Two Worlds
One Archive building, two civilizations sealed inside it. Same architecture — opposite philosophy. Neither world is real without the other.
World A · Sacred Fire The Apadana Above

Persepolis at full imperial scale. 72 stone columns with carved double-bull capitals. Processional walls showing tribute from 30 nations. Fire altars at every corridor junction — cold for 300 years until the Scholar arrives.

TruthVisible, ordered, luminous. The Sacred Fire civilization believed truth must be seen and lit.
PuzzlesSacred Fire classification: most dangerous documents are most restricted. Scholar's expertise works naturally here.
CreaturesFire-Wardens (immune to fire vessel), fire vessel is primary tool + save mechanism.
Vanguard AMilitary precision. 300 years managing the Apadana as logistics. Warmth is real AND calculated.
World B · The Hollow The Dark Archive Below

The exact same column grid — inverted underground. Columns hang from ceilings. Processional friezes show the same 30-nation procession — except the journey has gone wrong somewhere mid-frieze. The Scholar's fire vessel is the only warm light in 300 years.

TruthHidden, shadowed, cold. The Hollow civilization believed truth must be concealed to be protected.
PuzzlesHollow classification: most dangerous documents appear most accessible. Scholar's World A expertise fails here on first attempt.
CreaturesLantern-Thieves (drawn to fire vessel), Shadow-Walkers (processional figures gone wrong).
Vanguard BExpansive, patient, philosophical. 300 years in absolute dark. Measures time in generations.
The Core Design Rule Neither world is "good" or "evil." Sacred Fire chose visible truth. The Hollow chose hidden truth. Both are valid paths to complete knowledge — the civilization split itself trying to prove one could exist without the other. The Archive is the crossing point where both paths still exist simultaneously. The Scholar is the only person alive who can move between them. Both Vanguards have been waiting 300 years for exactly this person.
§ 02 · Mechanics
The Crossing System
Traversal Focal Points (TFPs) — Achaemenid astronomical instruments built into the Archive walls. The Scholar crosses through them. Each crossing adds Traversal Debt. The Vanguard never crosses. This asymmetry is the game.

How Crossing Works

The Scholar approaches a TFP — a bronze armillary sphere (World A) or jade star-clock (World B) built into a wall pillar. They peer through the central ring and see the other world through it: inverted, cold or warm. They step through. 2.2 seconds. Cannot be interrupted.

Every crossing increments the hidden Traversal Debt counter. As debt rises, the Boundary — the Archive's crossing-law made physical — manifests more frequently to pursue the Scholar. The Vanguard has zero crossing debt. She never crosses. The Scholar pays every cost.

§ 05 · Puzzle System
The Interference Puzzle System
Four locations where both Vanguards attempt the same mechanism simultaneously — and cannot solve it because of the other's invisible presence. The Scholar must distract one away. Neither Vanguard can learn a second world exists. But the puzzle leaves a trace that implies one does.
Four Rules — Every Interference Puzzle Honors All of Them
01The puzzle cannot be solved with both Vanguards present — not by scripting, but because of a physical or logical property of the mechanism itself.
02The Scholar must invent a reason to move one Vanguard away. The reason cannot reveal the other world exists. It must be plausible within what each Vanguard currently knows.
03Neither Vanguard learns a second world exists. But the solved puzzle leaves a trace that implies one does. The Scholar sees this. The Vanguards do not register its significance.
04The Scholar's choice carries behavioral weight in both Vanguards' invisible score. Both are watching. Both are filing. The Lion Phase is built from what they observed here.
Shared Second-World Trace Logic Every solved interference puzzle reveals something about the Archive's design that implies a second civilization knew about the first. The founders built instruments for two calendars, appointed two Keepers, designed the Foundation Obelisk's final clause in bilingual notation, oriented one pinhole cluster toward an underground sky. The Scholar accumulates these traces. No single trace is conclusive. Together, before the Scholar ever finds a Traversal Focal Point, they know: there is something below.
§ 06 · Interference Puzzle A
The Shadow Well
A solar calibration well that requires a single clean shadow to read its inscription. Two light sources from two worlds have split the shadow into two contradictory calendar readings. Neither half points anywhere.
A
WA/B-CY-01 · Central Courtyard · Mid Camel Phase The Shadow Well Failure type: Light interference — physical. Failure condition: two shadows from two world-sides.
EXPAND ↓
Why this well exists

Achaemenid astronomers built shadow wells — deep vertical shafts oriented precisely north-south — to track the sun at specific calendar dates. At noon on the equinox, sunlight falls straight down to illuminate a Calibration Marker at the floor: the cuneiform sequence that resets the Grand Armillary's annual cycle. The well requires one thing: a single, undivided shadow.

Why It Cannot Be Solved
The Scholar lowers the fire vessel toward the well. Two shadows appear — the fire vessel casts one, and Vanguard B's cold-light tube (bleeding through from World B as a faint cold shimmer) casts a second. The marker yields two overlapping partial readings with contradictory calendar dates. Both Vanguards conclude the mechanism is broken. The Scholar knows it is not.
Vanguard A · World A Positioned at the north marker, fire-starter in hand. Has been attempting this well for 3 centuries. Believes the mechanism degraded. Has a fire-starter — insufficient intensity to make the reading alone. Has been waiting specifically for the Scholar's fire vessel.
Vanguard B · World B Positioned at the south marker from the World B side, cold-light tube active. Also attempting this well for centuries. Needs the inner ring reading (Dark Archive calendar). Does not know Vanguard A is positioned identically from the other side — and never has.
Second-world implication
What the Scholar reads when it finally works
The Calibration Marker has two concentric ring sequences. The outer ring is the Sacred Fire calendar — the Armillary reset date. The inner ring uses inverted notation, offset by exactly six months. The well's founders built it to calibrate two archives simultaneously: one read from above (Sacred Fire), one read from below (the Hollow). The single well serves two systems. It was always designed for both.

Vanguard A, when the Scholar reports the inner ring: "I assumed that was a manufacturing artifact — the craftsman's test marks." She does not say she agrees it is a test mark. The assumption is now open.

§ 07 · Interference Puzzle B
The Resonance Obelisk
A limestone obelisk that reveals hidden inscription via iron-filing resonance when struck at the correct frequency. Two people striking from opposite world-sides produce destructive interference — the filings scatter, the inscription disappears.
B
WA/B-HF-04 · Eastern Alcove · Mid Camel Phase The Resonance Obelisk Failure type: Destructive acoustic interference. Two vibration patterns meet inside the stone and cancel.
EXPAND ↓
Why this obelisk exists

Resonance stones communicated between administrative offices by transmitting vibrations through the building's foundation. When struck correctly, iron filings packed between inner stone layers are released into surface channels, temporarily revealing concealed text. This obelisk carries the Keeper Designation — the administrative authorization phrase required to operate the Grand Armillary without triggering the Boundary. The founders encoded it this way because the knowledge was too sensitive for documents but too critical to lose.

Why It Cannot Be Solved
The Scholar strikes the correct five-beat pattern on the north face with the astrolabe casing. The iron filings begin to move. Simultaneously, Vanguard B — attempting the same stone from World B's south face — strikes her own three-century sequence. The two vibration patterns meet inside the stone and cancel. Filings scatter randomly. Vanguard A touches the stone surface: "it's vibrating at two frequencies simultaneously, which is physically impossible from one source." She notes this to the Scholar.
Vanguard A · North Face Has been attempting this stone for 300 years. Wrong striking material (not astrolabe casing). Wrong position. Has waited specifically for a Scholar trained in Achaemenid administrative resonance systems to attempt it with her.
Vanguard B · South Face Also attempting for 300 years. Strikes from south face using Hollow resonance conventions (inverted sequence). Also has the wrong material. Neither Vanguard knows the other has been failing at the same stone from the opposite side.
Second-world implication
What the Keeper Designation actually says
"Appointed: First Keeper of the Sacred Fire Archive, title conferred by the Council of the Founding. Co-Appointed: First Keeper of the Dark Archive Below, title conferred by the same Council at the same moment."

Two Keepers. Appointed simultaneously. By the same authority. The Archive was built from its first day to have two custodians — one per world. The current Vanguard has been managing a half-system for 300 years. There was always supposed to be a counterpart below.

Vanguard A, if the Scholar shares the full reading: "I assumed the Council changed their minds." She turns and walks to the east corridor without explaining why. She is walking because she needs to be somewhere her expression is not visible. Three hundred years of that second line. She has already known it was there. She has been alone anyway.

§ 08 · Interference Puzzle C
The Still-Water Oracle
A well whose still water maps the night sky through pinhole openings above. It has never worked — because both Vanguards have been approaching it from opposite world-sides for 300 years, each unknowingly disturbing the water for the other.
C
WA/B-CT-03 · Inner Courtyard · Early-Mid Camel The Still-Water Oracle Failure type: Proximity vibration from two world-sides. Water never stills. 300-year parallel failure — the most story-dense interference puzzle.
EXPAND ↓
Why this well exists — and why neither has ever read it

A deep stone well capped with a perforated stone disc. At specific astronomical moments, starlight projects through the pinholes onto the water below — a miniature night-sky map on still water. The map shows which star positions correspond to which Archive chambers, providing navigation data for the Armillary's celestial reset.

The well requires absolute stillness — no footsteps within four meters of its lip. The water takes 40 seconds to still from any disturbance. The reading lasts 90 seconds.

The Hidden History — 300 Years of Parallel Failure
The well is at the Archive's crossing junction — where both worlds overlap most closely. Whenever Vanguard A approached from World A, her footsteps disturbed the water. Simultaneously, from World B, Vanguard B's footsteps created a second disturbance at slightly different timing. Both concluded the well's mechanism is broken. Neither knows the other has been approaching it at the same moments for three centuries.

Vanguard A: "The well hasn't worked in my time here. The mechanism must have degraded." Vanguard B: "I've tried it periodically for two centuries. The water never stills." Both say almost the same thing. Neither notices.

Second-world implication
The 8th constellation
When the water finally stills, the Scholar counts the star groupings: 8. The sky visible from the Apadana contains 7. The Scholar examines the stone disc: one pinhole cluster is oriented 180° inverted from the others. It reads the sky below the Archive — not above. The well was designed to simultaneously map World A's sky and, through the inverted cluster, World B's sky from underground. The single extra constellation is the Dark Archive's pole star, visible only from below.

Vanguard A, when Scholar mentions 8 groupings: "I count 8 in my reading too." Said as confirmation. She has catalogued this number for centuries without asking what the 8th was for. The assumption that it was normal has sat unopened for 300 years. The Scholar has just opened it.

§ 09 · Interference Puzzle D
The Translation Obelisk
No physical mechanism fails. The Scholar is the resource being contested. Two commanders, two notation conventions, one translator operating under simultaneous contradictory instruction. The most socially complex interference puzzle — and the one with the highest behavioral score impact.

Why this obelisk exists — and what both Vanguards need

The Great Hall's central obelisk is the Archive's Foundation Record — the inscription placed at the building's dedication, describing its purpose and governing philosophy. Every major bureaucratic action in the Archive requires citing the appropriate clause as authorization.

Vanguard A needs Clause 7 — authorization for re-activating dormant fire altars in sealed chambers.
Vanguard B needs Clause 12 — authorization for a Hollow-notation ritual in an upper-archive space.
Both require precise, complete translation in the correct administrative Elamite format.

Why It Cannot Be Solved
Both Vanguards issue simultaneous translation requirements in different notation conventions. Vanguard A requests Sacred Fire Elamite grammar. Vanguard B requires Hollow-inverted term substitutions. After 11 minutes of cross-correction, the Scholar's draft is a hybrid that is administratively correct for neither — a format that does not exist in Archive precedent and will not function as legal authorization in either world. The Scholar is the problem. The Scholar is also the only solution.
Second-world implication

While working through the Foundation Record, the Scholar reaches Clause 18 — written in notation that is neither Sacred Fire Elamite nor Hollow-inverted. It uses both simultaneously. Only someone who can read both can translate it. The Scholar's presence here, translating both Clauses in one session, constitutes — per Clause 18 — the Archive's formal completion. They have been the trigger condition all along.

Scholar's private journal, that evening: "Fan Li knew when he was the hare. I have read Clause 18. I know what I am. I am continuing anyway."
