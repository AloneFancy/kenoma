Design Baseline

PZ-CC-DEMO · Camel Phase II · Demo Scene
Resonance Obelisk
observe → share data → choose side
BASELINE ▾

A paired obelisk system spanning WA and WB — two Vanguards have been canceling each other's tuning attempts for 300 years without knowing it. The Scholar is the only person who can observe both states simultaneously. Three solution paths: serve VA, serve VB, or coordinate both at escalating traversal cost.

Path A — Serve Vanguard A 1–2 crossings
Share WB tone data with VA. WA Obelisk completes. WB goes cold. Sealed alcove opens (oil + Grand Armillary foreshadow).
VA +5 VB −3 SOLVED_SIDED_VA
Path B — Serve Vanguard B stays in WB
Share WA frieze translation with VB. WB Obelisk completes. WA side goes incomplete. VB's journal chamber opens.
VB +6 VA −2 SOLVED_SIDED_VB
Path C — Unified Resolution 3–4 crossings · requires deep-reads
Both Vanguards positioned simultaneously. Scholar speaks activation phrase (deep-read gated). VA and VB see each other through TFP glass for 3 seconds. Neither moves. Both chambers open.
VA +4 VB +5 SOLVED_UNIFIED DEBT CRITICAL

Structural limit: all three paths are variations of the same information-transfer mechanic. The five new puzzles each use a distinct solution-space geometry.

New Puzzle Designs

PZ-WA-009 · World A · Administrative Wing
The Jurist's Scales
the puzzle is already solved — the Scholar decides whether to disturb it
WORLD A ▾
AUTHOR Artaxara, Chief Archivist-Jurist — final arbiter of historical record disputes
PURPOSEPhysical adjudication: tablet weight = epistemic burden of competing claims. Heavier side declared authoritative.
STATE Scales balanced for 300 years. 7 tablets vs. 6 tablets — physically impossible without a hidden counterweight.
RULE WB exiled tablets (the "losing" record) give context for what the WA balance was ruling on.

Seven tablets in the left pan. Six in the right. The left pan sits lower — impossible given the count. Artaxara added a hidden bronze counterweight under one tablet to reach balance not because the evidence was equal, but because the ruling she needed to make couldn't be wrong. Her thumb on the scale, hidden in plain sight for 300 years.

The WB exiled tablets (discoverable on an optional crossing) contain the six accounts arguing the Archive's dual-world architecture was accidental. The seven WA tablets argue it was deliberate. Artaxara ruled: deliberate. The counterweight made it so.

Path A — Accept the ruling
Replace the tablet over the counterweight. Leave. The founding myth holds. The Scholar understands institutional necessity and chooses not to disturb it. The only path that costs nothing materially — and produces the smallest warmth consequence.
VA +3 VB −2 SOLVED_JURIST_ACCEPT
Path B — Extract the counterweight
Remove the bronze cylinder. The left pan drops for the first time in 300 years. A sealed north drawer clicks open: Artaxara's private journal — written after the ruling, explaining why she lied and what she feared. The only document that explicitly confirms the dual-world architecture was an accident. Gates Scenario V (ThirdRecordAccess event).
VA −1 VB +7 THIRD_RECORD_ARTAXARA
Path C — Read and restore
Take the journal. Read it fully. Return the counterweight. The physical record remains undisturbed. The Scholar carries a truth they have no proof of. Both Vanguards note the Scholar distinguished between knowing and acting on knowledge.
VA +2 VB +4 SOLVED_JURIST_RESTORE

PZ-WB-011 · World B · Conservation Corridor
The Conservator's Seal
the creature is the lock mechanism — not the obstacle
WORLD B ▾
AUTHOR Senior Conservator Marduka — responsible for integrity of restricted Archive sections
PURPOSESeal a section too dangerous to destroy, too volatile to leave accessible. Used a Fire-Warden's Naming designation as a living lock — bonded to the doorframe.
STATE Fire-Warden bonded to arch for 280 years. Non-hostile. Cold blue flame. Fulfilling its designation. Cannot leave.
RULE Creature's true founding-era designation (WA Chamber Assignment Register) enables re-Naming in WB — relocating it and opening the door.

The Fire-Warden stands in a recessed arch, its body threaded through carved stone channels. It burns cold blue — the color of a creature that has been doing the same thing for so long it has forgotten what warmth was. This should be heartbreaking before it is interesting.

Marduka's designation above the arch is incomplete: he used current (post-split) chamber numbering but omitted the founding-era origin assignment. A Fire-Warden Named without its origin is Named to a location, not to its nature. It can be re-Named with the complete designation.

Path A — Re-Name with complete designation 1 crossing required · deep-read gated
Scholar crosses to WA, deep-reads the Chamber Assignment Register (25 sec). Returns. Speaks complete Avestan designation: origin (WA-003-Subsidiary-East) + current. The creature's flame shifts cold blue → warm amber. It moves — slowly, as if remembering how. Walks through the TFP with zero traversal debt. The Boundary does not manifest. This asymmetry is visible. Door opens. Marduka's conservation record inside.
VA +4 VB +6 NAMING_RELOCATION
Path B — Alternate physical route no crossing · lamp angle required
Scholar observes the room's physical structure. Jade lamp held at a specific angle reveals a crawl passage through collapsed WB floor. No naming, no crossing. The creature remains at the door. Scholar enters chamber, reads the record, exits. Marduka's seal stays intact — the locked room is still locked. Scholar chose efficiency over understanding.
VA +1 VB +3 SOLVED_ALT_ROUTE
Path C — Incorrect naming attempt no crossing · information not available
Scholar attempts to name the creature with a plausible-but-incomplete designation. Creature's flame dims 15% further. No hostile response. No UI notification. Wrong name — not a failed action, an informational event. No BMS event fires. Scholar must find the correct approach or take the alternate route. Teaches: the Naming system requires genuine knowledge, not plausible guesses.
NO BMS EVENT CREATURE DIMS

PZ-CC-012 · Calibration Channel · Dual-World
The Cartographer's Fold
the scholar's body is the calibration instrument
DUAL WORLD ▾
AUTHOR Daxya, Archive Cartographer — the only person who attempted to map both worlds simultaneously. She went mad trying. The madness was a correct result.
PURPOSEBronze mirror array producing a composite optical overlay of WA and WB through the TFP ring — revealing the fold space between worlds. She needed to know what connected them.
STATE Seven mirrors extant, misaligned by 300 years of decay. Daxya's alignment notes are split: WA has mirror angles, WB has observer position. Neither half works alone.
RULE Correct observer position in WB (knowledge) determines mirror angles in WA (material arrangement). The Scholar is the lens that assembles both halves.

The Scholar cannot see the correct mirror positions from WA — only from a specific observer point in WB. They cannot adjust the WA mirrors from WB. Every crossing to verify costs Traversal Debt. The Scholar who read both notes thoroughly on first pass needs one round trip. The Scholar who skimmed needs four crossings and significant debt.

Path A — Three-crossing alignment scholar as instrument
WB (observe, memorize) → WA (adjust from memory) → WB (verify). Most debt-efficient. Requires the Scholar to hold spatial information across a world transition. The Scholar who read both notes once and understood them can do this in one round trip. The Scholar who skimmed enters a debt spiral.
VA +3 VB +5 CARTOGRAPHER_SELF
Path B — Delegate to Vanguard A requires sharing WB notes · highest VA warmth event in scene
Scholar explains WB observer position to VA (requires having deep-read VB's notes — a behavioral tell about the Scholar's relationship to VB's knowledge). VA adjusts WA mirrors while Scholar holds position in WB. Image assembles. The Scholar gave VA knowledge from WB. VA notes this specifically.
VA +6 VB +2 CARTOGRAPHER_DELEGATE

What the assembled image shows: Through the TFP ring from WB observer position — the architecture of the fold space. A room between WA and WB, accessible from neither. Chamber WB-∞. Daxya's final note: "I have found the room that should not exist. It can only be reached from both sides simultaneously." This is the only document correctly describing how to reach the Chamber of Convergence. ThirdRecordAccess event fires on image assembly.

DARK VOICE
behind"She mapped it correctly. That's what broke her."
beside"You need to stand somewhere. The angle is the information." — fires when Scholar holds astrolabe at TFP

II PASS — WB position (knowledge) enables WA mirror arrangement
III PASS — image appears through TFP ring, no UI

PZ-WA-014 · World A · Philosopher's Quarter
The Sympathy Engine
the inputs were set before the scholar entered the room
WORLD A ▾
AUTHOR Philosopher-Archivist Zura — studying the difference between genuine scholarly engagement and performance of scholarship
PURPOSEThree oil-burning braziers connected to underground channels tracking oil use across the WA wing. Reflects what kind of scholar is present as relative flame heights.
STATE Channels intact. Braziers cold for 300 years. Wake on proximity to a Scholar with sufficient oil activity — immediately reflecting their accumulated behavioral pattern.
RULE N/A — WA only. WB combat oil registers as distinct pattern from WA navigation oil.

Three braziers ignite simultaneously when the Scholar enters. Their flames adjust to the Scholar's historical pattern within five seconds. Not this room — the entire run. The Scholar sees a physical representation of what they have been doing, encoded as relative warmth. Not labeled. Not explained.

Brazier A — oil spent on navigation and light (presence, attentiveness). Brazier B — oil donated to the Vanguard (generosity as logistics). Brazier C — oil spent on combat (survival instinct). A sealed column in the center opens when all three reach equal height.

Path A — Equilibrium
Scholar redistributes remaining oil to reach balance. The resource cost varies by how far from equilibrium the Scholar's pattern was — and which direction they must correct. No two equilizations are the same oil expenditure. Column opens. Zura's journal inside: she ran this experiment on herself for 15 years. Her pattern: Brazier B always slightly higher. Her note: "I believe this is why the Archive still has scholars in it."
VA +3 (if correcting toward combat) VB +5 (if correcting toward generosity) SYMPATHY_EQUILIBRIUM
Path B — Accept the imbalance
Scholar reads Zura's surface notes. Looks at the flame pattern. Does not attempt to change it. Leaves. Column stays sealed. Scholar goes to the next room carrying Zura's observation — that who you have been is different information from who you should be — without resolving which they are.
VA +1 VB +3
Path C — Attempt to game the machine
Scholar floods all three channels simultaneously. Braziers surge, then gutter. Extinguished. Must be relit. Pattern resets. Oil spent for nothing. The machine averages the full run, not the room — it cannot be gamed by a moment's performance. No BMS event. No UI notification.
NO BMS EVENT OIL CONSUMED

beside"The flame on the left is the answer to a question you didn't know you were being asked." — fires on room entry

PZ-WB-006 · World B · Reading Alcove
The Last Classification
the scholar solves vb's problem by seeing what vb cannot see about her own method
WORLD B ▾
AUTHOR Senior Archivist Tahmina — dying, could not trust official channels with what she knew
PURPOSE12-tablet three-category filing system (Administrative / Ceremonial / Private). Correct filing opens hidden panel containing her message — hidden as the 13th tablet no one could categorize.
STATE VB has been attempting to file these tablets for decades. 11 are sorted. Two remain. She keeps pulling the first-person tablet back because its content doesn't match her expectation of private correspondence. She is prioritizing content over form — misreading her own rule.
RULE WA master classification key (Tahmina's own words: "Classification follows form, not content") resolves which category the uncategorizable tablet belongs to.

VB is present. She has 11 tablets sorted, two remain. When the Scholar enters: "The eleventh category would not fit either the administrative record structure or the ceremonial format. I have been using the wrong frame." She does not ask for help. She is thinking aloud about being wrong. This is the most private thing VB does in the scene.

The uncategorizable tablet uses first-person voice throughout. Nothing in the Archive's official typology uses first person — except private correspondence. But VB keeps rejecting it because the subject matter is institutional, not personal. Tahmina's own rule: form over content. VB has been misapplying it for years.

Path A — File correctly, say nothing
Pick up the first-person tablet. Place it in the private correspondence slot. File the remaining administrative tablet correctly. 12 filed. Hidden panel opens. VB watches. She sees which tablet went where. Three seconds of stillness. Then: "Form over content. Yes. I was reading the wrong axis." She opens her notebook and corrects her classification system. The Scholar taught VB something about her own method by enacting it rather than explaining it.
VA +2 VB +8 HIGHEST SINGLE VB EVENT IN SCENE
Path B — File correctly, explain
After filing correctly, speak the principle aloud: "Form over content. Her own rule." VB receives this as instruction rather than observing the Scholar enact it. She already suspected this conclusion; being told it directly gives her less to discover. The intimacy of watching is lost.
VA +2 VB +5
Path C — Ask VB
Hold the problematic tablet toward VB: "What makes this one difficult?" VB identifies the first-person voice aloud. Stops mid-sentence. She has been looking at this for weeks and just heard herself say the answer. She files the tablet herself. Panel opens. She reads Tahmina's message before the Scholar does. Scholar catalyzed — did not solve.
VA +1 VB +4
Tahmina's message — Scholar's murmur during deep-read:
"She writes to no one in particular. She writes 'to the next one who cannot stop.' She has been here. She knows what this is." CONTENT: Location of a misfiled document in WA-003 confirming the dual-world architecture was accidental. Combined with PZ-WA-009 Path B/C, creates complete evidentiary chain.
I N/A
II PASS — WA classification key optionally aids WB filing
III PASS — VB's stillness and notebook correction are behavioral, not UI
IV PASS — Tahmina, her illness, her trust problem, her method

Cross-Puzzle Narrative Chain

The Dual Record — Artaxara + Tahmina

Two women, decades apart, who each knew the same truth and could not trust anyone with the whole of it. A Scholar who solves both puzzles in the critical path holds two independent confirmations of the same secret.

PZ-WA-009 · Path B Artaxara's private journal
+
PZ-WB-006 · Deep-read Tahmina's message

DUAL_RECORD_COMPLETE event Complete evidentiary chain
Neither document proves the architecture was accidental alone. Together, they do. The Scholar built this evidentiary chain themselves — across two rooms, from two dying women who hid the truth in different ways. This is the game's thesis expressed as puzzle design: the Scholar's excellence is what assembled the thing that will be used against them.

What the Obelisk cannot teach: the Jurist's Scales introduces doing-nothing as a meaningful option. The Conservator's Seal uses a combat mechanic as a structural key. The Cartographer's Fold makes the Scholar's body the instrument. The Sympathy Engine confronts the Scholar with their own accumulated pattern without warning. The Last Classification reverses the Scholar-Vanguard dynamic for the first and only time in the game. Together, the five puzzles cover every solution geometry the Obelisk leaves unexplored — and the cross-puzzle chain connects them into a narrative that is larger than any individual room.
