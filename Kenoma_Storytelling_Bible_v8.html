Preface
The Language of the Archive
How Zoroastrian philosophy became the world — in plain, accessible terms

KENOMA is set inside a Persian civilization whose belief system was built on an ancient idea: that the universe is divided between two equal forces — the Seen and the Unseen, light and darkness, the ordered and the hidden. The civilization that built the Archive believed you needed both to understand the complete truth. This split them in two.

We use simple, intuitive names throughout the game for all philosophical and cosmological concepts. No specialist knowledge is required. The philosophy is felt through the architecture, the creatures, and the story — not through terminology a player needs to look up.

The Core Philosophy — In One Paragraph The civilization that built the Archive believed the universe is divided between two equal halves: the Seen (Sacred Fire — truth that is visible, ordered, and lit) and the Unseen (the Hollow — truth that is hidden, shadow, and cold). Neither is "good" or "evil." The complete truth requires both simultaneously. This belief is why the Archive has two worlds: one studying Fire, one studying the Dark. Both are real. Both are valid. The civilization split itself trying to prove one side could exist without the other — and died before resolving the argument.

Chapter I
The Story — What KENOMA Is About
A tragedy about professional excellence. The Scholar succeeds at everything. That is the tragedy.
1.1
The Thesis — Fan Li's Aphorism
Eastern Zhou Chronicles · Fan Li · Sun Tzu Nietzsche · Zarathustra Man in the High Castle · Philip K. Dick

Fan Li, advisor to the King of Yue, won his king a war. On the day of victory he wrote a letter to his companion explaining why he was leaving immediately: "When the birds are gone, the good bow is put away. When the hare is dead, the hunting dog is cooked." He left. His companion stayed. His companion was eliminated. Fan Li became the wealthiest merchant in the eastern kingdoms and died in peace. The Scholar knows this text. They have taught it. They think it is a warning for people who serve bad rulers. It is not. It is a warning for anyone who serves at all — and makes themselves too useful to survive the conclusion.

KENOMA is a tragedy about professional excellence. The Scholar enters a sealed Persian archive and is, without question, exactly what the Archive needs. They translate things no one alive can read. They solve mechanisms sealed for three hundred years. They teach, donate, name, and build. Every decision is the correct professional decision. The tragedy is that every correct professional decision is also a step toward the conclusion.

The 士 Archetype · Eastern Zhou Psychology

The 士 — the wandering intellectual-servant of the Eastern Zhou kingdoms — cannot stop serving. This is not weakness. It is their nature: to find the problem that requires their exact skills, and to apply those skills without reservation until the problem is solved. Fan Li was 士. Sun Tzu was 士. The Scholar is 士. The Archive found the exactly correct version of this personality, and the Vanguard identified them three years before they arrived.

The Scholar's Eastern Zhou philosophical training is a character trait, not a world element. The Archive is Persian — its architecture, its creatures, its language. But the Scholar thinks in Fan Li and Sun Tzu aphorisms because that is what they read during their training. These voices live in their internal monologue. When the Dark Voice emerges in Act I, it speaks in the Scholar's own Eastern Zhou strategic vocabulary — because that is the Scholar's private language for clear-eyed analysis of a situation they are choosing not to see clearly.

1.2
The Three-Act Structure — Nietzsche's Three Metamorphoses
Thus Spoke Zarathustra · Three Transformations of the Spirit

In Zarathustra's first teaching, the spirit must pass through three stages to become fully itself. In KENOMA, these are not metaphors — they are the game's structural law. They are also, importantly, assigned to different characters: two of the three stages belong to the Scholar, and the third belongs to the Vanguard.

🐪 Act I — The Camel Nietzsche · "Thou Shalt"

The spirit that says yes to every burden. It kneels and asks: what is heaviest? Load me. The Scholar in Act I — carrying every Archive puzzle, donating every resource, teaching everything they know. The Camel finds meaning in service. It cannot imagine not serving. This is the Scholar's finest hour and their trap.

🦁 Act II — The Lion Nietzsche · "I Will"

The spirit that says no — that fights for its own freedom. The Scholar when the resonance instrument delivers a position report instead of a greeting. The Lion uses every trap the Camel set, every translation the Camel completed. The Lion's tools are the Camel's gifts turned inside out.

◎ Act III — The Child Nietzsche · "Sacred Yes"

The spirit that creates new values. This belongs to the Vanguard, not the Scholar. After the False Victory, what steps through the fold in the air is someone who has become fully, completely, irreversibly what they always were. The Scholar built the Child. That is the 士's tragedy at its largest scale.

"Man is a rope stretched over an abyss — a dangerous crossing, a dangerous looking-back, a dangerous shuddering and standing still. What is great in man is that he is a bridge, and not an end." Nietzsche · Thus Spoke Zarathustra · The Scholar's opening V.O. at the Archive gate · Spoken as pride · Heard, retroactively, as epitaph
1.3
Two Worlds, Two Truths — The Man in the High Castle
Philip K. Dick · The Man in the High Castle · The Book Within the Book

In Philip K. Dick's novel, a book exists within an alternate-history world where the Axis won World War II. The book — called The Grasshopper Lies Heavy — describes a history where the Allies won instead. Its readers are disturbed because the book is too specific, too internally consistent, too real. They cannot decide: is this world we live in the real one? Or is the book?

KENOMA poses the identical question in Persian stone. The Archive's two worlds are not "real world" and "dark mirror." They are two parallel civilizations that diverged at a single historical choice — Sacred Fire or the Hollow, visible truth or hidden truth. Both civilizations are equally real. Both built the same Archive at the same moment, from opposite sides of the same wall. The Archive is their crossing point, and the Scholar is the only person alive who can move through it.

Deep in the Archive is a document the Scholar comes to call The Third Record. It describes, in precise architectural and philosophical detail, the civilization that could have been: one that successfully integrated both Sacred Fire and the Hollow instead of splitting into two. It is the Archive's equivalent of The Grasshopper Lies Heavy. It describes a world that does not exist. It is, arguably, the most accurate document in either Archive. When the Scholar translates its final passage and the Vanguard — who has been watching silently from the doorway — hears what it contains, she says:

The Vanguard — On Learning the Third Record's Contents "That civilization would not have needed me." No inflection. She leaves the room. The Scholar sits with the translation tablet for a long time. The game does not explain whether this is regret, satisfaction, or simply a three-hundred-year-old fact she has already processed.

The Scholar's choice of which Vanguard to eliminate in Act II is, simultaneously, their choice of which world continues. The Third Record names the path that would have made this choice unnecessary. The Scholar reads it. They know it. The Archive gives them no mechanism to take it.

Chapter II
The Archive — Two Worlds in One Building
Persepolis above · The inverted dark mirror below · The crossing mechanism between them
2.1
How the Civilization Sealed Itself in Two

The scholars who built the Archive were part of the Achaemenid empire's most rigorous philosophical tradition. They held an unusual belief: that the Persian cosmological tradition was correct that the universe is divided between two equal forces — Sacred Fire (truth that is visible, ordered, luminous) and the Hollow (truth that is hidden, shadowed, and cold). Neither force is good or evil. Both are real. Complete knowledge required studying both simultaneously.

So they built the Archive in two layers. The Apadana above: Persepolis-scale hypostyle halls, 72 stone columns with double-bull capitals, processional carved walls showing tribute from thirty nations, fire altars at every junction. And beneath it, precisely mirrored, the Dark Archive: the same column grid inverted underground, the same carved processional walls — except the journey depicted has gone wrong somewhere between the beginning and end of every frieze, the tributaries' faces turning, their gifts becoming something else in transit.

The synchronization mechanism — the Grand Armillary, a seven-ring bronze astronomical instrument at the Archive's summit — kept both worlds stable. As long as it ran, both civilizations remained accessible through the crossing points built into the Archive's walls. The scholars who built it intended it to run forever: a permanent treaty between Sacred Fire and the Hollow, maintained by the instrument itself.

It is still running when the Scholar arrives. The civilization that built it died of other causes — leaving the Archive sealed, the Grand Armillary slowly degrading without maintenance, and the crossing points still active but accumulating debt with every use. The Vanguard, who has been managing the Archive's degradation for three hundred years, is why it has not fully collapsed. She needed it intact until the Scholar arrived. Now that they are here, it needs to hold for a few more weeks.

2.2
The Crossing Points — Moving Between Worlds
SH4 · The Hole in the Wall PoP WW · The Dahaka's Logic Tormented Souls · Cross-Environment Chains

The crossing points — Traversal Focal Points — are Achaemenid astronomical instruments built into the Archive's walls: armillary spheres and star-clock mechanisms calibrated to maintain the dimensional synchronization. The Scholar approaches one, peers through its central ring (seeing the other world through it, upside-down or inverted — the SH4 "look before you commit" animation), and steps through. The crossing takes 2.2 seconds. It cannot be interrupted mid-crossing.

Every crossing increments a hidden Traversal Debt counter. The higher the debt, the more frequently The Boundary — the Archive's crossing-law made physical — manifests to pursue the Scholar. The Vanguard has zero crossing debt. She does not cross. She waits in each world for the Scholar to bring what she needs. The Scholar pays all the crossing costs. This asymmetry is visible from the beginning of the game. Its full meaning is not visible until Act III.

World A Side · Sacred Fire Crossing from Above
A polished bronze armillary sphere set into a limestone pillar. The Scholar peers through its central ring and sees World B through it — cold, dark, inverted, columns hanging from an underground ceiling. The fire vessel in the Scholar's hand dims slightly near the focal point. Crossing feels like stepping through cold water into a room that is the same shape as a room they know, but whose every detail is wrong.
World B Side · The Hollow Crossing from Below
The same mechanism — jade rather than bronze, colder to the touch, emitting a low resonance the Scholar feels in their chest before they see it. World A's fire altars visible through the rings: warm, distant, upside down. Crossing feels like surfacing from cold water into absolute dark. The Scholar's fire vessel is the only warm light in the Dark Archive in three centuries. Everything that lives here already knows where it is.
Key Asymmetry — The Vanguard's Design The Boundary never pursues the Vanguard. She does not cross. She has designed her entire plan across three hundred years such that the Scholar bears all the crossing costs and she bears none. In Prince of Persia: Warrior Within, the Dahaka pursues the Prince because the Prince altered the timeline — the pursuit is not malice, it is consequence. The Boundary works the same way: it is the law pursuing the one who breaks it. The Scholar breaks it every time. The Vanguard never does.
2.3
World A — The Apadana of Sacred Fire
AitD · Fixed Camera Architecture SH4 · Safe Room Warmth Built Before Corruption Metro 2033 · Warmth as Diegetic State

The Apadana is Persepolis at its full imperial scale — sealed intact for three hundred years, every fire altar cold until the Scholar arrives. When the Scholar lights a fire altar (spending oil from the fire vessel), the fire spreads through carved channels in the floor to neighboring altars. Over Act I the Apadana becomes, corridor by corridor, genuinely beautiful: warm amber light, 72 columns revealed in their full carved detail, the thirty-nation processional friezes illuminated for the first time in centuries. This accumulation of warmth is deliberate game design. The Scholar should love this place. The False Victory must protect something the player genuinely values.

WA-001 The Gate of All Nations World A · Entry

Two great winged bull-guardians — their stone faces calm, wings spread wide enough to shadow six people — flank the Archive's only surface entrance. The inscription above the gate runs in three languages simultaneously: Old Persian, Elamite, and Babylonian. The Scholar can read all three. The Vanguard cannot read the third. This is the first thing the Scholar teaches her. She does not thank them. She files the information. The game's central dynamic — Scholar teaches, Vanguard learns, data accumulates — is established in the opening ninety seconds.

Opening Scene Function
Scholar names a corrupted Fire-Warden using the gate inscription — the Naming combat tutorial. The Vanguard eliminates a second Fire-Warden with her blade simultaneously. Both combat philosophies introduced in parallel: the Scholar's approach is slower, requires prior reading, and is permanent. The Vanguard's is instant and leaves the problem available to repeat elsewhere.
The Vanguard's First Observation
She watches the Scholar's naming method with complete, unblinking attention. Says nothing. On first playthrough this reads as interest. On second playthrough the silence is unmistakably data collection — she is watching the Scholar's method, timing it, filing the result against her behavioral model. She has been building this model for three years from the Scholar's academic dossier. First in-person data point: confirmed.
WA-003 The Eastern Treasury — Hall of Records World A · Primary Puzzle Hub

Thirty thousand cuneiform tablets, organized in a classification system the Scholar must reverse-engineer. This room should feel like a professional reward — the finest archive the Scholar has ever entered. Buried in the northwest stacks is a partially damaged tablet requiring a deep reading to decipher: the Vanguard's Archive entry record from three hundred and twelve years ago. Written in administrative Elamite — the empire's bureaucratic language, not the sacred scripts. Professional. Businesslike. Not a pilgrimage. A mission. The final line, undated: "Scholar profile identified. Arrival estimated within two generations." She knew the Scholar was coming before the Scholar's grandparents were born.

Overheard Scene — Metro Principle — Fully Missable
Scholar enters from the south passage and hears the Vanguard in the deep stacks speaking quietly to one of the dormant Fire-Wardens in the eastern alcove. She is issuing it a patrol schedule. In Elamite — the administrative language of the empire's military logistics. The Warden is obeying her. She has been managing the Archive's creatures as a logistics problem for three centuries. She turns, sees the Scholar: "You came early." She had a contingency for early arrival. This is the Metro moment — more character revealed in an overheard private moment than in any scripted cutscene.
The Grand Armillary Manual — Hidden Consequence
The Grand Armillary's operating manual is in this room — containing the activation sequence for the game's final major puzzle (PZ-007). A Scholar who deep-reads it here has the sequence memorized when they need it under Guardian pressure. A Scholar who skims will face the same puzzle with no prior information and 35 seconds before the Boundary reaches them. The game does not signal this choice's importance at the time it is made.
WA-007 The Grand Armillary Gallery — Archive Summit World A · Act II Climax · Guardian's Only Weakness

The Archive's highest accessible space — a circular gallery open to the night sky through a domed stone oculus. The Grand Armillary fills the center: seven concentric bronze rings, each tracking one of the Archive's seven sacred astronomical markers, large enough that the Scholar can walk between the inner rings. This instrument is simultaneously the Archive's synchronization mechanism, the crossing system's control center, and — per the operating manual in WA-003 — the only mechanism that can dissolve the Boundary permanently, by collapsing all Traversal Focal Points simultaneously. The scholars who built the Archive included this emergency protocol because they understood what they had created.

The Aline Cedrac Kill — Intellectual Combat (AitD:TNN Model)
Aline Cedrac defeats the Hybrid Elder in AitD: The New Nightmare using the laboratory's own lightning rod — not a weapon she carries, but the facility's infrastructure she has studied throughout the game. The Scholar's Grand Armillary sequence works identically: no weapon fired, no oil spent, no bolts used. The Scholar activates the Archive's own astronomical instrument — which they have been studying since PZ-001 — using a sequence found in a World B document. Scholarly comprehension as the killing blow.
The Six-Minute Window
After the Boundary dissolves, the Grand Armillary Gallery goes absolutely quiet under the stars. The Scholar stands at the Archive's highest point, having just sealed a dimensional enforcer outside reality using nothing but knowledge and the Archive's own instruments. This is the apex of the Scholar's professional life. The camera should hold on their face in the starlight — satisfied, complete. This triumph must land as genuine. The False Victory sequence begins six minutes later.
2.4
World B — The Dark Archive
SH1 · Fog as Gameplay · Obscured Navigation AitD:TNN · Light as Weapon and Vulnerability SH2 · Architecture as Guilt

The Dark Archive is not a dungeon. It is the Apadana's shadow given architecture. Same rooms, same column grid — but columns hang from ceilings, fire altars are cold void-pits from which darkness seeps upward, and the carved processional friezes show the same thirty-nation tribute delegation as above — except the journey has gone wrong. The tributaries' gifts are becoming something else in transit. Their faces are slowly turning. The procession has been moving for three hundred years and has not reached its destination.

The Scholar's fire vessel is the only warm light in the Dark Archive. This is both the primary tool and the primary vulnerability. Everything that lives in the Hollow can see the Scholar's exact location at all times, because the fire vessel announces it in amber through absolute dark. In AitD: The New Nightmare, Aline's light source is simultaneously her primary weapon and the thing that reveals her to the darkness. The Scholar's fire vessel works identically — the same act that illuminates also betrays.

WB-001 The Inverted Gate World B · Entry Point

The same gate as above — but the winged bull-guardians' faces are concave, pressed inward as if something large has been pushing at them from this side for centuries. The trilingual inscription above reads the same text as the surface gate — but in reverse language order. When the Scholar translates it, the reversal changes the meaning: the surface gate says "all who enter, enter with truth." The Inverted Gate says "all who enter, enter with what they believe is truth." This single syllable of difference is the entire philosophy of the Dark Archive.

Vanguard B — First Presence
Heard, not seen. Footsteps on stone, then silence somewhere beyond the fire vessel's illumination range. When the Scholar calls out, no answer. She is noting that they carry fire — that they have arrived using the Sacred Fire civilization's tools in the Hollow civilization's archive. She is recalibrating her timeline. This Scholar is useful but less prepared for the Hollow than she hoped. She has a contingency for this. She does not leave.
SH1 Navigation Model
In Silent Hill 1, the fog is not atmospheric decoration — it is a practical gameplay constraint that limits the player's vision and forces them to navigate by sound and careful movement rather than sight. The Dark Archive's absolute darkness functions identically: the Scholar navigates by fire vessel light (limited radius), by the astrolabe's resonance static (directional creature awareness), and by memory of the Apadana's mirror architecture. The Hollow rewards players who learned the Apadana above by heart.
WB-004 The Dark Library World B · Primary Puzzle Hub

The Hollow's equivalent of the Eastern Treasury — same tablet count, organized by Hollow-logic: dangerous texts appear most accessible, most valuable texts appear restricted. The Scholar must learn that Hollow-logic inverts Sacred Fire-logic in every classification decision. In the northwest alcove — the most apparently restricted position, therefore containing the most valuable texts — the Scholar finds two things: the cosmological sequence needed for PZ-007, and the journal of the previous scholar. Fifty years old. Fifty years dead. Their analytical style is close to the Scholar's own — similar intellectual habits, similar puzzle approach. Reading it is the most unsettling moment of the game. The Scholar is not the first. The previous one did not last. Vanguard B adjusted her methodology between attempts.

Vanguard B — Private Archive Scene (Metro Model)
Scholar enters from the south passage and hears Vanguard B at the far alcove, speaking quietly into one of the cold void-pits in the floor. Not prayer — requisition. She is asking something in the Hollow for information. She listens. She is satisfied. This is her private intelligence network: three centuries of cultivating information from the Dark Archive's entities. She turns and sees the Scholar. Does not apologize. Does not explain. "You found the library." Not a question. She has been tracking their location.
The Previous Scholar's Journal
The notes anticipate several of the Scholar's own analytical approaches. The handwriting is confident — the same kind of confident that the Scholar's own notes are. The last entry: "The Boundary appeared at the fourteenth crossing. I did not have enough time at the Armillary. I needed six more seconds." The Scholar reads this and understands: they are the second attempt. Vanguard B recalibrated her selection criteria after the first scholar died. The Scholar is the improved version of what she needed. This is the most unsettling document in the Archive, and it is fully optional to find.
WB-∞ The Chamber of Convergence — Third Record Location Both Worlds · Deep Archive · 5 Crossings Required

The Archive's deepest space — accessible only by crossing the Interface five times along the descending focal-point chain. Each crossing adds Traversal Debt; the Boundary's pressure climbs accordingly. The Chamber exists partially in both worlds simultaneously: fire altars on one side, void-pits on the other, the architecture of both civilizations present in the same room, neither canceling the other. In the center: a single tablet rack containing the Third Record. Both Vanguards have been meeting here for three centuries — the only space where they can both exist simultaneously without crossing.

The Third Record
Philip K. Dick's book-within-a-book describes the world that didn't happen — so vivid and internally consistent that its readers cannot determine which history is real. The Third Record does the same: describes, in precise architectural and philosophical detail, a civilization that integrated Sacred Fire and the Hollow rather than splitting. The civilization in the Third Record maintained the crossing as a permanent institution. They built a bridge that stayed a bridge. They did not have to choose. The Scholar reads this and understands that the Archive they have spent weeks translating is the record of a civilization that failed the experiment the Third Record describes as successful.
The Moment of Both Vanguards
Both Vanguards arrive at the Chamber simultaneously as the Scholar nears the final passage — one from each entrance, arriving at the same moment from opposite doorways. They stand on opposite sides of the Chamber's central divide as the Scholar reads the last passage aloud. When the Scholar looks up and sees both of them there — two versions of the same person, standing in the space where their civilization should have been — this is the game's emotional peak. Then Vanguard B says, without inflection: "That civilization would not have needed me." She leaves. Vanguard A watches her go and says nothing.
Chapter III
The Characters
The Scholar who cannot stop. The Vanguard who has been waiting. The Voice that knows the strategy.
3.1
The Scholar — The 士 Who Read Fan Li and Stayed Anyway
Eastern Zhou · 士 Archetype · Fan Li · Sun Tzu AitD:TNN · Aline Cedrac Combat Model Metro 2033 · Diegetic Status Tormented Souls · Physical Inventory

The Scholar is not naïve. They have read every relevant warning. They know Fan Li's aphorism — they have taught it. They know Sun Tzu's principle that a good general wins before the battle is fought. They know the 士 archetype from the inside: the wandering intellectual whose constitution requires finding the problem that matches their exact skills, and applying those skills without reservation until the problem is resolved. They know all of this about themselves. And they enter the Archive anyway — because the Archive is the finest, rarest, most precisely tailored problem they will ever encounter, and the 士 cannot decline that. Fan Li left. The Scholar stays. This is not ignorance. This is character.

Physical Appearance and Tools

Travel-worn magi robes — practical, not ceremonial — that accumulate visible damage over the game. No health number: the robe tears and darkens, the Scholar's posture degrades, their hands tremble on puzzle interfaces when badly hurt (Tormented Souls model). A leather satchel — the 15-slot physical inventory, opened with a 1.2-second animation, items held and rotated in 3D space. A bronze fire vessel at their hip. A crossbow found in Act I. Across the game, the Scholar's hands are always busy: tracing cuneiform during a translation, winding the crossbow mechanism after a fight, carefully pouring oil into the fire vessel from a clay flask.

The Two Vanguards — Shadow and Flame
Prince of Persia II · Shadow and Flame — Two Aspects PoP SoT · Farah's Betrayal Logic PoP WW · Kaileena's Sympathy Eastern Zhou · 將 Archetype — Military Commander

She was a senior commander in the Achaemenid Immortals corps before she entered the Archive three hundred years ago seeking tactical and cosmological knowledge. She is someone who made a plan when she had options, executed it patiently when she had only time, and is completing it now that she finally has the Scholar.

In Prince of Persia: Shadows and Flame, the Prince confronts two aspects of his own power — shadow and flame — that are simultaneously his greatest tools and the forces that will consume him if misunderstood. The two Vanguards are KENOMA's shadow and flame: the same person, the same extraordinary military intelligence, expressed across three hundred years under two different cosmological truths.

Military precision. Sentences end on certainties. Asks one question per subject, never repeats it. Expansive, patient, philosophical. Three centuries studying the Hollow has changed her relationship to time — she measures in generations now. Why She Is Not a Villain — Farah and Kaileena

Farah in Prince of Persia: The Sands of Time does not betray the Prince out of malice. She runs an independent calculation and reaches a different conclusion. Her calculation is correct. The Prince's survival was always secondary to the larger problem she was solving — she simply never told him, because telling him would have made him stop helping. The Vanguard works by exactly this logic. She managed the Scholar with great care. She wanted them to last long enough to complete the Archive's full translation. They lasted exactly long enough. She did not hurry the conclusion and did not delay it. She is a very good strategist. In her own frame, she has been entirely consistent from Day One.

Kaileena in Warrior Within is sympathetic because her goal is simple survival — not conquest, not malice, simply the desire not to be eliminated by a fate she had no hand in creating. She uses the Prince as a tool and genuinely cares about him and both things are simultaneously true. The Vanguard works identically: she cares about the Scholar. The three Camel Phase emotional resonance moments are real. The oil flask in the corridor is a genuine act of care and a calculated resource maintenance decision, at the same time, in the same gesture.

The Puzzles — Expertise as Key and Trap
Deep reading opens better solutions. The archive rewards the reader. The trap is what makes the reader valuable.
AitD 1 · Documents as Mechanical Fuel TS1+2 · Multi-Step Cross-Environment Chains PoP SoT · Environmental Trap Architecture SH2 · Delayed Invisible Consequence

Every major puzzle follows one design rule: the Scholar's professional expertise — cuneiform translation, archive classification knowledge, astronomical literacy in the Naming Tongue — is the mechanical key. Players who read deeply unlock better solutions. Players who skim improvise under pressure. The Archive rewards the reader. This is also Fan Li's aphorism from the puzzle design side: making the Scholar's capability mechanical makes it a trap, because the same reading ability that solves every puzzle is what makes them indispensable to the Vanguard.

PZ-001
Reading the Library's Mind — Eastern Treasury Catalogue
WA-003 · Early Camel · AitD Document System

The need: The Grand Armillary's operating manual contains the activation sequence for PZ-007. Finding it means understanding the Eastern Treasury's three-axis classification system.

  1. Scholar skims three visible index tablets — learns three axes exist, cannot determine their hierarchy. Not enough information yet.
  2. Standard-depth read of a fourth tablet (an administrative memo about locating a specific document) — reveals which axis is primary. Scholar now has the full system and can navigate to any tablet by coordinate.
  3. Navigate to the Grand Armillary manual using the three-axis coordinate. It is in the "restricted, Naming Tongue language, astronomy subject" position. A simple cipher locks the alcove — Scholar cracks it by recognizing three symbols that repeat across all the index tablets and whose sound values are determinable from context.
  4. The hidden consequence: Deep-reading the manual here stores the activation sequence. The game does not signal this. Players who skip it will face PZ-007 with the Boundary approaching in 35 seconds and no sequence memorized.
Why This Puzzle Belongs Here
Establishes the core AitD document-depth system: deep reading costs time but carries forward to future puzzles in ways the Scholar cannot predict. Every choice to skim is saving ten minutes now and spending survival later. The player has no idea they are making this trade-off. They will understand it in WA-007.

The Hollow valve sequence runs left-center-right. A Dark Archive inscription explains this inversion to a Scholar who reads it — and leaves a Scholar who skims to try the same sequence in both worlds (which fails, resets the system, and adds two minutes of Boundary-pressure waiting time).

What the Vanguard is doing: While the Scholar crosses to World B, Vanguard A is visible in the Hypostyle Forest background — examining the oil conduit channels in the floor. She is measuring them. PZ-004
The Dark Library's Inverted Logic
WB-004 · Mid Camel · Tormented Souls Cross-Environment Chain

The setup: The Dark Library organizes its tablets using Hollow-logic — which inverts Sacred Fire-logic in every classification axis. Chapter VII
The Ten Endings — What the Scholar Built
She never transforms. The quality of what the Scholar gave her determines the quality of what she completes as.

The two hidden behavioral scores determine which ending fires. The Vanguard does not transform in any ending. She straightens her tunic, turns, and is entirely, completely, irreversibly herself. What varies is the quality of that self — which is entirely a function of what the Scholar invested in the Archive, in her, in the crossing, in the knowledge they shared or withheld. In Silent Hill 2, the ending reflects who James was across the whole run. In KENOMA, the ending reflects what the Scholar built — because the Scholar is a builder, and the Vanguard is the building.

Lv.10–20

The Overwhelmed Flame

The Archive's knowledge floods a mind given insufficient foundation to receive it. Military training and cosmological comprehension fight each other. She moves erratically — never fully wrong, never fully right. Graceless. Sad. The Scholar watches someone fail at being what they could have been. This is the Scholar's stinginess made physical.

Lost in Her Own Dark

Three centuries of Hollow-knowledge collapses inward. She moves through the room in fractured patterns, unable to locate herself in the space she has inhabited for three hundred years. The Scholar watches someone become lost in their own home. The Scholar's failure to invest — in her, in the crossing, in the knowledge — has made her less than she was.

Lv.221–40

The Siege Commander

Tactical, competent, without transcendence. She destroys the Scholar's options through logistics — collapses corridors, seals fire altars, eliminates cover methodically. Attrition as strategy. She is very good at this. She is not at her ceiling. The Scholar sees capability below its potential and cannot unsee it.

The Uncertain Oracle

Hollow-knowledge partially integrated. She moves with occasional startling prescience — positions herself exactly where the Scholar is about to be — interspersed with errors a fully-prepared mind would not make. Superior intelligence with imperfect tools. The Scholar gave her good tools. Not enough of them.

Lv.341–60

The Commander Complete

Full military precision. She reads the room in one second, identifies the Scholar's position relative to their last save point and demonstrated crossbow range, closes the distance in minimum steps. Emotionless. The great commander archetype of the Eastern Zhou chronicles fully resolved. Not cruel. Accurate. This is the middle of what the Scholar could have given her.

The Patient Dark

She possesses the Dark Archive completely, without urgency. Always exactly ten steps behind the Scholar — never more, never less. The architecture quietly rearranges around her. Every exit is exactly where she calculated it would be. The Scholar is running through a room that has been designed for this moment for three centuries.

Lv.461–80

The Living Mirror

She has built a complete behavioral model. When the Scholar raises the crossbow, she has already moved. When they approach a trap, she stops one breath from the pressure plate and looks at them directly. She has simulated their complete decision tree. She waits for them to exhaust its branches. She built this mirror from what the Scholar gave her. The Scholar has been looking at themselves the whole time.

The Author of the Dark

She speaks throughout — not to the Scholar, but as if dictating a sacred text. Walks through the room extinguishing the Scholar's light sources using the Traversal Skill from across the room. Creatures stand aside for her as she passes — she Named them, too, learning the Naming Tongue from the Scholar's own translations. She can do everything the Scholar learned to do. Without any of the costs.

Lv.581–100

The Architect of the Apadana

She does not move toward the Scholar. She moves around them — closing exits using Traversal, repositioning fire altars, sealing the geometry of escape options one by one. When the room is finished she stands still. She waits for the Scholar to understand what they are standing in. Then she takes three steps. Before she begins: "You translated the gate inscription on your first day. You Named the Fire-Warden in the hypostyle before I had located its designation. Darkness Completed

She sits in the center of the Dark Archive's deepest chamber. Extinguishes the Scholar's fire vessel from across the room using Traversal. Watches them in absolute darkness. She does not need to see them. She has their complete behavioral model — every route preference, every crossbow range, every timing pattern across the entire run. What All Eight Sources Build Together Eastern Zhou Chronicles give the Scholar their psychology — the 士 who cannot stop, Fan Li's aphorism as thesis. Nietzsche's Zarathustra gives the three acts their shape and the Scholar their opening self-definition, spoken as pride, heard retroactively as epitaph. The Man in the High Castle gives the two worlds their equal weight — neither is real and both are real — and the Third Record names the path not taken. Alone in the Dark gives the Scholar their combat model: intellectual kills above all, light as weapon, documents as mechanical fuel. Silent Hill 1–4 gives the game its invisible spine: radio-static proximity, behavioral tracking that only becomes visible at the end, world-shift mid-level, and the safe room that becomes the haunted room. Prince of Persia II, SoT, WW, TT give the Vanguard her emotional architecture — Shadow and Flame as two aspects of one person, Farah's cold calculation, Kaileena's sympathetic tool-use, the Dahaka's consequence-logic, the Dark Prince's inner voice. Tormented Souls gives the puzzles their physical inventory logic and cross-environment chain structure. Metro 2033 gives the world its physicality — no HUD, every status physical, every resource a compound decision, every overheard moment more character than any cutscene.

Together they build one thing: a tragedy about professional excellence. The Scholar was the best bow the Archive ever held. When the birds were gone, the bow was put away. Past tense is not a judgment. It is a precision.
