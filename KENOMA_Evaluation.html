§ 01Evaluating the Final Synthesis — What It Got Right
Research confirms the synthesis where it matters most
Confirmed · Market Positioning Horror + Narrative = The Strongest Possible Indie Position
The synthesis identified KENOMA's genre position as a strength. Research confirms: Horror has been the #1 indie genre on Steam for three consecutive years. Narrative games surged from 17 hits in 2022 to 51 hits in 2025 — the largest genre growth in indie gaming. KENOMA sits at the intersection of both, which is not only the strongest market position but the one with the least overlap from competitors. Only 7.2% of indie horror games succeeded in 2024 — but those that succeed disproportionately outperform. The synthesis was right: this is a differentiated position.
Sources: Chris Zukowski Steam data 2025 · WN Hub analytics 2024 · Try Evidence survival horror report
Confirmed · Audio Priority Audio Is Not Just a Gap — It Is Structurally Primary
The synthesis called audio the most critical single gap. Research validates this with data: GDC 2024 audio analysis found that 70% of horror game immersion comes from sound cues, not visuals. One study found that implementing 3D audio zones in a stealth horror title increased player tension by 40% and session time by 18%. The synthesis was not merely identifying a production gap — it was identifying what the genre's highest-performing axis already knows. Audio in horror is not supporting; it is structural.
Sources: GDC 2024 Audio Analysis · Kreonit 3D audio case study · Hellblade II audio director interview 2024
Confirmed · Central Mechanic Originality The Protagonist-Built Antagonist Is Still Genuinely New
The synthesis claimed no game scores all 5 uniqueness criteria. Research confirms this from a different angle: current AI companion/antagonist research shows the industry is building reactive enemies (Hello Neighbor, TLOU2 enemies calling each other by name) and supportive companions (emotion-aware AI). No current game combines both — a companion who simultaneously builds genuine warmth AND uses that warmth as strategic intelligence for elimination. The Hello Neighbor model (AI learns routes you repeated) is adjacent but lacks the relationship layer. The synthesis was right.
Sources: NVIDIA ACE research 2024 · TLOU2 AI analysis · Hello Neighbor adaptive AI case study

The synthesis was right that this is both an opportunity and a responsibility.

§ 02What the Synthesis Missed — Six Blind Spots
Each one exposed by industry research. Each one matters to the project's quality or success.
The Overarching Blind Spot The synthesis was built from the inside of the project outward. Everything it found was already in the documents. Industry research reveals what the documents couldn't see: what the best games in this space are actually doing with audio, AI, localization, and market positioning — and where the gap between KENOMA's design ambition and current industry practice is widest.
Audio Research Missed 1 — The Dark Voice Needs Binaural Audio, Not Just Reverb

The synthesis specifies "Scholar's own voice + reverb" for the Dark Voice. This is adequate. Industry research reveals that Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice — the gold standard for internal-voice audio design — used binaural recording, not reverb. The difference is not cosmetic. Binaural audio places the voice inside the listener's head, emerging from behind them, from the left side of their skull, from a specific spatial location that changes line by line. Reverb produces an echo. Binaural produces a presence. For a voice that is the Scholar's own strategic intelligence surfacing against their will, presence is the correct sensation — not echo.

Hellblade II expanded this further, making binaural audio integral to world acoustics, not just internal voices. The Hollow's acoustic environment — cold stone, absolute darkness, the Scholar's fire vessel as the only warm sound — is exactly the environment where spatial audio produces the most dramatic effect. A sound heard behind the Scholar as they enter the Dark Archive is genuinely terrifying. The same sound on stereo is atmospheric.

Research data: Stealth horror title with 3D audio zones showed 40% tension increase and 18% longer session time. Binaural audio requires headphone use but does not need external hardware — and spatial audio environments show players staying 35% longer on average. FMOD and Wwise (standard middleware for Unity/Unreal) both support binaural HRTF implementation for indie budgets.
What KENOMA should do: Specify the Dark Voice as binaural from the beginning — not a post-production treatment but a design intent that shapes the recording. Every Dark Voice line should specify which direction it emerges from: behind (the Scholar's own suppressed thought surfacing), beside (strategic advice alongside their current action), or ahead (warning about what they're about to do). The Hollow should have its own full binaural acoustic identity. This is not expensive with FMOD/Wwise. It is a design decision made now.
AI Design Missed 2 — The Vanguard Needs an "Intensity Director" Layer

Dead Space Remake's "Intensity Director" dynamically controls lighting, audio, and enemy spawn timing based on the player's current stress state, creating a pacing system that prevents emotional flatness across long play sessions. Amnesia: The Bunker uses procedural resource and monster behavior placement for the same purpose. Neither system exists in KENOMA's design, and across 150 minutes of play, the current design risks emotional flatness in the Camel Phase's extended middle sections.

The synthesis correctly identifies the Camel Phase's three resonance moments — but between those moments, there is currently no pacing system. Week 3 of Act I risks feeling like "more translation, more warmth accumulating." The Intensity Director model would monitor how long since the last meaningful tension event and dynamically adjust: a creature patrol that passes unusually close to the Scholar's current position, a fire vessel that sputters briefly without being critically low, Vanguard A appearing in a room slightly before expected. These are not scripted events — they are pacing responses.

Research data: AI-Driven Fear Systems are identified as a defining element of horror in 2025: "Artificial intelligence now tailors fear based on player choices. Enemies adapt, sounds change, and scares are never repeated the same way twice." (Fearing.org, 2025)
What KENOMA should do: Add an Archive Tension System (ATS) — a lightweight pacing monitor that reads the time elapsed since the last tension event and the player's current resource state, then selects from a pool of environmental micro-events to maintain engagement without triggering the BMS. These events must be diegetically motivated (creature patrols adjust based on the Vanguard's real management patterns) and must never feel scripted. The Calibration Channel's audio cycle is already a tension management system — the ATS would extend this principle to the whole Archive.
Market Research Missed 3 — Chinese Is Now the Most Common Steam Language

The synthesis correctly identifies Persian/Farsi localization as missing. Research exposes a larger blind spot: Chinese is now the most common language among Steam players in 2025. Analysis of hit narrative games finds that "The Roottrees are Dead was the only narrative game lacking a Chinese translation" — and it was an outlier. Hit narrative games in 2025 are almost universally translated into Simplified Chinese. Games without Chinese translations are functionally invisible to a significant portion of the audience that drives narrative game success.

This matters particularly for KENOMA because the Achaemenid Persian setting has a specific resonance in Chinese historical and cultural education — the Silk Road connected Persia and China. KENOMA's themes of an ancient scholar reading a dead civilization's archive in two languages is specifically likely to resonate with Chinese academic and literary game audiences. This is not a generic localization — it is a targeted cultural connection.

Research data: "Chinese is now the most common language among players" (How To Market A Game, 2025). Narrative games are now 51 hits in 2025 vs 17 in 2022, with Chinese FMV narrative games representing a significant portion of new entries. Where Winds Meet (Chinese-developed) generated $100.5M and 87.6% positive reviews — demonstrating that Chinese cultural content now reaches global Steam audiences.
What KENOMA should do: Add Simplified Chinese to the localization plan alongside Farsi. The cultural connection between Achaemenid Persia and the Silk Road could be explicitly acknowledged in marketing for Chinese audiences. Chinese localization should be budgeted from Tier 1 (Chapter One), not deferred to Tier 2. A game about an archive of two truths, translated into a language that connects Persian and Chinese civilizations historically, is a self-reinforcing story.
AI Design Missed 4 — The Vanguard's Route Learning Has a Known Pitfall

The synthesis describes the Lion Phase's Terrain Control System and Utility Score without addressing a known failure mode in adaptive AI antagonists. Hello Neighbor — the most widely cited example of an antagonist that learns player routes — received mixed reviews precisely because players found the AI's adaptation either too predictable (it always blocked the route you just used) or too opaque (it blocked routes you weren't planning to use). The game's AI crossed the line from "formidable" to "frustrating" because the adaptation felt arbitrary rather than intelligent.

The Vanguard faces this exact risk. If her route-learning feels like "she always blocks what I just did," she becomes a reactive obstacle rather than a 300-year intelligence. If her route-learning is too accurate too fast, the Lion Phase becomes unfair. The synthesis documents the system without addressing this balance problem — which is the hardest calibration problem in adaptive AI antagonist design.

Research data: Games with AI that learns from player patterns are increasingly common. The challenge is "ensuring AI behavior remains fair" and "avoiding overly predictable patterns." TLOU2 solved this through enemy coordination and calling by name — making the adaptation feel social and human, not computational. The difference between "the AI reacted to what I did" and "the AI understands me as a person" is the difference between frustrating and terrifying.
What KENOMA should do: The Vanguard must adapt through understanding, not through mirroring. She doesn't block the route the Scholar just used — she pre-positions for the route that Scholar-as-modeled-person would take next. This requires the behavioral model to be producing predictions, not reactions. The specific design protection: Vanguard adaptation is always one move ahead, never one move behind. She's not responding to what the Scholar did. She's acting on what she predicts the Scholar will do. This distinction is what makes her feel intelligent rather than mechanical — and it's what justifies the 300-year model.
Discovery & Marketing Missed 5 — The Demo Is Also the Project's Best Marketing Asset

The synthesis treats the vertical slice as a production milestone (prove the mechanic, show to investors). Research reveals it is simultaneously the project's most powerful ongoing marketing tool. Keeping a free demo live after launch is strongly correlated with discovery, and only 5 of 65 hit games kept their demo live at data collection — making this a rare and high-signal move. The synthesis does not include a demo persistence strategy.

More specifically: KENOMA's vertical slice, if it successfully demonstrates the core mechanic in 30 minutes, is the most effective pitch for any external audience — players, publishers, and investors. The three-tablet, one-crossing, one-creature demo that proves the Vanguard treats two Scholars differently is exactly the kind of experience that gets written about, streamed, and shared. The synthesis treats the demo as a milestone. It should also be treated as a product.

Research data: "My gut tells me if you have a hit game there is something MAGIC about it that goes beyond first-play novelty" (Chris Zukowski, 2025). Demo availability: only 5/65 hit games kept demo live, suggesting strong positive signal from those who do. Content creators finding the game through a demo generates organic reach that no marketing spend can replicate.
What KENOMA should do: Design the vertical slice from the beginning as a permanently available demo product, not just an internal milestone. The WA-001 → WA-003 sequence, with VA Stages 1–3 and one full recognition moment, should be polished to final quality before any investor shows. Keep it live on Steam/itch.io permanently after Chapter One releases. The demo's ongoing availability is a continuous discovery mechanism that costs nothing to maintain.
Cultural Risk Missed 6 — The Synthesis Underestimates the Orientalism Risk

The synthesis identifies the Persian cultural setting as a strength and flags the need for a cultural consultant. What it does not address: academic research has established that Middle East / Persian settings in games face intense scrutiny for Orientalism — specifically, for treating the culture as an exotic backdrop rather than a historically specific civilization. The games most praised for handling this well (Assassin's Creed Mirage, Heaven's Vault) are praised specifically because they avoided treating the setting as decoration. The games most criticized did exactly what KENOMA must not do: used the aesthetics without the specificity.

KENOMA's design documents are extraordinarily specific — Achaemenid, not broadly "Middle Eastern"; Zoroastrian, not Islamic; Persepolis, not generic Persian fantasy. This specificity is protection. But the design documents are internal. The marketing materials, trailers, and Steam page must carry this specificity publicly, or the game risks being seen as "Persian aesthetic horror" rather than "a game that takes pre-Islamic Persian civilization seriously for the first time in horror."

Research data: Leiden Medievalists Blog (2025) identifies four dominant stereotypes and notes games that escape them do so through "historical specificity rather than cultural fantasy." Assassin's Creed Mirage received academic praise for avoiding these stereotypes. Games that make generic Middle Eastern aesthetics face backlash from both academic critics and the communities being represented.
What KENOMA should do: The cultural specificity that protects the design documents must be made explicit in all external-facing materials. The Steam description, the game's official description, early press materials — all of them should name Achaemenid, Persepolis, Zoroastrian, and pre-Islamic explicitly. Cultural specificity is both ethical protection and marketing differentiation. A game that says "Achaemenid horror" in its first sentence reaches a completely different audience than one that says "Persian horror." Both audiences should know which one they're engaging with.
§ 03Six Industry-Informed Upgrades
Each is a specific, buildable improvement that the research supports
Upgrade I · Audio Add Binaural HRTF to the Audio Direction Document as a Core Specification
Current State

The audio direction document doesn't exist yet. When written, it would specify "Scholar's voice + reverb" for the Dark Voice. The Dark Archive has an acoustic identity described in prose ("cold stone, absolute dark, fire vessel as only warm sound") but no technical specification.

The synthesis treats audio as a production gap to fill. The research suggests audio should be treated as a design language with technical requirements.

Upgraded State

The Dark Voice is specified as binaural from recording brief to implementation. Each line has a spatial direction: behind (suppressed thought), beside (concurrent calculation), ahead (predictive warning). The Dark Archive is specified as HRTF-implemented using FMOD or Wwise — not just reverb, but full spatial audio with occlusion and distance modeling. The crossing experience (2.2 seconds) uses spatial audio to produce the "stepping through cold water" sensation: World A's warmth sweeping away, World B's cold depth materializing from below.

This is implementable at indie budget using existing middleware. It is not expensive. It is a design decision made now, before recording begins. Once recording begins with the wrong specification, retroactive binaural treatment is possible but inferior.

Upgrade II · AI Archive Tension System — Lightweight Pacing Layer for the Full Camel Phase
Current State

The Camel Phase has three designed resonance moments (Recognition, Flask, Admission of Hope) and an overall emotional arc (professional delight → warmth → dread) but no pacing system between those moments. Sessions between resonance moments depend entirely on player-initiated activity.

Upgraded State

The Archive Tension System (ATS) monitors time since last tension event and current player resource state, selecting from a pool of environmental micro-events. Examples: a creature patrol moves unusually close (not a threat — just a reminder); the fire vessel sputters for 2 seconds without being critically low; the Vanguard appears in a room 3 minutes earlier than her modeled schedule; a tablet shifts slightly from where the Scholar left it. None of these are scripted cutscenes. All of them are diegetically motivated and pull no BMS events. The ATS makes the Archive feel alive between its designed peaks — without fabricating false alarms or cheapening the genuine tension moments.

Upgrade III · AI Vanguard Route Intelligence Respecified: Prediction, Not Reaction
Current State

The Terrain Control System describes the Vanguard using the Scholar's observed route patterns to pre-position in Act II. The risk: if the Vanguard blocks routes the Scholar just used, she feels like a reactive obstacle — frustrating rather than intelligent. Hello Neighbor received exactly this criticism.

Upgraded State

The Vanguard's route intelligence is explicitly respecified: she predicts next moves, never mirrors last moves. Her behavioral model is a probability map of where the Scholar will go based on the pattern of where they've been — not a block on where they just were. If the Scholar has been taking the eastern approach 70% of the time, she pre-positions to intercept the eastern approach. If the Scholar takes the western approach instead, she adapts — she doesn't block the western approach immediately, she updates the probability map.

The critical rule: the Vanguard is always one move ahead, never one move behind. Players who understand this will feel respected by the AI. Players who don't understand it will feel a faint, correct dread — she seems to know where you're going before you do. Both experiences are correct. The dread is earned because the model is real.

Upgrade IV · Localization Chinese + Farsi Localization Both Planned from Tier 1
Current State

The synthesis identifies Farsi localization as absent and calls for a cultural consultant. Chinese localization is not mentioned anywhere in any document. Chinese is now the most common language on Steam.

Upgraded State

Both Farsi and Simplified Chinese are budgeted from Chapter One. Farsi: cultural authenticity review is ongoing from the beginning of development; the translation is a product of that relationship, not a post-ship addition. Simplified Chinese: professional translation with a Chinese academic or literary advisor who understands the Silk Road / Achaemenid connection and can speak to that audience specifically. The Steam page in Chinese should lead with the historical specificity: the Achaemenid Empire's geographic position as the western terminus of what would become the Silk Road creates a genuine cultural link that Chinese historical game audiences — a sophisticated and underserved audience — will recognize.

Upgrade V · Demo Strategy Vertical Slice = Permanent Demo Product, Not Just a Production Milestone
Current State

The vertical slice is specified as an internal production milestone to prove the mechanic and attract investors/publishers. It is polished enough to show externally but is not specified as a permanent publicly available demo.

Upgraded State

The vertical slice is designed simultaneously as a production milestone AND a permanent demo product. It ships publicly on Steam Next Fest in the development cycle, remains live after Chapter One releases, and is explicitly designed to generate word-of-mouth. Its test is dual: (1) internal — does the Vanguard treat two different Scholars differently? (2) external — do players who finish the demo want to tell someone about it? The demo should produce the specific response: "I translated a tablet and the guard lady was warmer to me than she was to my friend who skipped everything." That sentence, appearing in a review or a Discord message, is worth more than any trailer.

Upgrade VI · Cultural Communication External Materials Must Be as Specific as Internal Documents
Current State

Internal documents are extraordinarily specific: Achaemenid, Zoroastrian, Persepolis, Asha/Druj, Sacred Fire. External materials (investor presentation, product presentation) describe the setting as "Persian" or "ancient Persian archive." This is adequate for pitching but insufficient for cultural protection and audience targeting.

Upgraded State

The Steam page, all press materials, and all public descriptions lead with the specific cultural framing: "KENOMA is set in a sealed Achaemenid archive — a two-world building modeled on Persepolis, encoding Zoroastrian cosmology, maintained for 300 years by a soldier of the Achaemenid Immortals." This specificity does three things: (1) protects against Orientalism critique by demonstrating genuine research; (2) attracts the academic and literary game audience that AC Mirage cultivated; (3) gives Iranian diaspora and Persian cultural communities a clear signal that this game has done the work. Cultural specificity is both ethical protection and marketing differentiation. These should never be treated as separate goals.

§ 05The Evaluative Conclusion
What the Industry Research Adds to the Synthesis The Synthesis Was an Accurate Self-Portrait. Research Shows What the Mirror Didn't Catch.
The Final Synthesis was built correctly from the inside: it identified the right doctrines, the right keystones, the right priority order for a game designed on these specific principles. What self-portrait can't show is what the genre has learned while you weren't looking.

The genre has learned that sound is the primary axis of horror immersion — not visuals, not narrative, sound. GDC 2024 put a number on it: 70%. Hellblade II and Dead Space Remake have demonstrated the implementation. KENOMA's audio gap is not just a production gap — it is a design gap on the genre's most load-bearing axis, and the synthesis was too internally focused to see it with the same urgency the research demands.

The genre has also learned that adaptive AI antagonists live or die by the distinction between prediction and reaction. Hello Neighbor's AI reacted. The Vanguard, as currently specified, could easily become a sophisticated reactor. The synthesis did not address this failure mode because it was building the system from the inside. Research on AI antagonist design exposes it from the outside.

And the market has learned that KENOMA's two most important underserved audiences are currently invisible to the project. Iranian diaspora and Persian cultural communities — the people who would champion a game that gets their civilization right — aren't in any marketing document. Chinese-language Steam players — now the platform's largest language group — aren't in any localization plan. These aren't afterthoughts. They're the communities most likely to generate the word-of-mouth that makes a narrative horror game reach 1,000 reviews.

The synthesis told you what the game is. The research tells you what the game needs to become to be itself — at full strength, in the right rooms, heard by the people it was made for.
What Hasn't Changed and Never Should Six doctrines. Five keystones. The oil flask as the game's central image. "Past tense is not a judgment. It is a precision." The Deep Read as the game's most important 25 seconds. The BMS as the invisible spine. The Iron Rule applied everywhere without exception. The Vanguard as the finest written character in a game at this budget. These survive every external evaluation because they were found through genuine thought about the game's soul — not through market research, not through trend analysis, not through competitive positioning. They are what KENOMA is. The research tells you how to build them better. It cannot tell you what they are. You already know what they are.
The Single Most Important Thing This Evaluation Adds Commission a binaural audio consultant before any recording begins. The Dark Voice, the crossing transition, the Hollow's acoustic identity, the fire vessel's sputtering at critical oil — all of these are currently described in prose that will be adequate with reverb and extraordinary with binaural HRTF. The GDC 2024 finding is not a recommendation; it is a measurement: 70% of horror immersion comes from sound. KENOMA's audio gap is the project's largest single risk. The binaural specification is the audio gap's single most important repair. Make that phone call this week, before the first recording session, before the first environment art pass, before the first line of the Dark Voice is directed. Everything downstream of audio is better if the audio direction is right from the start.
