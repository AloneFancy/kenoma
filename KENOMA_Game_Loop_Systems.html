§ 00The Master Game Loop — One Loop, Three ExpressionsREAD → ACT → OBSERVE → ADAPT

KENOMA runs one primary game loop throughout all three phases. The loop's content changes — what the player reads, what actions are available, what they observe — but the structure is identical. This is the game's deepest design decision: the player never learns a new loop. They run the same loop with increasing stakes and decreasing resources.

Master Loop Architecture READ → ACT → OBSERVE → ADAPT · Phase determines the content of each node
The Central Design Constraint Every action the player takes in any phase writes to the Behavioral Model System (BMS). The BMS is the connective tissue between all three phases. The Camel Phase fills it with warmth data. The Lion Phase reads it for Vanguard combat AI. The Child Phase reads it for Scenario resolution. No action is ever wasted — the player builds their consequence in Act I without knowing it. The BMS is the architectural reason the three phases feel like one continuous story rather than three separate chapters.

How It Works

Every Scholar action fires an ActionEvent with: type, location, quality (deep/shallow/skipped), timestamp, and observer (VA/VB/both/none). The BMS aggregates these events into running scores.

The BMS never exposes data directly to the player. Its output is the Vanguard AIs' behavior changes (RSS), the Utility Score (USS), and the final Scenario selector (SRS). The player reads the system through its effects on NPCs — not through a UI panel.

Every action has a default weight. A deep translation: +3 WarmthRegister[VA], +2 WarmthRegister[VB], +1 RecognitionStage progress. A withheld translation: −2 each. A demonstrated combat method (VA observed): +1 VA recognition accuracy, −2 USS[VA] in Lion Phase.

Dependencies · Risks

Feeds into: RSS (stage progression), USS (lion phase utility), SRS (scenario resolution). Reads from: TDS (translation quality), TVS (crossing patterns), RES (donation events).

Design risk: Event granularity. Too coarse → Vanguard AI feels arbitrary. Too fine → 60fps event spam. Solution: batch events per room-session (Scholar leaves room → BMS processes session). Fire score updates at session-end, not per-action.

Stage Transition Thresholds

Stage 1→2: WarmthRegister[VA or VB] reaches 15. Triggers: Vanguard stops asking methodology questions, begins providing context without prompting.

Stage 2→3: Reaches 30. Triggers: Pre-staged resources on Scholar's route, room access expands, Vanguard begins answering anticipated questions.

Stage 3→4: Reaches 50. Triggers: Deep personal dialogue unlocks (grief tablet gate, VB confessions). Resonance Moments 1–3 become available to fire (they require their own sub-conditions).

Stage 4→5: Reaches 70. Triggers: Vanguard stops offering information. Shorter more precise replies. Pre-emptive positioning in Lion Phase. Model is complete.

Stage regression: Withheld translations, deceptions detected, Dark Voice followed: −8 WarmthRegister per event. Stage regression is possible down to Stage 2 minimum (Stage 1 never re-enters — she already knows the Scholar came).

Behavioral Outputs per Stage

Stage 1: VA asks × 1 per topic, times Scholar translations, offers nothing. VB speaks only when addressed.

Stage 2: VA provides patrol info, VB annotates tablets. Both begin contributing to shared work.

Stage 3: VA pre-stages oil on Scholar route. VB reveals shortcuts. Resonance Moments gated open.

Stage 4: VA completes Scholar's sentences (predictive responses). VB makes philosophical confessions. Maximum warmth window.

Stage 5: Both go quieter. Fewer words. Precise. The model is done. They are verifying, not learning. Lion Phase pre-positioning begins activating.

The Three Depths

SKIP: Scholar walks past. No BMS event. No knowledge gained. No consequence... except the gap it leaves. Skipped tablets can become Act II resources that don't exist.

SKIM (default): 8–12 second interaction. Scholar gets surface meaning — sufficient for most immediate puzzle purposes. BMS writes: +1 WarmthRegister if shared with Vanguard. The creature's name if an origin inscription. Partial knowledge stored.

DEEP: 25–40 second interaction. Scholar enters focused reading mode (camera locks slightly, ambient sound narrows). Full knowledge stored to KnowledgeBank. Unlocks: full Naming tier for creature inscriptions, Armillary sequence memory, Clause 18 recognition, Dark Voice aphorism database. Costs time and robe exposure (standing still = vulnerability). Returns are asymmetric — DEEP reads compound across the full run.

Downstream Consequences

PZ-007 (Armillary): DEEP read of the manual = Scholar has sequence memorized. 28 seconds to activate. SKIM or skip = Scholar improvises under Boundary pressure. 35 seconds. 7-second difference in the game's most dangerous window.

Naming Tier: Only available if the creature's origin inscription was DEEP-read. Skim: 8-second stun available. Skip: no options beyond combat tiers.

Dark Voice content: The Voice only has aphorisms available from texts the Scholar has DEEP-read. A Scholar who skimmed everything hears their Voice speaking generic strategic advice. A Scholar who read deeply hears their exact texts cited back at them. The Voice uses the Scholar's own vocabulary — but only vocabulary the Scholar actually has.

Mechanic Specification

Every crossing increments CrossingCount and adds to TraversalDebt. TFP-04 costs +2 per use; others +1. Debt decays at 0.01/second while Scholar remains in one world. Rapid crossing prevents decay.

BoundaryState transitions: Debt 0–0.3 → DORMANT (astrolabe quiet). Debt 0.3–0.6 → ACTIVE (astrolabe static begins, Boundary appears in distant rooms). Debt 0.6–1.0 → HUNTING (Boundary actively pursues, astrolabe in continuous warning).

The Iron Rule — enforced by TVS: Knowledge from World B unlocks World A chamber/puzzle. Material from World A produces World B physical change. TVS validates every cross-world action against this rule. Invalid applications simply fail with no animation — the Scholar tries, nothing happens. The rule is never explained in text. It is taught through PZ-002 and observed consistently.

Vanguard crossing delta: Neither Vanguard has a CrossingCount. Their TraversalDebt is always 0. TVS tracks this delta explicitly — it is used by USS in Lion Phase. The Scholar is the only thing with Debt.

World-Switch Interaction

The 2.2-second crossing window is uninterruptible. Both Vanguards know this. Stage 5 VA has measured the Scholar's average crossing time (she started timing on Week 1). In Lion Phase, she can close distance by 3.8 meters in 2.2 seconds. The Scholar who crosses without checking the approach angle has given VA a free approach.

Astrolabe as TVS HUD: The physical astrolabe object is the only TraversalDebt display. Three ring positions: settled (Debt <0.3), active (0.3–0.6), urgent (0.6+). When Debt reaches 1.0 in Lion Phase, the astrolabe's resonance signal cuts out permanently — the Scholar has no proximity warning for the rest of the game. This is a resource depletion, not a game event.

Economy Design

The Camel Phase is deliberately resource-generous. Oil is findable in every room. Bolts respawn on creature patrol routes. The Scholar learns to spend freely — because the Camel's nature is generosity. This is intentional priming: when the Lion Phase tightens the economy, the player feels the scarcity as a tonal shift, not a punishment.

Donation writes to BMS. Scholar gives oil to VB (her cold-light tube needs fuel): +3 WarmthRegister[VB]. Scholar gives bolts to VA for patrol supply: +2 WarmthRegister[VA]. The economy is not just survival — it is relationship capital. Every donation is both resource expenditure and BMS investment.

Lion Phase economy: Resource spawn rates drop 40%. Donation opportunities disappear (Vanguards are no longer accepting). Every bolt spent on a missed shot is a bolt not available for the next required shot. The Scholar who was generous in Act I now faces an economy calibrated to exactly how many resources they saved.

Robe Integrity as health proxy: No number displayed. Visual state only: intact → frayed → torn → deeply damaged → critical (hands shake on tablet interactions). At critical, the Scholar's SKIM read time increases +6 seconds (trembling hands). This directly pressures the Scholar's core competency.

Combat Tier Resource Costs

Tier 1 (Environmental): 0 oil, 0 bolts — uses only traps set in Camel Phase. Finite: once used, gone. Maximum efficiency, requires Camel Phase setup investment.

Tier 2A (Fire Vessel): 5–8 oil per sustained exposure. Competes with navigation oil use. A Scholar who fires at Hollow creatures is also dimming their navigation light.

Tier 2B (Crossbow): 1 bolt per shot, 3 seconds to reload (fully exposed). Maximum 12 bolts total. Bolts can become Naming Bells (2 bolts + 1 stone). The Scholar who makes Naming Bells cannot shoot. This trade is the game's central resource decision.

Tier 3 (Naming): 0 cost. Requires only prior DEEP read. The game's most powerful action costs nothing except time and reading depth — which is the Scholar's most abundant resource if used correctly.

Utility Score Mechanics

USS starts at 100.0 for both Vanguards at Act II turn. It depletes through Scholar combat (each bolt fired: −3), trap discovery by Vanguard (−5), Archive completion advances (−2 per % completed beyond 80%), and prolonged Scholar inactivity (−1.5/minute).

USS can recover: Scholar completes a high-value translation: +6. Scholar Names a creature VA couldn't Name: +10 and VA_Threshold rises by 5 (she needs to observe more). Scholar enters an unvisited WB room: +4 VB_Utility. Scholar produces a cross-world insight VB's model didn't predict: +12 VB_Utility (maximum single recovery).

Threshold crossing triggers: VA_Utility < VA_Threshold → VA_State → CLOSE (she begins pre-positioning, closing exits). VA_Utility < (VA_Threshold × 0.6) → VA_State → EXEC (she begins the hunt). Same logic for VB, independently.

The Gap is playable: If VA enters EXEC before VB, the Scholar can still keep VB in WAIT by producing VB_Utility recoveries. A Scholar fighting VA while VB observes is a fundamentally different encounter than both in EXEC simultaneously. The Gap is the Lion Phase's richest strategic space.

Spare State Logic

SpareActive = true when a Vanguard in EXEC receives a USS recovery event that pushes Utility above (Threshold + 15). She transitions back to CLOSE, not WAIT.

Spare is always temporary. The Scholar must produce another recovery event within 8 minutes of game time or SpareActive expires and she returns to EXEC. The Scholar cannot rest. Spare is earned continuously, not banked.

What a Spare looks like: VA in EXEC receives Scholar's completion of an Archive section she couldn't access. She stops approaching. She stands still for 3 seconds (USS processing). Then she moves to observe rather than close. The Scholar has bought time — not safety.

Hysteresis prevents oscillation: VA_Threshold rises by 3 every time SpareActive fires. Earning spares becomes progressively harder. The Scholar cannot repeatedly produce the same recovery event — she has already updated her model to account for it. The utility of any single strategy decays.

Room State Logic

SCHOLAR-controlled: Scholar's traps are active. Resources Scholar staged are present. Scholar knows all exit positions (they entered this room in Camel Phase). Vanguard has not scouted this room in Act II yet — her AI path-plan treats it as uncertain terrain and approaches with reduced speed.

CONTESTED: Both parties have partial knowledge. Scholar traps may have been scouted (if Vanguard entered while Scholar was in the other world) but not necessarily removed. The Scholar doesn't know which traps are still viable. The Vanguard doesn't know if the Scholar has restaged traps since her last visit. Both are operating on incomplete information.

VANGUARD-controlled: Scholar traps removed or disabled. Vanguard has pre-positioned resources she will use. Scholar approaches into a room she built but that now serves someone else's plan. The Scholar's own architectural knowledge is working against them — they know every exit, but so does the Vanguard, and she's already covered the relevant ones.

Trap System Integration

Traps placed in Camel Phase: Only Scholar-placed traps from Act I persist to Act II. The Scholar who set more traps earlier has more options now. Each trap has a TrapObserved flag — VA in Stage 5 watched the Scholar arm specific traps and has marked them in her route plan. Unobserved traps remain viable; observed ones do not.

The Camel Phase payoff: The Scholar who worked outside VA's observed zones in Act I set traps she didn't see. Those traps fire in Act II at full effectiveness. The Scholar who always worked where VA could observe was building a relationship AND inadvertently training her adversary's counter-strategy. This is the structural reason warmth and danger are the same thing.

Score Calculation

FinalScore composition:

WarmthRegister[both] at peak / 2 → weight 35%
ArchiveCompletePct at Act II turn → weight 25%
ResonanceMomentsHit count (0–3) × 8 → weight 24%
DonationLog depth → weight 10%
DarkVoiceAccepted bonus (handled with fullness) → weight 6%

SurvivorWorld: Determined by which Vanguard's execution succeeded. WA survivor = Sacred Fire aftermath. WB survivor = Hollow aftermath. Each Scenario archetype has two scene variants — the content is the same archetype (grief structure, portrait structure, etc.) but expressed through the surviving Vanguard's philosophy. This gives the game 10 meaningfully distinct endings from 5 scenario archetypes × 2 world variants.

JournalDiscovered: If Scholar's private journal was found by the Vanguard (she accesses it in Scenarios 4–5), the ClosingLineVariant selects from the more intimate pool. The journal is in the Scholar's last work room — she will find it in all Tier 4+ scenarios because she always returns to verify the model against its final state.

Scene Construction

The Child Phase is not a cutscene. It is a navigable sequence with the Vanguard as the active agent. The player's camera follows but cannot direct. The Vanguard walks to specific locations, performs specific actions, speaks specific lines — all determined by SRS outputs.

Scene construction order: (1) Three-second silence. (2) SRS selects movement script for Scenario archetype. (3) Vanguard executes movement script. (4) FinalLine selected from ClosingLineVariant pool. (5) NorthSeeded flag determines post-credits scene availability.

The three-second silence has no animation variation — it is always identical. The weight it carries changes entirely based on what was built before it. Same gesture, five different meanings. This is the design delivering on the promise: the same action means different things depending on everything that preceded it.

3.1Child Phase Architecture — Witness Loop
The Inversion Principle The Child Phase inverts the player's role. In Acts I and II, the player directed the Scholar and observed the Vanguards' reactions. In Act III, the Vanguard is the agent — she moves, acts, speaks — and the player observes her reactions to what the Scholar built. The player has no input. The input happened across the preceding 150 minutes. The Child Phase is the read-out of everything the player wrote into the BMS.

§ 04Production Build Order — System TiersDependencies · Weeks · Vertical Slice → Full Game
Production Principle Build what is needed to demonstrate the core concept first. The BMS + TDS + basic RSS + one Traversal crossing + one creature encounter = a playable vertical slice that proves the game's unique mechanic. Everything else is expansion. Build in the order that dependencies require, not in the order that feels most exciting.

Must feel like: The Scholar translates something deeply, shares it with VA, VA responds differently than she would have if they had skimmed. One crossing. One creature encounter. The warmth is real. The BMS is running. That is the demo.

§ 06Production Risk Register12 flagged risks · Severity · Mitigation
Risk IDSystemDescriptionSeverityMitigation
RISK-01 SYS-06 USS Vanguard feels random. If USS threshold crossing is not smooth — if players see VA in WAIT and two seconds later in EXEC with no visible intermediate signal — the AI feels arbitrary rather than intelligent. This breaks the relationship's emotional coherence. HIGH Mandatory playtest gate: dedicated 6-session USS tuning sprint after Tier 1 ship. Players must be able to identify, in retrospect, why VA transitioned. CLOSE state behavioral signals (pre-positioning, exit-closing) must be legible. Add CLOSE→EXEC delay timer (minimum 90 seconds) to prevent snap-transitions.
RISK-02 SYS-01 BMS BMS event spam. If BMS writes events every frame rather than per session, performance degrades and event weighting loses signal in noise. Common failure mode: everything is equally weighted because everything fires equally often. HIGH Batch events per room-session as specified. BMS.ProcessSession() fires on Scholar exit, not on every action. Only 12 core event types in Tier 0 — resist scope creep. Profile BMS performance explicitly in Tier 0 milestone.
RISK-03 SYS-02 RSS Stage 5 feels like a difficulty spike, not a narrative beat. If Stage 5 Vanguard behavior change reads as "she got harder" rather than "she's done learning," the model completion loses its intended weight and becomes a complaint about unfair difficulty. MEDIUM Stage 5 must be reached through player investment, not time. A player who didn't build the relationship won't reach Stage 5 — they'll face Stage 3 competence. Stage 5 must feel earned. Playtester language check: if testers say "she got harder" rather than "she knew me," the behavioral signals need tuning.
RISK-04 SYS-03 TDS Deep reads feel like punishment (slow and boring) rather than investment. If the 25–40 second DEEP read interaction is not engaging in itself — visually, aurally, narratively — players will consistently skim, and the TDS downstream consequences will never manifest. The demo will then seem to be about skimming. HIGH The DEEP read interaction must be the game's best-feeling moment. Scholar's voice murmuring the translation. Cuneiform characters highlighting as they're parsed. Ambient sound narrowing to focus. The 25 seconds must feel like reward, not cost. Art and audio must prioritize this interaction above all others in Tier 0.
RISK-05 Child Phase Child Phase feels like a cutscene, not a resolution. If players feel they are watching a video after a game, the emotional payoff is reduced. The key is the player retaining a sense that what they see is the direct consequence of their choices — not the game playing a conclusion at them. MEDIUM The Vanguard's route through the Child Phase must include at least 2 interactive "witness" moments — moments where the player can direct the camera to look at something the Vanguard is also looking at. Not control, but gaze. The player should feel present, not dismissed. Three-second silence must land before any music event — let the absence breathe.
RISK-06 Resonance Moments Resonance Moments fire on a timer, not on evidence. If the Recognition scene fires regardless of what the Scholar has done in the preceding two weeks, the warmth is guaranteed atmosphere, not earned relationship. Players notice — they feel the scene is pre-scripted. HIGH Gate all three Resonance Moments behind RSS stage thresholds as specified. The Recognition only produces "Yours is better" at Stage 3+. Below Stage 3: "I had a translation." Test explicitly: a playthrough where Scholar skims everything must produce noticeably flatter Vanguard responses. If the flat version is indistinguishable, the system is broken.
RISK-07 SYS-07 TCS Terrain control feels like invisible scripting. If the Scholar discovers their Camel Phase traps have been neutralized without any in-world signal they missed, TCS creates frustration rather than strategic loss. "How did she know?" is a legitimate complaint if the information was never available to observe. MEDIUM TrapObserved flag must have visible evidence. If VA observed a trap being set, there must be a signal the player could have noticed — VA's gaze direction at the time, a slight pause in her patrol, a tablet annotation referencing the trap's location. Players who were paying attention should be able to reconstruct "she was watching."
RISK-08 Dark Voice Dark Voice gives game hints. If the Voice ever says anything that solves a puzzle or suggests a tactical move the player hasn't considered, it becomes a hint system with reverb. The entire character of the Scholar collapses — they are no longer someone who knows and chooses, they are someone being coached. HIGH All Dark Voice lines must be reviewed for hint content before ship. Rule: the Voice speaks what the Scholar already knows, not what the Scholar should do next. If a line can reasonably be read as advice, it is cut. The Voice is self-knowledge surfacing, not external guidance arriving.
RISK-09 Economy Balance Camel Phase is not generous enough — players hoard. If players are resource-anxious in Act I, they will not donate to Vanguards, will not use environmental traps liberally (saving them for later), and will not invest in Naming Bell crafting. The Camel's nature (generous, burden-bearing) must be mechanically incentivized, not just narratively stated. MEDIUM Camel Phase resource respawn rate must be tuned so a generous Scholar never feels resource-anxious. Donations must produce immediate visible RSS feedback. Players must feel that giving away resources produces more than hoarding them. Playtest specifically for donation rate — if it's below 30% of available donation opportunities, the economy is too tight.
RISK-10 SYS-08 SRS Scenarios I and II feel like punishments for playing badly. If low-score endings feel like the game judging the player negatively, the design premise breaks — the game is supposed to produce genuine outcomes, not grade performance. A Scholar who gave little should produce a specific kind of ending, not a bad ending. MEDIUM Scenario I writing must be genuinely distinct, not diminished. The Vanguard who faces an incomplete archive has a real problem to solve — she is not failing, she is recalibrating. Testers who experience Scenario I first should feel "that was a different story" not "that was the worse story." This is a writing calibration requirement, not a design change.
RISK-11 Dual-World Logic The Iron Rule is applied inconsistently across rooms. TS2's critical failure was each zone reinventing the crossing logic. KENOMA has one rule. If any puzzle or room-specific mechanic violates the rule — even once, even with good intentions — the player's intuition breaks and every subsequent crossing becomes uncertain. HIGH The Iron Rule is locked at PZ-002 and never revisited. Every cross-world action in every puzzle must be reviewed against the rule before ship. TVS validation is technical enforcement. If a puzzle design requires bending the rule, the puzzle design changes — the rule does not.
RISK-12 Act I→II Transition The turn feels like a betrayal reveal. If players experience the Act II turn as "she was secretly evil all along," the Camel Phase's emotional investment was false — players were deceived, not invested. The distinction between a betrayal narrative and a completion narrative is entirely in the transition's execution. HIGH The turn must be staged as a completion, not a reveal. VA's Clause 18 statement (at Stage 4+) tells the Scholar and the player exactly what is happening before it happens. The Scholar sets down the tablet. They pick up the crossbow. This is not surprise. This is decision. Playtest language check: testers must say "I knew it was coming and it still landed" not "I didn't see that coming." The latter means the Camel Phase failed to prepare them.
